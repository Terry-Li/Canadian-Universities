     <p><b>Oregon State University</b> (<b>OSU</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Coeducation" title="Coeducation" class="mw-redirect">coeducational</a>, <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public</a> <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">research</a> <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Corvallis,_Oregon" title="Corvallis, Oregon">Corvallis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The university offers undergraduate, graduate and doctoral degrees and a multitude of research opportunities. There are more than 200 <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">academic degree</a> programs offered through the university. OSU's programs in <a href="/wiki/Microbiology" title="Microbiology">microbiology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_engineering" title="Nuclear engineering">nuclear engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Public_health" title="Public health">public health</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biochemistry" title="Biochemistry">biochemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zoology" title="Zoology">zoology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oceanography" title="Oceanography">oceanography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Food_science" title="Food science">food science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pharmacy" title="Pharmacy">pharmacy</a> are recognized nationally as top tier programs.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The OSU's <a href="/wiki/Liberal_arts" title="Liberal arts" class="mw-redirect">liberal arts</a> programs have also grown significantly and the department is considered a &quot;cornerstone&quot; of the institution.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> More than 200,000 people have attended OSU since its founding.<sup id="cite_ref-Ranking_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ranking-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/The_Carnegie_Foundation_for_the_Advancement_of_Teaching" title="The Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching" class="mw-redirect">Carnegie Foundation</a> classifies Oregon State University as part of its top tier of research institutions.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>As of 2008, OSU is one of seventy-three <a href="/wiki/Land-grant_university" title="Land-grant university">land-grant</a> universities.<sup id="cite_ref-LandGrant_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LandGrant-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The school is also recognized as a <a href="/wiki/Sea_grant_colleges" title="Sea grant colleges" class="mw-redirect">sea-grant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Space_grant_colleges" title="Space grant colleges" class="mw-redirect">space-grant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sun_grant_colleges" title="Sun grant colleges">sun-grant</a> institution, making it one of only two US institutions to obtain all four designations and the only public university to do so (<a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell</a> is the only other with similar designations).<sup id="cite_ref-Ranking_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ranking-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> OSU receives more funding for research, annually, than all other public higher education institutions in Oregon combined.<sup id="cite_ref-Ranking_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ranking-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oregon_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article: 
      <a href="/wiki/History_of_Oregon_State_University" title="History of Oregon State University">History of Oregon State University</a>
     </div> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oregon_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Oregon_State_University_clock_tower.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Oregon_State_University_clock_tower.jpg/170px-Oregon_State_University_clock_tower.jpg" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Oregon_State_University_clock_tower.jpg/255px-Oregon_State_University_clock_tower.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Oregon_State_University_clock_tower.jpg/340px-Oregon_State_University_clock_tower.jpg 2x" /></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:Oregon_State_University_clock_tower.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf9/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a>
        </div> OSU's Bell Tower
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>The university's roots date back to 1856, when <b>Corvallis Academy</b>, the area's first community school for primary and preparatory education, was founded. In 1858, the school's name was changed to <b>Corvallis College</b> and formally incorporated by members of the <a href="/wiki/Freemasonry" title="Freemasonry">Freemasons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The school offered its first college-level curriculum in 1865, under the administration of the <a href="/wiki/Methodist_Episcopal_Church,_South" title="Methodist Episcopal Church, South">Methodist Episcopal Church, South</a>.</p> 
     <p>On August 22, 1868, official Articles of Incorporation were filed for Corvallis College. October 27, 1868, is known as OSU Charter Day, the day that the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Legislative_Assembly" title="Oregon Legislative Assembly">Oregon Legislative Assembly</a> designated Corvallis College as the Agricultural College of the state of Oregon and the recipient of <a href="/wiki/Land-grant_university" title="Land-grant university">Land Grant</a> fund income. As part of this designation, the college was required to comply with the requirements set forth in the <a href="/wiki/Morrill_Land-Grant_Colleges_Act" title="Morrill Land-Grant Colleges Act" class="mw-redirect">First Morrill Act</a>. The name was changed to <b>Corvallis State Agricultural College</b> and was then authorized to grant the <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Bachelor of Science</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts_(postgraduate)" title="Master of Arts (postgraduate)" class="mw-redirect">Master of Arts</a> degrees. The first graduating class was in 1870, granting <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> degrees.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oregon_Agricultural_College">Oregon Agricultural College</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oregon_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Oregon Agricultural College">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Style_show_at_Multnomah_Hotel_in_Portland_Oregon_1920.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Style_show_at_Multnomah_Hotel_in_Portland_Oregon_1920.jpg/220px-Style_show_at_Multnomah_Hotel_in_Portland_Oregon_1920.jpg" width="220" height="178" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Style_show_at_Multnomah_Hotel_in_Portland_Oregon_1920.jpg/330px-Style_show_at_Multnomah_Hotel_in_Portland_Oregon_1920.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Style_show_at_Multnomah_Hotel_in_Portland_Oregon_1920.jpg/440px-Style_show_at_Multnomah_Hotel_in_Portland_Oregon_1920.jpg 2x" /></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:Style_show_at_Multnomah_Hotel_in_Portland_Oregon_1920.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf9/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a>
        </div> OAC Home Ec department at 
        <a href="/wiki/Multnomah_Hotel" title="Multnomah Hotel">Multnomah Hotel</a> in Portland, 1920
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>In 1890 the college became known informally as <b>Oregon Agricultural College</b> (<b>OAC</b>), with the name change becoming official several years later. Orange was adopted as the school color, with black as the background. The <a href="/wiki/Olmsted_Brothers" title="Olmsted Brothers">Olmsted Brothers</a> developed the first Campus Master Plan in 1909, emphasizing trees and an architectural harmony showcasing basic classical forms in brick. The current campus design generally follows this original plan and is laid on a grid of wide, tree-lined streets. Well-partitioned buildings accent open lawns along the main passages and are mostly surrounded by clusters of tall trees. The overall scheme gives it a quaint, early American institutional feel common to campuses found in the Midwest or Southern U.S.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p> 
     <p>The Division of College Extension was organized in 1911 with Ralph Dorn Hetzel as director and, in 1912, the first off-campus faculty were placed in Marion and Wallowa counties. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">Army</a> <a href="/wiki/ROTC" title="ROTC" class="mw-redirect">ROTC</a> became active in 1917, replacing the original Cadet Corps formed by students studying <a href="/wiki/Military_Science" title="Military Science" class="mw-redirect">Military Science</a>. In 1919, OAC began a horticultural products processing program, the first of its kind in the United States. Accreditation was granted in 1924 by the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Association_of_Secondary_and_Higher_Schools" title="Northwest Association of Secondary and Higher Schools">Northwest Association of Secondary and Higher Schools</a>.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oregon_State">Oregon State</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oregon_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Oregon State">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="thumb tleft"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Irish_Bend_Covered_Bridge_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Irish_Bend_Covered_Bridge_2.jpg/220px-Irish_Bend_Covered_Bridge_2.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Irish_Bend_Covered_Bridge_2.jpg/330px-Irish_Bend_Covered_Bridge_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Irish_Bend_Covered_Bridge_2.jpg/440px-Irish_Bend_Covered_Bridge_2.jpg 2x" /></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:Irish_Bend_Covered_Bridge_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf9/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a>
        </div> 
        <a href="/wiki/Irish_Bend_Covered_Bridge" title="Irish Bend Covered Bridge">Irish Bend Covered Bridge</a> - The west side of campus is dedicated, primarily, to agricultural research. It is also home to this historic landmark.
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>1927 marked yet another name change, this time to Oregon State Agricultural College. The Oregon Unification Bill passed by the Legislative Assembly in 1929 placed the school under the oversight of the newly formed <a href="/wiki/Oregon_State_Board_of_Higher_Education" title="Oregon State Board of Higher Education">Oregon State Board of Higher Education</a>. Doctoral education was first provided in 1935 with the conferral of four <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Doctor of Philosophy</a> degrees. This year also saw the creation of the first summer session. The growing diversity in degree programs offered led to another name change in 1937, when the college became <b>Oregon State College</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-OSU_1920_chron_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OSU_1920_chron-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The university's current title, <b>Oregon State University,</b> was adopted on March 6, 1961 by a legislative act signed into law by Governor <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hatfield" title="Mark Hatfield">Mark Hatfield</a>.</p> 
     <p>In 2007, Scott Reed was named the Vice Provost for Outreach and Engagement as OSU Extension Service and <a href="/wiki/Oregon_State_University_Extended_Campus" title="Oregon State University Extended Campus">OSU Extended Campus</a> were aligned under this new division.</p>

     <p>For the Fall 2011 academic year, the university received over 12,000 freshman applications. U.S. News &amp; World Report considers OSU to be &quot;selective.&quot;<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oregon_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Research has played a central role in the university's overall operations for much of its history.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> Most of OSU's research continues at the Corvallis campus, but an increasing number of endeavors are underway at various locations throughout the state and abroad. Current research facilities, beyond the campus, include the Seafood Laboratory in <a href="/wiki/Astoria,_Oregon" title="Astoria, Oregon">Astoria</a> and the Food Innovation Laboratory in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> The university's college of oceanic and atmospheric sciences operates several state-of-the-art laboratories, including the <a href="/wiki/Hatfield_Marine_Science_Center" title="Hatfield Marine Science Center">Hatfield Marine Science Center</a> and two oceanographic research vessels out of <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Oregon" title="Newport, Oregon">Newport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> The oceanography department is now leading the largest ocean science project in U.S. history. The project dubbed &quot;Endurance Array,&quot; features a fleet of undersea gliders and six sites with multiple observation platforms. The first three of the platforms will be deployed off Newport in 2013 and a second set of three off Grays Harbor in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Largest_ocean_science_project_in_U.S._history_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Largest_ocean_science_project_in_U.S._history-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> OSU also manages nearly 11,250 acres (4,550&nbsp;ha) of forest land, which includes the McDonald-Dunn Research Forest.<sup id="cite_ref-OSUcollegeforests_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OSUcollegeforests-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The 2005 <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Classification_of_Institutions_of_Higher_Education" title="Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education">Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education</a> recognized Oregon State as a &quot;Comprehensive Doctoral with Medical/Veterinary&quot; university. This is one of only three such universities in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a> to be classified in this category. In 2006, Carnegie also recognized the university as having &quot;Very High Research Activity,&quot; which makes OSU the only university in Oregon to attain these combined classifications.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Sea_Grant_Colleges" title="Sea Grant Colleges" class="mw-redirect">National Sea Grant College Program</a> was founded in the 1960s. OSU is one of the original four Sea Grant Colleges selected in 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-HistSeaGrant_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HistSeaGrant-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 1967 the <a href="/wiki/OSU_Radiation_Center" title="OSU Radiation Center" class="mw-redirect">Radiation Center</a> was constructed at the edge of campus, housing a 1.1 <a href="/wiki/Megawatt" title="Megawatt" class="mw-redirect">MW</a> <a href="/wiki/TRIGA" title="TRIGA">TRIGA</a> Mark II Research <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_reactor" title="Nuclear reactor">Reactor</a>. The reactor is equipped to utilize <a href="/wiki/Highly_enriched_uranium" title="Highly enriched uranium" class="mw-redirect">Highly Enriched Uranium (HEU)</a> for fuel. Rankings published by <i><a href="/wiki/U.S._News_%26_World_Report" title="U.S. News &amp; World Report">U.S. News &amp; World Report</a></i> in 2008 placed Oregon State eighth in the nation in graduate <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_engineering" title="Nuclear engineering">nuclear engineering</a>.</p> 
     <p>OSU was one of the early members of the federal <a href="/wiki/Space_grant_colleges" title="Space grant colleges" class="mw-redirect">Space Grant</a> program.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> Designated in 1991, the additional grant program made Oregon State one of only 13 schools in the United States to serve as a combined <a href="/wiki/Land-grant_university" title="Land-grant university">Land Grant</a>, Sea Grant and Space Grant university. Most recently, OSU was designated as a federal <a href="/wiki/Sun_Grant_Colleges" title="Sun Grant Colleges" class="mw-redirect">Sun Grant</a> institution. The designation, made in 2003, now makes Oregon State one of only two such universities (the other being <a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell University</a>) and the only public institution with all four designations.</p> 
     <p>In 1999, OSU finished a $40 million remodel to the campus library. Known as the <a href="/wiki/Valley_Library" title="Valley Library" class="mw-redirect">Valley Library</a>, the totally remodeled building was selected by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Library_Journal" title="The Library Journal" class="mw-redirect">The Library Journal</a></i> as their 1999 Library of the Year, the first academic library so named.<sup id="cite_ref-Ranking_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ranking-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 2001, the university's Wave Research Laboratory was designated by the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a> as a site for tsunami research under the Network for <a href="/wiki/Earthquake_engineering" title="Earthquake engineering">Earthquake Engineering</a> Simulation. The <a href="/wiki/O._H._Hinsdale_Wave_Research_Laboratory" title="O. H. Hinsdale Wave Research Laboratory">O. H. Hinsdale Wave Research Laboratory</a> is located on the edge of the campus and is one of the largest and most sophisticated laboratories for education, research, and testing in coastal, ocean and related areas in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-WRL-aboutus_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WRL-aboutus-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Environmental_Health_Sciences" title="National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences">National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences</a> funds two research centers at Oregon State University. The Environmental Health Sciences Center<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> has been funded continually since 1969 and the Superfund Research Center<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> is a newer center that started funding in 2009.</p> 
     <p>OSU administers the <a href="/wiki/Andrews_Forest" title="Andrews Forest">H.J. Andrews Experimental Forest</a>, a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Forest_Service" title="United States Forest Service">United States Forest Service</a> facility dedicated to forestry and ecology research. The Andrews Forest is a UNESCO <a href="/wiki/Man_and_the_Biosphere_Programme" title="Man and the Biosphere Programme">International Biosphere Reserve</a>.</p>

     <p>Other recent recognitions include a 2007 ranking by <a href="/wiki/Stack_magazine" title="Stack magazine"><i>STACK</i> magazine</a>, in which OSU was selected as one of the nation's top 50 universities in &quot;academics, athletic opportunity and overall performance.&quot; The school ranked 29th overall, making it one of only two Northwest schools in the top 30 and the only university from the state to receive a ranking.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>As a top tier forestry school, OSU is widely considered the nation's leader in the subject. Of the 53 forestry programs at North American universities; OSU's College of Forestry was ranked first by a 2006 survey &quot;in the total number of professional publications, first in the number of 'citations' to those publications, and is perceived by academic colleagues as the leading forestry program in North America.&quot;<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>Its Department of Geosciences, College of Veterinary Medicine, College of Pharmacy, Public Health, Nuclear Engineering as well as many other programs and departments are among the top ranked in the nation.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> The school itself is also ranked by the EPA as one of the greenest schools for using renewable energy and for its recycling programs.</p>

In 1994, OSU was rated the safest campus in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific-12_Conference#Pacific-10" title="Pacific-12 Conference">Pac-10</a> in a study of universities.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> In September 2008, the Oregon State University campus was designated the <b>Oregon State University Historic District</b> by the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> (NRHP).<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> It is the only college or university campus in Oregon to have a <a href="/wiki/Historic_district" title="Historic district">historic district</a> designation.<sup id="cite_ref-PDXBus_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDXBus-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> The effort to have the <a href="/wiki/John_Charles_Olmsted" title="John Charles Olmsted">John Charles Olmsted</a>-designed campus listed on the NRHP took two years.<sup id="cite_ref-PDXBus_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDXBus-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

This new branch campus is called <a href="/wiki/OSU-Cascades" title="OSU-Cascades" class="mw-redirect">OSU-Cascades</a> and offers students living in the more central region of the state an opportunity to attend select classes at a campus location closer to their homes.</p>

     <p>Oregon State University has numerous national and internationally-famous alumni who have contributed significantly to their professions. Among over 200,000 OSU alumni, scientist and peace activist <a href="/wiki/Linus_Pauling" title="Linus Pauling">Linus Pauling</a> may be the most famous. Pauling is the only recipient of two unshared <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prizes</a>, awarded in the fields of chemistry and peace.<sup id="cite_ref-Ranking_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ranking-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>Notable science and computing alumni include <a href="/wiki/United_States_National_Academy_of_Sciences" title="United States National Academy of Sciences" class="mw-redirect">United States National Academy of Sciences</a> members <a href="/wiki/Paul_H._Emmett" title="Paul H. C. Green">A. C. Green</a>; and 2006 <a href="/wiki/NBA_champion" title="NBA champion" class="mw-redirect">NBA champion</a> and 9-time NBA All-Star <a href="/wiki/Gary_Payton" title="Gary Payton">Gary Payton</a>. OSU alumni have earned more than 15 Olympic medals; medal-winning Olympians include high jumper <a href="/wiki/Dick_Fosbury" title="Dick Fosbury">Dick Fosbury</a>, creator of the <a href="/wiki/Fosbury_Flop" title="Fosbury Flop">Fosbury Flop</a>; first American woman to high jump over 6 feet (1.8&nbsp;m) <a href="/wiki/Joni_Huntley" title="Joni Huntley">Joni Huntley</a>; and undefeated amateur wrestler <a href="/wiki/Robin_Reed" title="Robin Reed">Robin Reed</a>. 1996 graduate <a href="/wiki/Les_Gutches" title="Les Gutches">Les Gutches</a> was also a Freestyle Wrestling World Champion.</p> 
     <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Others">Others</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oregon_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Others">edit</a>]</span></h4> 
     <p>Other notable alumni include <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> astronauts <a href="/wiki/William_Oefelein" title="William Oefelein">William Oefelein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Donald_Pettit" title="Donald Pettit">Donald Pettit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Playboy" title="Playboy">Playboy</a> <a href="/wiki/Playboy_Playmate" title="Playboy Playmate">Playmates</a> of The Year <a href="/wiki/Jodi_Ann_Paterson" title="Jodi Ann Paterson">Jodi Ann Paterson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sara_Jean_Underwood" title="Sara Jean Underwood">Sara Jean Underwood</a>, and Turkish billionaire <a href="/wiki/H%C3%BCsn%C3%BC_%C3%96zye%C4%9Fin" title="H&uuml;sn&uuml; &Ouml;zyeğin">H&uuml;sn&uuml; &Ouml;zyeğin</a>.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Faculty_and_staff">Faculty and staff</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oregon_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Faculty and staff">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">

      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Oregon_State_University_faculty_and_staff" title="List of Oregon State University faculty and staff">List of Oregon State University faculty and staff</a>

     <p>OSU has several notable faculty members, including <a href="/wiki/Entomology" title="Entomology">entomology</a> professor <a href="/wiki/George_Poinar,_Jr." title="George Poinar, Jr.">George Poinar, Jr.</a>, whose work extracting DNA from insects fossilized in amber was the inspiration for the novel and film <i><a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_(novel)" title="Jurassic Park (novel)">Jurassic Park</a></i>.</p> 
     <p>OSU baseball coach <a href="/wiki/Pat_Casey" title="Pat Casey">Pat Casey</a> was named Coach of the Year by several publications in both 2006 and 2007 when he led the baseball team to back-to-back national championships, and former OSU basketball coaches <a href="/wiki/Slats_Gill" title="Slats Gill">Slats Gill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Miller" title="Ralph Miller">Ralph Miller</a> are both members of the <a href="/wiki/Naismith_Memorial_Basketball_Hall_of_Fame" title="Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame">Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame</a>. Current OSU head basketball coach <a href="/wiki/Craig_Robinson_(basketball_coach)" title="Craig Robinson (basketball coach)" class="mw-redirect">Craig Robinson</a> is the brother-in-law of President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_life">Student life</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oregon_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Student life">edit</a>]</span></h2>

     <p><a href="/wiki/Corvallis,_Oregon" title="Corvallis, Oregon">Corvallis</a> is the tenth largest city in the state. Still, it is a relatively small community and many of the local events have a strong connection to the university. Oregon State has over 300 active student organizations and groups, the most of any university in the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_University_System" title="Oregon University System">Oregon University System</a>. The campus is located only a few hours driving distance from any number of outdoor recreation opportunities. Several federal and state natural forests and parks make up popular student destinations. These include the <a href="/wiki/Cascade_Range" title="Cascade Range">Cascade Range</a>, a rugged coastline, several large forests, the high desert and numerous rivers and lakes. <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>, Oregon's largest city, is 85 miles (137&nbsp;km) north of the campus.</p> 
     <p>From 1930<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup> until 1968, Oregon State University was home to the Gamma chapter of <a href="/wiki/Phrateres" title="Phrateres">Phrateres</a>, a philanthropic-social organization for female college students. Gamma was the third chapter of the organization, which eventually had over 20 chapters in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and the United States.</p> 
     <p>The majority of older students at Oregon State University live off-campus, but on-campus housing is available and typically home to incoming freshmen. There are 14 residence halls on campus, which are organized into individual Hall Councils. Residents make up the membership and each council holds their own elections to select management over the hall government. All of the councils are managed by the Residence Hall Association (RHA).<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/The_LaSells_Stewart_Center" title="The LaSells Stewart Center">The LaSells Stewart Center</a> is the conference and performing arts center for the campus. Many famous speakers have graced the stage of the campus' main auditorium, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Austin_Auditorium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Austin Auditorium (page does not exist)">Austin Auditorium</a>, while the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Corvallis-OSU_Symphony&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Corvallis-OSU Symphony (page does not exist)">Corvallis-OSU Symphony</a> plays there frequently. The OSU Office of Conferences and Special Events is located within the auditorium.</p> 
     <p>The University is host to a radio station, <a href="/wiki/KBVR_(FM)" title="KBVR (FM)" class="mw-redirect">KBVR 88.7 FM</a>, a television station, <a href="/wiki/KBVR" title="KBVR">KBVR</a> TV 26, and an award-winning student newspaper, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Barometer" title="The Daily Barometer">The Daily Barometer</a>.</i></p> 
     <p>Two Oregon State students are members of the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Student_Association" title="Oregon Student Association">Oregon Student Association</a> Board of Directors.</p> 
     <p>Notable among a number of songs commonly played and sung at various events such as <a href="/wiki/Graduation" title="Graduation">commencement</a>, <a href="/wiki/Convocation" title="Convocation">convocation</a>, and athletic games are: <i><a href="/wiki/Hail_to_Old_OSU" title="Hail to Old OSU">Hail to Old OSU</a></i> and the Alma Mater.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletics">Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oregon_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Athletics">edit</a>]</span></h2>

     <p>In a 2008 national ranking based on academics, athletic opportunity and overall performance, Oregon State was chosen as one of the &quot;premier&quot; universities in America. This ranking, performed by <a href="/wiki/Stack_magazine" title="Stack magazine"><i>STACK</i> magazine</a>, places Oregon State 29th in the nation's &quot;Elite 50&quot; universities and uncontested within the state that year.<sup id="cite_ref-Elite50_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Elite50-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup> Since then, the University of Oregon has joined Oregon State in the STACK rankings.</p> 
     <p>The history of Oregon State athletics dates back to 1893, when &quot;Jimmie the <a href="/wiki/Coyote" title="Coyote">Coyote</a>&quot; was chosen as the college's mascot.<sup id="cite_ref-alumni-mascothistory_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alumni-mascothistory-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup> This was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/American_Beaver" title="American Beaver" class="mw-redirect">beaver</a> in 1910; it has remained the school's mascot. In 1915, the college became one of the four charter members of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_Conference" title="Pacific Coast Conference">Pacific Coast (Athletic) Conference</a>.</p> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">Football</a> is played in <a href="/wiki/Reser_Stadium" title="Reser Stadium">Reser Stadium</a>. The current costumed mascot <a href="/wiki/Benny_Beaver" title="Benny Beaver">Benny the Beaver</a> made his first appearance in 1952. The next year, 1953, saw the opening of the football facility, Parker Stadium (now named <a href="/wiki/Reser_Stadium" title="Reser Stadium">Reser Stadium</a>). The Raising Reser campaign expanded the stadium from 35,000 seats to 46,200 throughout 2006–07. A <a href="/wiki/Time_lapse" title="Time lapse" class="mw-redirect">time lapse</a> video recording of the expansion is viewable on the internet.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup> 1962 saw OSU's (and the west coast's) first <a href="/wiki/Heisman_Trophy" title="Heisman Trophy">Heisman Trophy</a> winner, quarterback <a href="/wiki/Terry_Baker" title="Terry Baker">Terry Baker</a>. The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oregon" title="University of Oregon">University of Oregon</a> is often seen as the school's key athletic rival, with the annual <a href="/wiki/Civil_War_(college_football_game)" title="Civil War (college football game)">Civil War</a> football game between the two teams being one of the nation's longest-lived rivalries.</p> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Trysting_Tree" title="Trysting Tree">Trysting Tree</a> is the name of Oregon State's golf course, dedicated in 1988, the tournament level course has been recognized by <i><a href="/wiki/Golfweek" title="Golfweek">Golfweek</a></i> magazine as one of the top five collegiate golf courses on the West Coast.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Its name is traced to a tree near Benton Hall where student couples would meet and make dates. <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">Basketball</a> is held in <a href="/wiki/Gill_Coliseum" title="Gill Coliseum">Gill Coliseum</a>, named after former Beavers coach <a href="/wiki/Slats_Gill" title="Slats Gill">Slats Gill</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Civil_War_(college_rivalry)" title="Civil War (college rivalry)">Civil War</a> is one of the most contested rivalries in the nation. <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">Baseball</a> is held in <a href="/wiki/Goss_Stadium_at_Coleman_Field" title="Goss Stadium at Coleman Field">Goss Stadium at Coleman Field</a>. The OSU baseball team, managed by <a href="/wiki/Pat_Casey" title="Pat Casey">Pat Casey</a>, won back-to-back <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I_Baseball_Championship" title="NCAA Division I Baseball Championship">NCAA Division I Baseball Championships</a> in 2006 and 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Softball" title="Softball">Softball</a> is held in the OSU Softball Complex. Opened in April 2001, the $1.5 million dollar OSU Softball Complex seats 750. Oregon State hosted a Regional and Super Regional tournament in the 2006 NCAA tournament, winning both and moving on to the Women's College World Series.</p> 
     <p>Oregon State has a total of three <a href="/wiki/NCAA" title="NCAA" class="mw-redirect">NCAA</a> championships. In addition to the two baseball titles, the Beavers won the 1961 <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Men%27s_Cross_Country_Championship" title="NCAA Men's Cross Country Championship" class="mw-redirect">NCAA Men's Cross Country Championship</a>. In 1975, the men's <a href="/wiki/Rowing_(sport)" title="Rowing (sport)">rowing</a> Varsity-4 with coxswain team won the <a href="/wiki/Intercollegiate_Rowing_Association" title="Intercollegiate Rowing Association">Intercollegiate Rowing Association</a> National Collegiate Rowing Championships in <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_New_York" title="Syracuse, New York">Syracuse, New York</a>, establishing a course record which stood for 15 years.<sup id="cite_ref-osurow_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-osurow-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2006 and 2008, the Oregon State racquetball team won the USA <a href="/wiki/Racquetball" title="Racquetball">racquetball</a> intercollegiate championship.<sup id="cite_ref-osur_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-osur-59"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diversity">Diversity</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oregon_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Diversity">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>As of 2009, the total student enrollment (undergraduate and graduate) at OSU was 21,969. As of November 11, 2010, total enrollment had increased to 23,761 students. The jump gives OSU the largest enrollment of the state's two major universities.<sup id="cite_ref-StudentsUpdate_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StudentsUpdate-60"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In accordance with the University’s mission for diversity, many organizations, clubs, and departments have been formed, including the Office Of Community and Diversity<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> and several cultural and resource centers.</p> 
     <p>Oregon State University has several cultural centers aimed at promoting diversity and supporting students of color, including the Lonnie B. Harris Black Cultural Center, Native American Longhouse, Asian &amp; Pacific Cultural Center, and the Centro Cultural C&eacute;sar Ch&aacute;vez.</p> 
     <p>In addition to its mission of ethnic diversity, Oregon State University supports its lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender population with a Pride Center.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oregon_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>On Oct. 26, 2007, Oregon State University publicly launched &quot;The Campaign for OSU,&quot; a $625-million fundraising effort and the university's first comprehensive capital campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup> Originally intended as a seven-year effort, in October 2010 the campaign goal was increased to $850 million after gifts from more than 53,000 donors surpassed the initial goal nearly a year ahead of schedule. In March 2012 leaders announced a new goal of $1 billion to further provide opportunities for students, strengthen Oregon and conduct research that changes the world. Campaign priorities include initiatives supporting a diverse, high-achieving student body and those focused on three core areas of distinction in OSU’s strategic plan:<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup> sustainable earth ecosystems, health, and economic growth and social progress. The campaign will run through 2014 and will help propel the university to a new level of distinction and global leadership.</p> 
     <p>OSU launched the campaign in collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_State_University_Foundation" title="Oregon State University Foundation">Oregon State University Foundation</a>, the nonprofit organization chartered to raise and administer private funds in support of the university's education, research and outreach. A Campaign Steering Committee of alumni and friends is led by co-chairs Patricia V. Reser ’60, James H. Rudd and Patrick F. Stone ’74.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup> The OSU Foundation is governed by a volunteer Board of Trustees.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65"><span>[</span>65<span>]</span></a></sup> It holds assets of $563 million and manages the majority portion of the university’s endowment, valued at $412 million (June 31, 2011).<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66"><span>[</span>66<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
