     <p><b>The Ohio State University</b>, commonly referred to as <b>Ohio State</b> or <b>OSU</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public</a> research university in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus, Ohio</a>. Founded in 1870, as a <a href="/wiki/Land-grant_university" title="Land-grant university">land-grant university</a> and ninth university in <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Morrill_Act_of_1862" title="Morrill Act of 1862" class="mw-redirect">Morrill Act of 1862</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> the university was originally known as the <b>Ohio Agricultural and Mechanical College</b>. The college began with a focus on training students in various agricultural and mechanical disciplines but was developed into a comprehensive university under the direction of Governor <a href="/wiki/Rutherford_B._Hayes" title="Rutherford B. Hayes">Rutherford B. Hayes</a> and by 1878, the college changed its name to its current name. It has since grown into the <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_United_States_university_campuses_by_enrollment" title="List of largest United States university campuses by enrollment" class="mw-redirect">third largest university campus</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2007, Ohio State was officially designated as the <a href="/wiki/Flagship_universities" title="Flagship universities" class="mw-redirect">flagship</a> institution of Ohio's public universities as part of the newly centralized <a href="/wiki/University_System_of_Ohio" title="University System of Ohio">University System of Ohio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> It is considered to be one of America's <a href="/wiki/Public_Ivy" title="Public Ivy">Public Ivy</a> universities, which recognizes top public research universities in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-publicivy_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-publicivy-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Along with its main campus in Columbus, Ohio State also operates a regional campus system with regional campuses in <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_University,_Lima_Campus" title="Ohio State University, Lima Campus">Lima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_University,_Mansfield_Campus" title="Ohio State University, Mansfield Campus">Mansfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_University,_Marion_Campus" title="Ohio State University, Marion Campus">Marion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_University,_Newark_Campus" title="Ohio State University, Newark Campus">Newark</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_University_Agricultural_Technical_Institute" title="Ohio State University Agricultural Technical Institute">Wooster</a>.</p> 
     <p>The university is also home to an extensive student life program, with over 1,000 student organizations; intercollegiate, club and recreational sports programs; student media organizations and publications, <a href="/wiki/Fraternities" title="Fraternities" class="mw-redirect">fraternities</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sororities" title="Sororities" class="mw-redirect">sororities</a>; and an active student government association. Ohio State athletic teams compete in <a href="/wiki/Division_I_(NCAA)" title="Division I (NCAA)">Division I</a> (Football Bowl Subdivision for football) of the <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">NCAA</a> and are known as the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_Buckeyes" title="Ohio State Buckeyes">Ohio State Buckeyes</a>. The university is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Big_Ten_Conference" title="Big Ten Conference">Big Ten Conference</a> for the majority of sports. The <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_Buckeyes_men%27s_ice_hockey" title="Ohio State Buckeyes men's ice hockey">Ohio State Buckeyes men's ice hockey</a> program competes in the <a href="/wiki/Central_Collegiate_Hockey_Association" title="Central Collegiate Hockey Association">Central Collegiate Hockey Association</a>, and its <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_Buckeyes_women%27s_ice_hockey" title="Ohio State Buckeyes women's ice hockey">women's hockey</a> program competes in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Collegiate_Hockey_Association" title="Western Collegiate Hockey Association">Western Collegiate Hockey Association</a>. In addition, the OSU men's volleyball is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_Intercollegiate_Volleyball_Association" title="Midwestern Intercollegiate Volleyball Association">Midwestern Intercollegiate Volleyball Association</a> (MIVA) while the men's lacrosse team is a member of the <a href="/wiki/ECAC_Lacrosse_League" title="ECAC Lacrosse League">ECAC Lacrosse League</a>. OSU is one of only thirteen universities in the nation that plays Division I FBS football and Division I ice hockey. Alumni and former students have gone on to prominent careers in government, business, science, medicine, education, sports, and entertainment. Effective Monday, July 1, 2013, Executive Vice President and <a href="/wiki/Provost_(education)" title="Provost (education)">Provost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_A._Alutto" title="Joseph A. Alutto">Joseph A. Alutto</a>, assumed the role of <a href="/wiki/Acting_President" title="Acting President" class="mw-redirect">Acting President</a> following the retirement of <a href="/wiki/E._Gordon_Gee" title="E. Gordon Gee">E. Gordon Gee</a>. Mr. Alutto will continue to serve until the <a href="/wiki/Board_of_Trustees" title="Board of Trustees" class="mw-redirect">Board of Trustees</a> appoints the new president.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article: 
      <a href="/wiki/History_of_The_Ohio_State_University" title="History of The Ohio State University" class="mw-redirect">History of The Ohio State University</a>
     </div> 
     <p>The Ohio State University was founded in 1870 as a <a href="/wiki/Land-grant_university" title="Land-grant university">land-grant university</a> in with the <a href="/wiki/Morrill_Act_of_1862" title="Morrill Act of 1862" class="mw-redirect">Morrill Act of 1862</a> under the name of the Ohio Agricultural and Mechanical College. The school was originally situated within a farming community located on the northern edge of Columbus. While some interests in the state had hoped that the new university would focus on matriculating students of various agricultural and mechanical disciplines, Governor <a href="/wiki/Rutherford_B._Hayes" title="Rutherford B. Hayes">Rutherford B. Hayes</a> foresaw a more classic, comprehensive university, and manipulated both the university's location and its initial board of trustees towards that end. The university opened its doors to 24 students on September 17, 1873. In 1878, the first class of six men graduated. The first woman graduated the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> Also in 1878, in light of its expanded focus, the college permanently changed its name to the now-familiar &quot;The Ohio State University&quot;, with &quot;The&quot; as part of its official name.<sup id="cite_ref-arl.org_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arl.org-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Ohio State began accepting graduate students in the 1880s, and in 1891, the school saw the founding of its law school, Moritz College of Law. It would later acquire colleges of medicine, dentistry, optometry, veterinary medicine, commerce, and journalism in subsequent years.</p> 
     <p>Although development had been hindered in the 1870s by hostility from the state's agricultural interests and competition for resources from <a href="/wiki/Miami_University" title="Miami University">Miami University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ohio_University" title="Ohio University">Ohio University</a>, both issues were eventually resolved. In 1906, Ohio State's status as the state's flagship campus was written into law by the Ohio legislature through the Eagleson Bill. In 1916, Ohio State was elected into membership in the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_American_Universities" title="Association of American Universities">Association of American Universities</a>.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campus">Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Campus">edit</a>]</span></h2>

     <p>Ohio State's 1,764 acres (7.14&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>) of main campus is approximately 2.5 miles (4.0&nbsp;km) north of the city's downtown. Four buildings are currently listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>: Enarson Hall, <a href="/wiki/University,_Hayes_and_Orton_Halls" title="University, Hayes and Orton Halls">Hayes Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Stadium" title="Ohio Stadium">Ohio Stadium</a>, and <a href="/wiki/University,_Hayes_and_Orton_Halls" title="University, Hayes and Orton Halls">Orton Hall</a>. Unlike earlier Ohio state universities such as Ohio University and Miami University, which have campuses with a consistent architectural style, architecture on the Ohio State campus does not conform to a unifying theme such as <a href="/wiki/Gothic_revival" title="Gothic revival" class="mw-redirect">Gothic revival</a> or <a href="/wiki/Georgian_architecture" title="Georgian architecture">Georgian</a>. Instead, the buildings at Ohio State are a mix of traditional, modern and post-modern styles. The <a href="/wiki/William_Oxley_Thompson_Memorial_Library" title="William Oxley Thompson Memorial Library">William Oxley Thompson Memorial Library</a>, anchoring the western end of The Oval, is Ohio State library's main branch and single largest repository. The Thompson Library was designed in 1913 by the Boston firm of Allen and Collens in the Italianate Renaissance Revival style, and its placement on the Oval was suggested by the Olmsted brothers who had designed New York City's <a href="/wiki/Central_Park" title="Central Park">Central Park</a>. In 2006, the Thompson Library began a $100 million dollar renovation with the stated aims of becoming a &quot;global benchmark twenty-first century&quot; library while maintaining the building's classical Italian Renaissance architecture.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Overall, Ohio State operates the 18th largest university <a href="/wiki/Research_library" title="Research library" class="mw-redirect">research library</a> in North America with a combined collection of over 5.8 million volumes. Additionally, the libraries receive approximately 35,000 serial titles on a regular basis. Its recent acquisitions were 16th among university research libraries in North America.<sup id="cite_ref-arl.org_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arl.org-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Ohio State's library system encompasses twenty-one libraries located on its Columbus campus. An additional eight branches are located at off-campus research facilities, regional campuses, and a book storage depository near campus. In all, the Ohio State library system encompasses fifty-five branches and specialty collections. Some of the more significant collections include The <a href="/wiki/Byrd_Polar_Research_Center" title="Byrd Polar Research Center">Byrd Polar Research Center</a> Archival Program containing the Archives of Admiral <a href="/wiki/Richard_E._Byrd" title="Richard E. Byrd">Richard E. Byrd</a> as well as a significant collection of polar research materials, The <a href="/wiki/Hilandar_Research_Library" title="Hilandar Research Library">Hilandar Research Library</a> which contains the world's largest collection of <a href="/wiki/Medieval" title="Medieval" class="mw-redirect">medieval</a> <a href="/wiki/Slavic_languages" title="Slavic languages">Slavic</a> manuscripts on microform, <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Research_Library" title="Cartoon Research Library" class="mw-redirect">The Ohio State Cartoon Library &amp; Museum</a>, which is the world's largest repository of original cartoons, The Lawrence and Lee Theatre Research Institute and the archives of Senator <a href="/wiki/John_Glenn" title="John Glenn">John Glenn</a>.</p>

     <p>Anchoring the traditional campus gateway at the eastern end of The Oval is the <a href="/wiki/Wexner_Center_for_the_Arts" title="Wexner Center for the Arts">Wexner Center for the Arts</a>. Designed by architects <a href="/wiki/Peter_Eisenman" title="Peter Eisenman">Peter Eisenman</a> of New York and Richard Trott of Columbus, the center opened in 1989. Its founding was financed in large part by Ohio State alumnus <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Wexner" title="Leslie Wexner" class="mw-redirect">Leslie Wexner</a> with a gift of twenty-five million dollars in the 1980s. The center was founded to be a comprehensive visual arts center encompassing all aspects of visual and performing arts with a focus on new commissions and artist residencies. Part of its design was to pay tribute to the armory that formerly had the same location. Its groundbreaking <a href="/wiki/Deconstructivism" title="Deconstructivism">deconstructivist</a> architecture has resulted in it being lauded as one of the most important buildings of its generation. Its design has also been criticized as proving less than ideal for many of the art installations that it has attempted to display. The centerpiece of The Wexner Center's permanent collection is <a href="/wiki/Picasso" title="Picasso" class="mw-redirect">Picasso's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Nude_on_a_black_armchair" title="Nude on a black armchair" class="mw-redirect">Nude on a Black Armchair</a></i>, which was purchased by alumnus <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Wexner" title="Leslie Wexner" class="mw-redirect">Leslie Wexner</a> at auction for forty-five million dollars and then donated to the university.</p> 
     <p>To the south of The Oval is another, somewhat smaller, expanse of greenspace commonly referred to as The South Oval. At its eastern end, it is anchored by the Ohio Union. To the west are Enarson Hall, the Kuhn Honors House, Browning Amphitheatre (a traditional stone <a href="/wiki/Greek_theatre" title="Greek theatre" class="mw-redirect">Greek theatre</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Mirror_Lake_(Ohio)" title="Mirror Lake (Ohio)">Mirror Lake</a>. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Knowlton_School_of_Architecture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Knowlton School of Architecture (page does not exist)">Knowlton Hall</a>, dedicated in October 2004, is located at the corner of West Woodruff Avenue and Tuttle Park Place, adjacent to <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Stadium" title="Ohio Stadium">Ohio Stadium</a>. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Knowlton_School_of_Architecture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Knowlton School of Architecture (page does not exist)">Knowlton Hall</a> along with the <a href="/wiki/Max_M._Fisher_College_of_Business" title="Max M. Fisher College of Business">Fisher College of Business</a> and Hitchcock Hall form an academic nucleus in the Northwestern corner of North campus. Knowlton Hall is home to the KSA Caf&eacute;, the disciplines of Architecture, Landscape Architecture, City and Regional Planning, and about 550 undergraduate and graduate students. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Knowlton_School_of_Architecture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Knowlton School of Architecture (page does not exist)">Knowlton Hall</a> stands out from the general reddish-brown brick of Ohio State's campus with its distinctive white marble tiles that cover the entire exterior of the building. This unique wall cladding was requested by Austin E. Knowlton, the namesake of and main patron to the creation of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Knowlton_School_of_Architecture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Knowlton School of Architecture (page does not exist)">Knowlton Hall</a>. Knowlton also requested that 5 white marble columns be erected on the site, each column representing one of the classical orders of <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">Architecture</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-herrick_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-herrick-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/OSU_College_of_Medicine_and_Public_Health" title="OSU College of Medicine and Public Health" class="mw-redirect">Ohio State College of Medicine</a> is located on the southern edge of the central campus. It is home to the <a href="/wiki/James_Cancer_Hospital" title="James Cancer Hospital">James Cancer Hospital</a>, a cancer research institute and one of the <a href="/wiki/National_Cancer_Institute" title="National Cancer Institute">National Cancer Institute's</a> forty-one <a href="/wiki/NCI-designated_Cancer_Center" title="NCI-designated Cancer Center">comprehensive cancer centers</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/The_Richard_M._Ross_Heart_Hospital" title="The Richard M. Ross Heart Hospital" class="mw-redirect">the Richard M. Ross Heart Hospital</a>, a research institute for cardiovascular disease.</p>

     <p>In 1916, Ohio State became the first university in Ohio to be extended membership into the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_American_Universities" title="Association of American Universities">Association of American Universities</a>, and remains the only public university in Ohio among the organization's 60 members. <i><a href="/wiki/Public_Ivy" title="Public Ivy">The Public Ivies</a>: America's Flagship Public Universities</i> (2000) by Howard and Matthew Greene listed Ohio State as one of a select number of public universities offering the highest educational quality.</p> 
     <p><i>U.S. News &amp; World Report?s</i> widely read rankings of undergraduate colleges in America currently places Ohio State as the 18th best public university and 55th overall ranked university in America. Ohio State ranked 14th in US News' new &quot;Up and Coming&quot; colleges section. The list includes the top colleges in the nation &quot;that are making improvements in academics, faculty, students, campus life, diversity, and facilities. These schools are worth watching because they are making promising and innovative changes.&quot;<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Internationally, in 2011 <a href="/wiki/QS_World_University_Rankings" title="QS World University Rankings">QS World University Rankings</a><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> ranked Ohio State University 111th in the world, and awarded the university with the maximum five stars for excellence.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> It scored 88th in Social Sciences in the international subject rankings. Four years back, China's <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University" title="Shanghai Jiao Tong University">Shanghai Jiao Tong University</a> placed Ohio State as the 61st ranked university in the world in their 2007 <a href="/wiki/Academic_Ranking_of_World_Universities" title="Academic Ranking of World Universities">Academic Ranking of World Universities</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The Lombardi Program on Measuring University Performance at <a href="/wiki/Arizona_State_University" title="Arizona State University">Arizona State University</a> detailed analysis and rankings of American universities currently places Ohio State as the 24th ranked university in America, the 10th ranked public university in the country and the top overall university in Ohio. Of their nine ranking criteria, Ohio State ranked in the top-25 in four categories and between 26?50 in an additional four categories.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Washington_Monthly" title="Washington Monthly">Washington Monthly</a> college rankings, which seek to evaluate colleges' contributions to American society based on factors of social mobility, cutting edge research, and service to the country by their graduates, currently place Ohio State as 12th in the nation and 10th among public universities.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Ohio State is also the only public university in Ohio to which the <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Foundation_for_the_Advancement_of_Teaching" title="Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching">Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching</a> has given both its highest overall classification of <i>Doctoral/Very High Research Activity</i> and highest undergraduate admissions classification of <i>more selective</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p><i>US News</i> ranks the undergraduate program at Ohio State's <a href="/wiki/Max_M._Fisher_College_of_Business" title="Max M. Fisher College of Business">Fisher College of Business</a> 12th in America and the top undergraduate business school in Ohio. The graduate program of the Fisher College is ranked 21st in America and is the top graduate school of business in Ohio. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i> ranked The Fisher College as the 29th ranked MBA program in the world in their 2005 <i>&quot;Which MBA?&quot;</i> issue.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> Fisher's Executive MBA program was ranked 3rd nationally for return on investment by <a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a> in 2008 citing a 170 percent return on an average of $66,900 invested in tuition and expenses during the 18-month program.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2006, The Public Accounting Report ranked Ohio State's accounting department 9th in the nation for undergraduate programs and 10th in the nation for graduate programs. In each case, the ranking was the highest among Ohio universities.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>In a 2007 report released by the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a>, Ohio State?s research expenditures for 2006 were <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>652 million, placing it 7th among public universities and 11th overall, also ranking 3rd among all American universities for private industry sponsored research.      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Big_Ten_Committee_on_Institutional_Cooperation">Big Ten Committee on Institutional Cooperation</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Big Ten Committee on Institutional Cooperation">edit</a>]</span></h4> 
     <p>The Ohio State University is a participant in the <a href="/wiki/Committee_on_Institutional_Cooperation" title="Committee on Institutional Cooperation">Committee on Institutional Cooperation</a>. The Committee on Institutional Cooperation (CIC) is the academic consortium of the universities in the <a href="/wiki/Big_Ten_Conference" title="Big Ten Conference">Big Ten Conference</a> plus former conference member, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago" title="University of Chicago">University of Chicago</a>. The initiative also allows students at participating institutions to take distance courses at other participating institutions. The initiative also forms a partnership of research. Engaging in $8 billion in research in 2010, CIC universities are providing powerful insight into important issues in medicine, technology, agriculture, and communities. <sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> Students at participating schools are also allowed &quot;in-house&quot; viewing privileges at other participating schools' libraries.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> They also employ collective purchasing, which has saved member institutions $19 million to date.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>Undergraduate admissions to Ohio State are classified as ?more selective? by <i><a href="/wiki/US_News_%26_World_Report" title="US News &amp; World Report" class="mw-redirect">US News &amp; World Report</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Princeton_Review" title="The Princeton Review">The Princeton Review</a></i> and according to the data are the most selective for any public university in Ohio. OSU does not consider GPA when for incoming freshman admission, but rather ACT or SAT test scores. Ohio State?s freshman class has included at least 100 National Merit Scholars for nine of the last ten years.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template"><span title="&nbsp;since November 2012" style="white-space: nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot">dead link</a></i>]</span></sup></p> 
     <p>Tuition for full-time, Ohio residents attending Ohio State for the 2010?2011 academic year is $9,420.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> For the 2006?2007 academic year, tuition at Ohio State for Ohio residents placed it as the fifth most expensive public university and slightly beneath the weighted average tuition of $8,553 among Ohio's thirteen public four-year universities.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> In addition to being named a <i>Best in the Midwest</i> selection by <i>The Princeton Review</i>, Ohio State was also the only public university in Ohio to make their list of <i>America's 150 Best Value Colleges</i>.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Endowment_and_fundraising">Endowment and fundraising</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Endowment and fundraising">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Ohio State was among the first group<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> of four public universities to raise a $1 billion <a href="/wiki/Financial_endowment" title="Financial endowment">endowment</a> when it passed the $1 billion mark in 1999. At year?s end 2005, Ohio State?s endowment stood at $1.73 billion, ranking it seventh among public universities and 27th among all American universities.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> In June 2006, the endowment passed the $2 billion mark.</p> 
     <p>In recent decades, and in response to continually shrinking state funding, Ohio State has conducted two significant multi-year <a href="/wiki/Fundraising" title="Fundraising">fundraising</a> campaigns. The first concluded in 1987 and raised $460 million?a record at the time for a public university. The ?Affirm Thy Friendship Campaign? took place between 1995 and 2000. With an initial goal of raising $850 million, the campaign?s final tally was $1.23 billion, placing Ohio State among the small group of public universities to have successfully conducted a $1 billion campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> At his welcoming ceremony, returning President E. Gordon Gee announced that, in the Fall of 2007, Ohio State would be launching a $2.5 billion fund-raising campaign.</p>

     <p>The Office of Student Life is responsible for many of the outside-the-classroom aspects of student life at Ohio State. Among these are student housing; food service; health, wellness and counseling; activities, organizations and leadership development; recreation and intramurals. The Office of Student Life also operates the <a href="/wiki/Jerome_Schottenstein_Center" title="Jerome Schottenstein Center" class="mw-redirect">Schottenstein Center</a>, the Fawcett Center, the <a href="/wiki/Blackwell_Inn" title="Blackwell Inn">Blackwell Inn</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Union" title="Ohio Union">Ohio Union</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Drake_Union" title="Drake Union">Drake Events Center</a>, and the Wilce Student Health Center, named for football coach and university physician <a href="/wiki/John_Wilce" title="John Wilce">John Wilce</a>. The Office of Student Life also oversees the Student Wellness Center, which offers services such as nutrition counseling, financial coaching, HIV and STI testing, sexual assault services, and alcohol and other drug education.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Ohio State has several student managed publications and media outlets. <i>The Makio</i> is the official yearbook.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>The Makio?s</i> sales plummeted by 60% during the early 1970s; the organization went bankrupt and stopped publication during the late 1970s. The book was revived from 1985 to 1994 and has since been revived again in 2000 thanks to several student organizations. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lantern" title="The Lantern">The Lantern</a></i> is the school's daily newspaper and has operated as a laboratory newspaper in the School of Communication (formerly the School of Journalism) since 1881. <i>Mosaic</i> is a literary magazine published by Ohio State, which features undergraduate <a href="/wiki/Fiction" title="Fiction">fiction</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poetry" title="Poetry">poetry</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a>. <i>The Sundial</i> is a student written and published humor magazine. Founded in 1911 it is one of the oldest humor magazines in the country, After a 17 year hiatus in which no magazine was published it has recently been revived.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> Ohio State has two improvisational comedy groups, <i>The 8th Floor Improv</i> and <i>Fishbowl Improv</i>, who regularly perform long and short-form improv around campus and across the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup> There are two student-run radio stations on campus. <a href="/w/index.php?title=OHIO.FM&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="OHIO.FM (page does not exist)">OHIO.FM</a> is the music station and Scarlet and Gray Sports Radio broadcasts eleven different Ohio State sports.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup> Both stations broadcast on an Internet audio stream (no broadcast signals are available in Columbus). Students also operate a local <a href="/wiki/Cable_TV" title="Cable TV" class="mw-redirect">cable TV</a> channel known as <a href="/wiki/Buckeye_TV" title="Buckeye TV">Buckeye TV</a>, which airs primarily on the campus closed cable system operated by the Office of the Chief Information Officer (OCIO).</p> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_University_Marching_Band" title="Ohio State University Marching Band" class="mw-redirect">marching band</a> is also a longstanding tradition at Ohio State. It is the largest all-brass and percussion band in the world. The traditional school songs from &quot;<a href="/wiki/Carmen_Ohio" title="Carmen Ohio">Carmen Ohio</a>&quot; to &quot;<a href="/wiki/Hang_on_Sloopy" title="Hang on Sloopy" class="mw-redirect">Hang on Sloopy</a>&quot; to &quot;Fight the Team Across the Field&quot;, are arranged to fit this instrumentation. The band is famous for &quot;<a href="/wiki/Script_Ohio" title="Script Ohio" class="mw-redirect">Script Ohio</a>&quot;, during which the band marches single-file through the curves of the word &quot;Ohio&quot;, much like a pen writes the word, all the while playing the French march &quot;<a href="/wiki/Le_R%C3%A9giment_de_Sambre_et_Meuse" title="Le R&eacute;giment de Sambre et Meuse">Le Regiment de Sambre et Meuse</a>.&quot; At the end of the performance, the &quot;i&quot; in &quot;Ohio&quot; is &quot;dotted&quot; by a high-stepping senior <a href="/wiki/Sousaphone" title="Sousaphone">sousaphone</a> player.</p> 
     <p>Notable among a number of songs commonly played and sung at various events such as <a href="/wiki/Graduation" title="Graduation">commencement</a> and <a href="/wiki/Convocation" title="Convocation">convocation</a>, and athletic games are: <i>Across the Field</i>, The Ohio State University <a href="/wiki/Fight_song" title="Fight song">fight song</a> and &quot;Buckeye Battle Cry&quot;.</p> 
     <p>The tradition of high quality bands is not limited to the football field. OSU's School of Music contains several high quality concert bands consisting of graduate and undergraduate music majors and non-music majors. The OSU Wind Symphony, frequently receives praise on the national level, recently having been selected to perform at the 2003 College Band Directors National Association (CBDNA) Convention and at the Ohio Music Educators Association Conference in 2001, 2004, 2006, and 2008; the OSU Symphonic Band performed in 2007. The OSU Wind Symphony has recently released its newest album, &quot;Southern Harmony,&quot; the <a href="/wiki/Naxos_Records" title="Naxos Records">Naxos</a> Label in 2009. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_Jazz_Ensemble&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ohio State Jazz Ensemble (page does not exist)">Ohio State Jazz Ensemble</a> performed at the <a href="/wiki/Montreux_Jazz_Festival" title="Montreux Jazz Festival">Montreux Jazz Festival</a> in 1975, 1978, 1986, 1996, and 2001. It has also appeared at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mexico_City_International_Jazz_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mexico City International Jazz Festival (page does not exist)">Mexico City International Jazz Festival</a> in 1990 and the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea_Jazz_Festival" title="North Sea Jazz Festival">North Sea Jazz Festival</a> in 1986, 1996, and 2001. In addition there is also an OSU Symphony Orchestra.</p> 
     <p>In addition to strong bands, the university is also recognized for outstanding choral performance. The Ohio State Men's <a href="/wiki/Glee_Club" title="Glee Club" class="mw-redirect">Glee Club</a>, formed in 1875.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1990, led by Professor James Gallagher, the Men's Glee Club participated in the International Musical <a href="/wiki/Eisteddfod" title="Eisteddfod">Eisteddfod</a> in Llangolen, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> and won the male chorus competition by an unprecedented 20 points before, in a unanimous decision of the judges, being named &quot;Choir of the World&quot;?the first American <a href="/wiki/Choir" title="Choir">choir</a> to win such an honor. The Glee Club is under the direction of Dr. Robert J. Ward. The Ohio State Women's <a href="/wiki/Glee_Club" title="Glee Club" class="mw-redirect">Glee Club</a> was established in 1903.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup> In the group?s recent past, under the leadership of Dr. Hilary Apfelstadt, the Glee Club has been selected to sing for state and regional conferences of the Ohio Music Education Association and the American Choral Directors Association. Beginning its season under the direction of Dr. Richard Schnipke, the OSU Women?s Glee Club was honored to have the opportunity to sing for the Ohio Choral Directors Association annual conference in June 2010.</p> 
     <p>Ohio State's &quot;<a href="/wiki/Buckeye_Bullet" title="Buckeye Bullet">Buckeye Bullet</a>&quot; electric car broke the world record for the fastest speed by an electric vehicle on October 3, 2004 with a speed of 271.737&nbsp;mph (437.3&nbsp;km/h) at the <a href="/wiki/Bonneville_Salt_Flats" title="Bonneville Salt Flats">Bonneville Salt Flats</a> in <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup> The vehicle also holds the US record for fastest electric vehicle with a speed of 314.958&nbsp;mph (506.9&nbsp;km/h), and peak timed mile speed of 321.834&nbsp;mph (517.9&nbsp;km/h). The vehicle was designed, built and managed by a team of engineering students at the university's &quot;Center for Automotive Research-Intelligent Transportation&quot; (CAR-IT). In 2007, <a href="/wiki/Buckeye_Bullet" title="Buckeye Bullet">Buckeye Bullet 2</a> was launched. This follow-up effort was a collaboration between Ohio State engineering students and engineers from the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford Motor Company</a> and will seek to break the landspeed record for hydrogen cell powered vehicles.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>A unique aspect to Ohio State's multi billion dollar endowment is the Student Investment Management Program. Upperclass finance students taking Business Finance 724 are given the opportunity to manage a twenty million dollar investment fund. Returns from the student managed funds often outperform the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a> and frequently even the university's own professional fund managers.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The Residence Hall Advisory Council (RHAC), which is a representative body of all students living in the University's residence halls, helps evaluate and improve the living conditions of the residence halls.</p>

That claim is often disputed by Men's Glee Club, but after consultation with Ohio Union Staff, Agricultural Education Society was named as the university's oldest organization.</p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Diversity">Diversity</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Diversity">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Ohio State's main campus has been lauded in recent years for the racial diversity of its student body. In various surveys and rankings it has been included among the best campuses in the nation for African Americans.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> Additionally, Ohio State ranked 10th in the nation in 2006 for the numbers of African American doctors graduated. <i>The Advocate College Guide for LGBT Students</i> lists Ohio State as one of the best campuses in America for <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> students.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Ohio State, despite selective admissions, has also maintained a high amount of socio-economic diversity among its students. The 2007 freshman class contained 22.7% of first generation college students which far exceeded the national norm on American campuses of 15.9%.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>Ohio State operates 31 on-campus residence halls divided into three geographic clusters: South Campus (site of the university's original dormitories), North Campus (largely constructed during the post-war enrollment boom) and West Campus or &quot;The Towers.&quot; Within the residence hall system are 40 smaller living and learning environments defined by social or academic considerations. Ohio State also offers four honors residence halls: Bradley Hall, Lincoln House, Siebert Hall, and Taylor Tower.</p> 
     <p>Separate housing for graduate and professional students is maintained on the Southern tier of campus near the medical complex. Family housing is maintained at Buckeye Village at the far northern edge of campus beyond the athletic complex. At the university's southeast corner along High Street, and across from the <a href="/wiki/Moritz_College_of_Law" title="Moritz College of Law">Moritz College of Law</a>, new apartments have been built for law students in conjunction with the area's Campus Gateway project.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Honors_programs">Honors programs</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Honors programs">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Ohio State offers two distinct honors programs for high ability <a href="/wiki/Undergraduates" title="Undergraduates" class="mw-redirect">undergraduates</a>: Honors and Scholars. The Honors program is open to students in all majors. The Scholars program is centered around thirteen specific programs such as &quot;Architecture Scholars&quot;, &quot;Media, Marketing, and Communications Scholars&quot;,&quot;Biological Sciences Scholars&quot;, &quot;International Affairs Scholars&quot;, &quot;Business Scholars&quot; and &quot;Politics, Society and Law Scholars.&quot; Students in the Scholars program are expected to live and take select classes with other members of the program. Additionally, Ohio State offers the Honors Collegium with membership extended to ten incoming freshmen and following the Spring of a student's first or second year to the university's top undergraduates. Collegium students try to compete for internships, graduate schools and nationally competitive awards, such as the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Scholarship" title="Marshall Scholarship">Marshall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhodes_Scholarship" title="Rhodes Scholarship">Rhodes</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Truman_Scholarship" title="Truman Scholarship" class="mw-redirect">Truman</a> Scholarships.</p> 
     <p>Ohio State also administers two large-scale <a href="/wiki/Scholarship" title="Scholarship">scholarship programs</a> to ensure access to the university to high-ability students from <a href="/wiki/Low-income" title="Low-income" class="mw-redirect">low-income</a> or traditionally underrepresented groups. The first of these, The Young Scholars Program, was initiated in 1988. 120 promising minority students from Ohio's nine largest urban public school districts are selected prior to entering high school. The program offers a series of academic camps each summer and counseling throughout the students' high school careers. Upon completion of the program, which also mandates a college preparatory curriculum and minimum grade point average, the students are guaranteed admission to Ohio State as well as any need-based financial aid necessary. The Land Grant Scholarship was initiated in 2005. This program seeks to ensure access to Ohio State to high-ability students from low-income backgrounds. Ohio State has committed to offering a full-ride scholarship each academic year to at least one student from each of <a href="/wiki/Ohio_county_government" title="Ohio county government">Ohio's 88 counties</a>.</p> 
     <p>Ohio State maintains an honors center in the Kuhn Honors and Scholars House which had served as the University President's residence until the 1960s. Four dormitories are designated all or in part as honors residences: Taylor, Bradley, Siebert, and Lincoln.</p>

     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Union" title="Ohio Union">Ohio Union</a> was the first student union built by an American public university. The Ohio Union is dedicated to the enrichment of the student experience, on and off The Ohio State University campus. The first Ohio Union, located on the south edge of the South Oval, was constructed in 1909 and was later renamed Enarson Hall. The second Ohio Union was completed in 1950 and was located prominently along High Street, southeast of the Oval. It was a center of student life at The Ohio State University for more than 50 years, providing facilities for student activities, organizations and events, and serving as an important meeting place for campus and community interaction. In addition, many student services and programs were housed in the union, along with dining and recreational facilities. The second Ohio Union was demolished in February 2007 to make way for the new Ohio Union which was finished in 2010. During this time, student activities had been relocated to Ohio Stadium and other academic buildings.</p>

There are over 1,000 registered student organizations that involve many thousands of students.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup> The university's <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Ohio_State_Speech_and_Debate_Team&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="The Ohio State Speech and Debate Team (page does not exist)">debate team</a> has won the state <a href="/wiki/National_Forensics_Association" title="National Forensics Association" class="mw-redirect">National Forensics Association</a> tournament several times.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.      <p><a href="/wiki/Block_O" title="Block O">Block &quot;O&quot;</a> is currently the largest student-run organization on the campus of The Ohio State University. Block &quot;O&quot; serves as the official <a href="/wiki/Student_cheering_section" title="Student cheering section" class="mw-redirect">student cheering section</a> at athletic events for the University.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership_and_service">Leadership and service</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Leadership and service">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The Union's vision is to prepare students to be responsible, engaged leaders committed to community participation for social action and change. Programs with which students can get involved include are the Leadership Collaborative, Leadership Ohio State, Residence Halls Advisory Council, LeaderShape, Buckeye Service Council, Community Commitment Day, SERV team, Service Squad, and BUCK I SERV alternative break trips. Additionally, the Service-Learning Institute offers courses that educate students while also helping the greater community. All of these programs have the ultimate goal of making students better leaders, people, and citizens of Ohio.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_governments">Student governments</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Student governments">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>At The Ohio State University, there are three recognized student governments that represent their constituents.</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Undergraduate Student Government (USG), which consists of elected and appointed student <a href="/wiki/Representation_(politics)" title="Representation (politics)">representatives</a> who serve as liaisons from the <a href="/wiki/Undergraduate" title="Undergraduate" class="mw-redirect">undergraduate</a> student body to university officials. USG seeks to outreach to and work for the students at The Ohio State University.</li> 
      <li>Council of Graduate Students (CGS), which promotes and provides <a href="/wiki/Academic" title="Academic" class="mw-redirect">academic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Academic_administration" title="Academic administration">administrative</a>, and social programs for the university community in general and for <a href="/wiki/Graduate_students" title="Graduate students" class="mw-redirect">graduate students</a> in particular. The Council provides a forum in which the graduate student body may present, discuss, and set upon issues related to its role in the academic and non-academic aspects of the University community.</li> 
      <li>Inter-Professional Council (IPC), which is a representative body of all professional students in the colleges of <a href="/wiki/Dentistry" title="Dentistry">Dentistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">Law</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">Medicine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Optometry" title="Optometry">Optometry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharmacy" title="Pharmacy">Pharmacy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Veterinary_Medicine" title="Veterinary Medicine" class="mw-redirect">Veterinary Medicine</a>. Its purpose is to act as a liaison between these students and the governing bodies of the University.</li> 
     </ul>

     <p>Ohio State's intercollegiate sports teams are called the &quot;Buckeyes&quot; (derived from the colloquial term for people from the state of <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> and after the state tree, the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Buckeye" title="Ohio Buckeye" class="mw-redirect">Ohio Buckeye ? Aesculus glabra</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65"><span>[</span>65<span>]</span></a></sup> and participate in the <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">NCAA</a>'s Division I in all sports (Division I FBS in football) and the <a href="/wiki/Big_Ten_Conference" title="Big Ten Conference">Big Ten Conference</a> in most sports. (The men's <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">hockey</a> program competes in the <a href="/wiki/Central_Collegiate_Hockey_Association" title="Central Collegiate Hockey Association">Central Collegiate Hockey Association</a>, and its women's hockey program competes in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Collegiate_Hockey_Association" title="Western Collegiate Hockey Association">Western Collegiate Hockey Association</a>). The school colors are scarlet and gray. <a href="/wiki/Brutus_Buckeye" title="Brutus Buckeye">Brutus Buckeye</a> is the mascot. Ohio State currently has 36 varsity teams.</p> 
     <p>Ohio State is one of only four universities (the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California at Berkeley</a> being the others) to have won national championships in all three major men's sports (baseball, men's basketball, and football).<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66"><span>[</span>66<span>]</span></a></sup> Ohio State is also one of only two universities to appear in the national championship games in both football and men's basketball in the same calendar year (the other university is the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Florida" title="University of Florida">University of Florida</a>, who defeated Ohio State in both contests in 2007.) Ohio State has also won national championships in men's volleyball, men's swimming &amp; diving, men's outdoor track &amp; field, men's golf, men's gymnastics, men's fencing, co-ed fencing, and multiple synchronized swimming championships.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67"><span>[</span>67<span>]</span></a></sup> The Ohio State equestrian team has won eight Intercollegiate Horse Show Association national championships.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68"><span>[</span>68<span>]</span></a></sup> Since the inception of the <a href="/wiki/NACDA_Director%27s_Cup" title="NACDA Director's Cup" class="mw-redirect">Athletic Director's Cup</a>, Ohio State has finished in the top 25 each year, including top 6 finishes in three of the last five years.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69"><span>[</span>69<span>]</span></a></sup> During the 2005?2006 school year Ohio State became this first Big Ten team to win conference championships in Football, Men's Basketball and Women's Basketball. Ohio State repeated the feat during the 2006?2007 school year, winning solo championships in all three sports. In 2007, Sports Illustrated nicknamed Ohio State's athletic program as being &quot;The Program&quot; due to the unsurpassed facilities, unparalleled amount of men's and women's sport teams, their success, and the financial support of an impressive fan base.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70"><span>[</span>70<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Outstanding sports figures that were student athletes at Ohio State include 1936 Olympics gold medalist <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Owens" title="Jesse Owens">Jesse Owens</a> &quot;the Buckeye Bullet&quot; (track and field), <a href="/wiki/John_Havlicek" title="John Havlicek">John Havlicek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Lucas" title="Jerry Lucas">Jerry Lucas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Knight" title="Bobby Knight" class="mw-redirect">Bobby Knight</a>, <a href="/wiki/Byron_Mullens" title="Byron Mullens">Byron Mullens</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Larry_Siegfried" title="Larry Siegfried">Larry Siegfried</a> (basketball), 2010 Olympics silver medalist <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Kesler" title="Ryan Kesler">Ryan Kesler</a> (ice hockey), <a href="/wiki/Katie_Smith" title="Katie Smith">Katie Smith</a> and the first 3-time player of the year in Big Ten Basketball history <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Davenport" title="Jessica Davenport">Jessica Davenport</a> (women's basketball), <a href="/wiki/Frank_Howard_(baseball_player)" title="Frank Howard (baseball player)" class="mw-redirect">Frank Howard</a> (basketball and baseball), <a href="/wiki/Jack_Nicklaus" title="Jack Nicklaus">Jack Nicklaus</a> (golf); and <a href="/wiki/Chic_Harley" title="Chic Harley">Chic Harley</a> (three-time All-American football running back). Ohio State football players have combined for seven <a href="/wiki/Heisman_Trophy" title="Heisman Trophy">Heisman Awards</a> including the only two-time winner <a href="/wiki/Archie_Griffin" title="Archie Griffin">Archie Griffin</a> in 1974 and 1975, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_George" title="Eddie George">Eddie George</a> in 1995, and most recently <a href="/wiki/Troy_Smith" title="Troy Smith">Troy Smith</a> in 2006. Hall of Fame coaches at Ohio State have included <a href="/wiki/Paul_Brown" title="Paul Brown">Paul Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woody_Hayes" title="Woody Hayes">Woody Hayes</a> in football, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Taylor_(basketball_coach)" title="Fred Taylor (basketball coach)" class="mw-redirect">Fred Taylor</a> in basketball, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Larry_Snyder_(athlete)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Larry Snyder (athlete) (page does not exist)">Larry Snyder</a> in track and field, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Peppe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mike Peppe (page does not exist)">Mike Peppe</a> in swimming and diving. Hall of fame players, in pro-football, include <a href="/wiki/Sid_Gillman" title="Sid Gillman">Sid Gillman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lou_Groza" title="Lou Groza">Lou Groza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dante_Lavelli" title="Dante Lavelli">Dante Lavelli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Parker_(American_football)" title="Jim Parker (American football)">Jim Parker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Warfield" title="Paul Warfield">Paul Warfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dick_LeBeau" title="Dick LeBeau">Dick LeBeau</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bill_Willis" title="Bill Willis">Bill Willis</a>.</p>

     <p>Ohio State operates a <a href="/wiki/Public_television" title="Public television" class="mw-redirect">public</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_station" title="Television station">television station</a>, <a href="/wiki/WOSU-TV" title="WOSU-TV">WOSU-TV</a> (virtual channel 34 / DT 38, a local <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a> <a href="/wiki/TV" title="TV" class="mw-redirect">TV</a> station), as well as two public radio stations, <a href="/wiki/WOSU-FM" title="WOSU-FM">WOSU-FM</a> 89.7(<a href="/wiki/National_Public_Radio" title="National Public Radio" class="mw-redirect">NPR</a>/<a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> <a href="/wiki/Talk_radio" title="Talk radio">news/talk</a>) and <a href="/wiki/WOSA" title="WOSA">WOSA-FM</a> 101.1 (<a href="/wiki/European_classical_music" title="European classical music" class="mw-redirect">Classical</a>, &quot;Classical 101&quot;) in Columbus.</p> 
     <p>In 2003, the television station began broadcasting in <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">high definition</a>.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People">People</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: People">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Faculty">Faculty</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Faculty">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Ohio State?s faculty currently includes a Nobel Prize-winning physicist, 21 members of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_National_Academy_of_Sciences" title="United States National Academy of Sciences" class="mw-redirect">National Academy of Sciences</a> or <a href="/wiki/National_Academy_of_Engineering" title="National Academy of Engineering">National Academy of Engineering</a>, four members of the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Medicine" title="Institute of Medicine">Institute of Medicine</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71"><span>[</span>71<span>]</span></a></sup> and 177 elected fellows of the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_for_the_Advancement_of_Science" title="American Association for the Advancement of Science">American Association for the Advancement of Science</a>. In 2009, 17 Ohio State faculty were elected as AAAS Fellows. Each year since 2002, Ohio State has either led or been second among all American universities in the number of their faculty elected as fellows to the AAAS.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72"><span>[</span>72<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73"><span>[</span>73<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In a recent study by Harvard University's Graduate School of Education, Ohio State was one of five universities rated as &quot;exemplary&quot; workplaces for junior faculty. In the study, 31 universities and 11 liberal arts colleges were evaluated on tenure clarity and fairness, nature of work including workloads, quality of students, and teaching environment, compensation, work and family balance, collegiality and overall satisfaction.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74"><span>[</span>74<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In the last quarter century, 32 Ohio State faculty members have been awarded the prestigious <a href="/wiki/Guggenheim_Fellowship" title="Guggenheim Fellowship">Guggenheim Fellowship</a>, which is more than all other public and private Ohio universities combined. In 2008, three Ohio State faculty were awarded Guggenheim Fellowships, placing Ohio State among the top 15 universities in the nation.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75"><span>[</span>75<span>]</span></a></sup> Since the 2000?2001 award year, 55 Ohio State faculty members have been named as <a href="/wiki/Fulbright_Program" title="Fulbright Program">Fulbright Fellows</a>, the highest of any Ohio university.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76"><span>[</span>76<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alumni">Alumni</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohio_State_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Alumni">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article: 
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Ohio_State_University_people" title="List of Ohio State University people">List of Ohio State University people</a>
     </div> 
     <p>Ohio State currently has over 475,000 living alumni located around the world<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77"><span>[</span>77<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Ohio State alumni include <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pulitzer_Prize" title="Pulitzer Prize">Pulitzer Prize</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a> <a href="/wiki/Gold_Medal" title="Gold Medal" class="mw-redirect">Gold Medalists</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Medal_of_Honor" title="Medal of Honor">Medal of Honor</a> recipients, ambassadors, as well as <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEOs</a> and members of the <a href="/wiki/Forbes_400" title="Forbes 400">Forbes 400</a> list of the world's wealthiest individuals. Numerous graduates have gone on to become Governors, Senators and members of Congress. Ohio State alumni have appeared on the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">TIME</a></i> twelve times, with the artwork of alumnus <a href="/wiki/Roy_Lichtenstein" title="Roy Lichtenstein">Roy Lichtenstein</a> featured on an additional two <i>TIME</i> covers.</p> 
     <p>Ohio State alumni are enshrined in the <a href="/wiki/National_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame_and_Museum" title="National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum">Baseball Hall of Fame</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cooperstown,_New_York" title="Cooperstown, New York">Cooperstown</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pro_Football_Hall_of_Fame" title="Pro Football Hall of Fame">NFL Hall of Fame</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_Hall_of_Fame" title="Basketball Hall of Fame" class="mw-redirect">Basketball Hall of Fame</a>. Its athletes have won a combined eighty-three <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic</a> medals and three times received the <a href="/wiki/James_E._Sullivan_Award" title="James E. Sullivan Award">Sullivan Award</a> as the nation's top amateur athlete. <a href="/wiki/Jack_Nicklaus" title="Jack Nicklaus">Jack Nicklaus</a> has been called &quot;the greatest golfer in history&quot; while <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Owens" title="Jesse Owens">Jesse Owens</a> has been called &quot;the greatest Olympian in history.&quot; Twice, Ohio State alumni have graced the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Sports_Illustrated" title="Sports Illustrated">Sports Illustrated</a></i> as its <a href="/wiki/Sportsman_of_the_Year" title="Sportsman of the Year">Sportsman of the Year</a>. Roboticist <a href="/wiki/James_S._Albus" title="James S. Albus">James S. Albus</a> was named a &quot;Hero of US Manufacturing&quot; by Fortune Magazine in 1997.</p>
