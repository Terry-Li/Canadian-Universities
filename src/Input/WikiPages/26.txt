     <p><b>Brock University</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public</a> <a href="/wiki/Research_university" title="Research university" class="mw-redirect">research university</a> located in <a href="/wiki/St._Catharines" title="St. Catharines">St. Catharines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. It is the only university in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> that is located in a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO_Biosphere_Reserve" title="UNESCO Biosphere Reserve" class="mw-redirect">UNESCO Biosphere Reserve</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-brocku_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brocku-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> located at the center of Canada's <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Peninsula" title="Niagara Peninsula">Niagara Peninsula</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Escarpment" title="Niagara Escarpment">Niagara Escarpment</a>. The university bears the name of <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Brock" title="Isaac Brock">Maj.-General Sir Isaac Brock</a>, who was responsible for defending Upper Canada against the United States during the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>.</p> 
     <p>Brock offers a wide range of programs at the undergraduate and graduate levels, including professional degrees, and is 3rd ranked among Canadian undergraduate research universities based on research publication output and impact indicators.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Brock University is the only school in Canada and internationally to offer the MICA (Mathematics Integrated with Computing and Applications) program.</p> 
     <p>Brock's Co-op program is well known to be Canada’s fifth-largest, and the third largest in <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> as of 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-brocku_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brocku-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Graduates enjoy one of the highest employment rates of all <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> universities at 97.2 percent.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Brock has 12 <a href="/wiki/Canada_Research_Chairs" title="Canada Research Chairs" class="mw-redirect">Canada Research Chairs</a> and 9 faculty members which have received the 3MTeaching Fellowship Award, the only national award that recognizes teaching excellence and educational leadership.<sup id="cite_ref-brocku_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brocku-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>In 1963 the Brock University Founders’ Committee, chaired by Arthur Schmon, offered Dr. James A. Gibson the invitation to become the founding president.<sup id="cite_ref-dr.library.brocku.ca_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dr.library.brocku.ca-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Brock University was established by <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.brocku.ca/university-secretariat/brock-university-act">the Brock University Act</a> in 1964.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> When the University first opened in September 1964, classes were held at the St. Paul Street United Church in downtown <a href="/wiki/St._Catharines" title="St. Catharines">St. Catharines</a> for 13 weeks until the Glenridge Campus was completely renovated.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Brock's Glenridge campus was officially opened on October 19, 1964 with Dr. James A. Gibson as the University's founding President. In 1996 Brock University honoured Gibson by naming the University Library in his honour.<sup id="cite_ref-dr.library.brocku.ca_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dr.library.brocku.ca-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Richard L. Hearn was appointed the University's inaugural <a href="/wiki/Chancellor" title="Chancellor">Chancellor</a> in 1967.</p> 
     <p>Brock University is named after Major-General <a href="/wiki/Sir_Isaac_Brock" title="Sir Isaac Brock" class="mw-redirect">Sir Isaac Brock</a>, who commanded the British and Canadian forces during the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>. Although, the British and Canadian forces went on to win, Isaac Brock lost his life during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Queenston_Heights" title="Battle of Queenston Heights">Battle of Queenston Heights</a> in 1812, fought 20&nbsp;km from the present-day site of the campus. His last words are said to have been <i>Surgite!</i> (Latin for &quot;push on&quot;) — now used as the University's motto. For his contribution to Canada, Isaac Brock was voted the 28th Greatest Canadian in a 2004 poll, conducted by the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Canadian Broadcasting Corporation">Canadian Broadcasting Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Jack_N._Lightstone" title="Jack N. Lightstone">Jack N. Lightstone</a> became the school's president on July 1, 2006. The University Chancellor is Ned Goodman, founder of <a href="/wiki/Dundee_Wealth_Management" title="Dundee Wealth Management" class="mw-redirect">Dundee Wealth Management</a>.</p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Faculty_of_Education">Faculty of Education</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Faculty of Education">edit</a>]</span></h3>

     <p>Brock's Faculty of Education was established in 1965 as the St. Catharines Teachers’ College. In 1971 the Teachers’ College became the College of Education, an integral part of the Brock University community. In 1990, the College of Education designation was changed to the Faculty of Education to better reflect its academic relationship with the university.</p> 
     <p>Today, Brock's Faculty of Education has an outstanding reputation for teacher-education programs. It is widely regarded as one of Ontario's premiere teacher preparation schools, renowned for its strict selectivity (only the top 10% of applicants are admitted).</p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Co-op">Co-op</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Co-op">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Co-op education offers undergraduate students an opportunity to combine academics with valuable work experience.</p> 
     <p>The Faculty of Business at Brock has one of the most extensive co-op offerings in Ontario. The Accounting Co-op program, one of the first in the province, has been in existence for over 20 years. Brock's Co-op programs consistently offer 100% placement - representing the highest placement rate of all Ontario Universities.</p> 
     <p>At the graduate level, degrees are offered in several arts and sciences disciplines.</p> 
     <p>In addition, <a href="/wiki/Concordia_Lutheran_Theological_Seminary" title="Concordia Lutheran Theological Seminary">Concordia Lutheran Theological Seminary</a> (an institution of the <a href="/wiki/Lutheran_Church_-_Canada" title="Lutheran Church - Canada" class="mw-redirect">Lutheran Church - Canada</a>) is affiliated with the university and offers a four-year <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Divinity" title="Master of Divinity">Master of Divinity</a>.</p> 
     <p>Its location in the <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Peninsula" title="Niagara Peninsula">Niagara Peninsula</a> puts Brock at the center of cool-climate grape and wine research. It carries the distinction of being the only university in Canada to offer an Honours <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Bachelor of Science</a> degree in <a href="/wiki/Oenology" title="Oenology">Oenology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Viticulture" title="Viticulture">Viticulture</a> - see the <a href="/wiki/Cool_Climate_Oenology_and_Viticulture_Institute" title="Cool Climate Oenology and Viticulture Institute">Cool Climate Oenology and Viticulture Institute</a>.</p> 
     <p>The very honorable Bachelor of Accountancy Co-op is known as one of the best accounting related programs in the nation of Canada. Recently having its acceptance averages raised to mid 80s - 90s.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_life">Student life</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Student life">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Brock_University_Students%27_Union" title="Brock University Students' Union">Brock University Students' Union</a> represents all undergraduate students at Brock University. It operates various services and businesses run by students. It is located in the Alumni Students' Centre.</p> 
     <p>The Brock Centre for the Arts is located on campus and features two large theatres, The Sean O'Sullivan (537 seats) and The David S. Howes (508 seats). The centre has a strong reputation in the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Municipality_of_Niagara" title="Regional Municipality of Niagara">Niagara Region</a> for presenting some of the biggest names in Canadian Arts and Entertainment, Academics, and Politics.</p> 
     <p>The School of Fine and Performing Arts owns Rodman Hall, a downtown art gallery and teaching centre. The school is exploring a possible joint venture with the City of St. Catharines to operate a performing arts venue nearby (tentative name &quot;Niagara Centre for the Arts&quot;).</p>

Brock University scholarships for <a href="/wiki/Aboriginal_peoples_in_Canada" title="Aboriginal peoples in Canada">Aboriginal</a>, <a href="/wiki/First_Nations" title="First Nations">First Nations</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9tis" title="M&eacute;tis">M&eacute;tis</a> students include: Enbridge Aboriginal Bursary; <sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Over 150 Brock University Donor Awards are available with a combined total of over $500,000.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campus">Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Campus">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arthur_Schmon_Tower">Arthur Schmon Tower</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Arthur Schmon Tower">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="thumb tleft"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Brock_University_Arthur_Schmon_Tower_Aug_2008_2.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Brock_University_Arthur_Schmon_Tower_Aug_2008_2.JPG/250px-Brock_University_Arthur_Schmon_Tower_Aug_2008_2.JPG" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Brock_University_Arthur_Schmon_Tower_Aug_2008_2.JPG/375px-Brock_University_Arthur_Schmon_Tower_Aug_2008_2.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Brock_University_Arthur_Schmon_Tower_Aug_2008_2.JPG/500px-Brock_University_Arthur_Schmon_Tower_Aug_2008_2.JPG 2x" /></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption">

        </div> Arthur Schmon Tower
       </div>

     </div> 
     <p>The Arthur Schmon Tower was built in 1968 and is the flagship building of Brock University. Dr. Schmon was the primary force behind getting a university established in the Niagara peninsula and was the chairman of the Brock University Founders' Committee, formed in 1962. The Schmon Tower building houses the campus Library, as well as the offices of the University administration and the Matheson Learning Commons. On a clear day, atop the Schmon Tower is afforded a direct line-of-sight view of the <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> skyline, across <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">Lake Ontario</a>.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Welch_Hall">Welch Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Welch Hall">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Welch Hall is home to Brock's Faculty of Education, the Instructional Resource Centre, as well as the David S. Howes Theatre. It recently underwent an expansion, designed by Diamond and Schmitt Architects and costing in excess of $8 million, which added additional lecture halls and administrative offices as well as upgrades to Welch Hall's facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mackenzie_Chown_Complex">Mackenzie Chown Complex</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Mackenzie Chown Complex">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Brock_University_campus.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Brock_University_campus.JPG/250px-Brock_University_campus.JPG" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Brock_University_campus.JPG/375px-Brock_University_campus.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Brock_University_campus.JPG/500px-Brock_University_campus.JPG 2x" /></a>

     </div> 
     <p>Originally designed by former Brock Chancellor <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Moriyama" title="Raymond Moriyama">Raymond Moriyama</a>, the Mackenzie Chown Complex primarily contains seminar rooms and science laboratories. The Mackezie Chown Complex also houses the Pond Inlet. Mackenzie Chown was the former mayor of <a href="/wiki/St._Catharines" title="St. Catharines">St. Catharines</a>, chairman of the Brock's Board of Trustees, and chairman of the fund raising committee for a new science laboratory building to be added to the Complex.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taro_Hall">Taro Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Taro Hall">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Taro Hall is home to Brock's <a href="/wiki/Brock_University_Faculty_of_Business" title="Brock University Faculty of Business" class="mw-redirect">Faculty of Business</a>. It was opened in 1990 and was designed by Moriyama &amp; Teshima Architects. Taro Hall is primarily used for Faculty offices, including the Dean of the Faculty of Business, and lecture halls. It contains six lecture halls for undergraduate students (BBA &amp; BAcc), three lecture halls for graduate students (<a href="/wiki/MBA" title="MBA" class="mw-redirect">MBA</a>, MAcc, IMAcc, and MSc), and a number of break-out rooms. There is also a student lounge and computer commons specifically for graduate business students. In December 2007, Taro Hall underwent an extensive renovation to upgrade the entire facility.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Plaza_Building">Plaza Building</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Plaza Building">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:BrockUPlaza.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/BrockUPlaza.jpg/250px-BrockUPlaza.jpg" width="250" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/BrockUPlaza.jpg/375px-BrockUPlaza.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a8/BrockUPlaza.jpg 2x" /></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:BrockUPlaza.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf8/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a>
        </div> Plaza
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>Completed in 2007, and designed by MacKay-Lyons Sweetapple Architects, the Plaza Building contains the Faculty of Social Sciences, as well as computer facilities, seminar rooms and the new <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.campusstore.brocku.ca/">Brock Campus Bookstore</a>. The Campus Store occupies the first floor (considered the 200 level, or level 2, in order to match Brock University's historic method of numbering its facilities). The third floor houses seminar rooms and computer labs, as well as the Political Science Department and its offices. The fourth floor houses additional seminar and computer rooms, as well as the Economics Department and its offices. The fifth and sixth floors of this building are restricted access and can only be entered via security card verification. These two restricted floors are home to the Jack &amp; Nora Walker Canadian Center for Lifespan Development Research.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Walker_Complex">Walker Complex</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Walker Complex">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="thumb tleft"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:BrockUWC.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/dd/BrockUWC.jpg/250px-BrockUWC.jpg" width="250" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/dd/BrockUWC.jpg/375px-BrockUWC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/d/dd/BrockUWC.jpg 2x" /></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:BrockUWC.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf8/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a>
        </div> Walker Complex
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>The Walker Complex contains an athletic complex and academic offices. Originally constructed in 1973 and designed by Moffit, Moffit &amp; Kinoshita Architects, the building has had a number of additions including a major expansion in 2002 by Moriyama &amp; Teshima Architects. The Eleanor Misener Aquatic Centre, an <a href="/wiki/Olympic-size_swimming_pool" title="Olympic-size swimming pool">Olympic size</a> competitive swimming pool that features a depth adjustable hydraulic floor in shallow end (0–4&nbsp;feet), a movable bulkhead, 5m diving tower, two 1m springboards and two 3m springboards, two Tarzan ropes, and a whirlpool. The Zone, a 5,100&nbsp;sq&nbsp;ft (470&nbsp;m<sup>2</sup>) state-of-the-art facility including over 60 pieces of cardio equipment as well as strength training equipment. Fitness and Dance studios are located on the upper level as are classrooms. The lower level includes the Ian D. Beddis Gymnasium, equipped with drop down curtains that can divide the large space into four separate gymnasia. Each area has one basketball, one volleyball and three badminton courts, that will accommodate recreational activities and intramural sports. Convocation is held in this space twice per year. It is 23,900 square feet (2,220&nbsp;m<sup>2</sup>) with a ceiling height of 30&nbsp;ft (9.1&nbsp;m) The Beddis gymnasium 200m suspended track that has a rubberized surface. The Bob Davis gymnasium is home to the <a href="/wiki/Brock_Badgers" title="Brock Badgers">Brock Badgers</a> Basketball, Volleyball and Wrestling teams. The Leo Leblanc Rowing Centre is the training facility for the Badger varsity rowing teams and the St. Catharines Rowing Club. The eight-person tank allows rowers to simulate on-water training.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cairns_Family_Health_and_Bioscience_Research_Complex_.28CFHBRC.29">Cairns Family Health and Bioscience Research Complex (CFHBRC)</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Cairns Family Health and Bioscience Research Complex (CFHBRC)">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.brocku.ca/cairns-health-bioscience">Cairns Family Health and Bioscience Research Complex</a> was completed in 2012. Designed by architects Alliance Architects of Toronto, Canada in association with Payette Associates of Boston, USA, this 176,000 square foot building contains the University's most advanced research laboratories and is intended to advance <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>'s science and technology infrastructure and position Brock as a leader in human health and <a href="/wiki/Biosciences" title="Biosciences" class="mw-redirect">biosciences</a>. CFHBRC researchers, who include six <a href="/wiki/Canada_Research_Chair" title="Canada Research Chair">Canada Research Chairs</a>, conduct world-class research in areas such as <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a> treatments, infectious diseases, <a href="/wiki/Biotechnology" title="Biotechnology">biotechnology</a> and green chemistry. The facility will have a business incubator to forge partnerships between research and industry, and give rise to an economic cluster in advanced health studies and biomanufacturing in the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Municipality_of_Niagara" title="Regional Municipality of Niagara">Niagara Region</a>. The complex will also be home to the Niagara Campus of the <a href="/wiki/McMaster_Faculty_of_Health_Sciences" title="McMaster Faculty of Health Sciences">Michael G. DeGroote School of Medicine</a>. The CFHBRC will be developed based on principles of sustainability and quality environments, following the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Green_Building_Council" title="Canadian Green Building Council" class="mw-redirect">Canadian Green Building Council</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design</a> (LEED) system. A LEED-certified CFHBRC facility will support the University’s vision for quality state-of-the-art facilities with innovative design, quality materials and sustainable systems.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Construction of the CFHBRC began in August 2009, and officially opened in September 2012.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Residences">Residences</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Residences">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Brock's <a href="/wiki/St._Catharines" title="St. Catharines">St. Catharines</a> campus is currently home to 2,391 students, in 6 separate residences.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> The majority of these are first-year students, though there is a sizable population of upper-year students who live in residence as well. Traditional-style residences are serviced by Sodexho food services, who operate the DeCew and Lowenberger Dining Halls, and are also responsible for a number of other miscellaneous residence tasks as well.</p> 
     <p><b>DeCew Residence</b></p> 
     <p>DeCew is the oldest of the Residences, it was built in 1969 and designed by world renown architect John Andrews in association with St. Catharines architects Salter Fleming Secord. DeCew contains 411 beds in a traditional residence style building.</p> 
     <p><b>Gordon and Betty Vallee Residence</b></p> 
     <p>Gordon and Betty Vallee Residence, commonly referred to simply as <i>Vallee</i>, is another traditional-style residence built in 1995. All of the rooms are single-rooms with a washroom shared between two students.</p> 
     <p><b>Alan Earp Residence</b></p> 
     <p>Alan Earp Residence was officially opened in 2001, and is home to 255 students. The buiulding was designed by Moriyama and Teshima Architects.The building is very similar to Vallee Residence, in that all of its rooms are single-rooms with a washroom shared between two students.</p> 
     <p><b>Arnie Lowenberger Residence</b></p> 
     <p>Arnie Lowenberger Residence is the newest of Brock University's residences, and officially opened midway through 2003. The residence building was designed by Teeple Architects in association with Tillmann Ruth Mocellin Architects and is named for Dr. Arnie Lowenberger, Professor emeritus, past Athletic Director and Dean of Physical Education. Dr. Lowenberger was the first Director of Residences for Brock University.</p> 
     <p><b>Village Residence</b></p> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:BrockUQV.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/BrockUQV.jpg/250px-BrockUQV.jpg" width="250" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/BrockUQV.jpg/375px-BrockUQV.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1b/BrockUQV.jpg 2x" /></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:BrockUQV.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf8/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a>
        </div> Quarry View Residence
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>Village Residence currently contains 888 beds for students. It was originally opened in 1989, though expansions followed in 1993, 1998, and 2002. It is one of Brock's two non-traditional style residences, where 5 students in three single-rooms and one double-room share a common living space, as well as a kitchen. Village is not serviced by Sodexo food services, though students living there have the option of purchasing meal plans to use at the university's Dining Halls.</p> 
     <p><b>Quarry View Residence</b></p> 
     <p>Quarry View Residence opened in the fall of 2003, along with Lowenberger. It contains 288 beds, which are divided into 3- or 4-person units entirely in single-rooms. Quarry View, like Village, is a non-traditional residence where students have a common living area and kitchen.</p> 
     <p>The enabling legislation is the <i>Brock University Act, S.O. 1964</i> <sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> Many of the buildings on campus were designed by the architectural firm, <a href="/wiki/Moriyama_%26_Teshima_Architects" title="Moriyama &amp; Teshima Architects" class="mw-redirect">Moriyama &amp; Teshima Architects</a>.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hamilton_Campus">Hamilton Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Hamilton Campus">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Brock University also maintains a campus in the city of <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Ontario" title="Hamilton, Ontario">Hamilton, Ontario</a>.<a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.ed.brocku.ca/hamilton.html">[3]</a> The primary user of this the facility is Faculty of Education, specifically the Pre-service Department and Graduate Studies in Education and Continuing Education.</p> 
     <p>The Hamilton Campus was officially opened November 2, 2000. It is a fully functioning campus complete with modern computer labs, an Instructional Resource Centre / Library, gymnasium, large classrooms, full and part-time faculty office space, food services, conference facilities, a board room, and green spaces within the courtyards and surrounding lawns of the campus.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Downtown_Campus">Downtown Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brock_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Downtown Campus">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Construction is scheduled to commence in the fall of 2012 on the Marilyn I. Walker School of Fine and Performing Arts in <a href="/wiki/Downtown_St._Catharines,_Ontario" title="Downtown St. Catharines, Ontario" class="mw-redirect">downtown St. Catharines</a>.</p>
