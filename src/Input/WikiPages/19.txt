     <p><b>Memorial University of Newfoundland</b>, (colloquially known as <b>Memorial University</b> or <b>&quot;MUN&quot;</b>) is a comprehensive university located primarily in <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador">St. John's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">Newfoundland and Labrador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.</p> 
     <p>With historical strengths in education, engineering, business, geology, and medicine, it is one of the top comprehensive universities in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-Ranking_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ranking-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> With over 17,500 students, it is also the largest university in <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CanadianUniversities_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CanadianUniversities-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> MUN's four main campuses are served by more than 1100 faculty and 2,300 staff members. Memorial University of Newfoundland has been featured in the <a href="/wiki/SJTU" title="SJTU" class="mw-redirect">SJTU</a> World university rankings, the TOP 500 and many other rankings.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campuses">Campuses</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Campuses">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>MUN has four main campuses and two satellite campuses across three regions of Newfoundland and Labrador, and in two countries (Canada and England). The main campus in <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador">St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador</a> is split by Prince Philip Drive where the northern section is bounded by <a href="/wiki/Pippy_Park" title="Pippy Park">Pippy Park</a>, and the southern section is largely surrounded by residential areas.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="St._John.27s_campus">St. John's campus</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: St. John's campus">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The largest campus is located in St. John's. <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip_Drive" title="Prince Philip Drive" class="mw-redirect">Prince Philip Drive</a> runs east-west through the main campus, with Westerland Road bordering it to the west, Elizabeth Avenue to the south and Allandale Road to the east. The majority of the academic buildings are located south of Prince Philip Drive; the Arts and Administration building, Science building, Chemistry and Physics, Mathematics, Music, Education, Physical Education and the Bruneau Centre for Research and Innovation. The University Centre is home to the food court, bookstore, campus bar (&quot;Breezeway&quot;), and the <a href="/wiki/CHMR-FM" title="CHMR-FM">CHMR-FM</a> campus radio station.</p> 
     <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Libraries_and_Archives">Libraries and Archives</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Libraries and Archives">edit</a>]</span></h4> 
     <p>The Memorial University Libraries contain collections of university Archives, Fine Arts 1880' s to present; Human History and Natural Sciences. <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Memorial University of Newfoundland Folklore and Language Archive collection consists of manuscripts, tapes, records, photographs and artifacts pertaining to Maritime Provinces, specifically Newfoundland, Labrador. <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The Queen Elizabeth II Library, contains 1,058,500 monographs, 93,900 maps, 13,600 audio-visual titles and over 9,000 journal titles. The Commons, located on the main floor of the library, has computers available for use by students as well as a Digital Media Centre.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marine_Institute">Marine Institute</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Marine Institute">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Marine_Institute_of_Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland" title="Marine Institute of Memorial University of Newfoundland">Marine Institute</a> is a marine <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_technology#Canada" title="Institute of technology">polytechnic</a> institution located on Ridge Road in St. John's within Pippy Park, north of the city. It has unique facilities such as a full ships bridge simulator and the worlds largest flume tank. It offers degrees, diplomas, certifications and industry training for the maritime sector.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Grenfell_Campus">Grenfell Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Grenfell Campus">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p><i>See <a href="/wiki/Grenfell_Campus,_Memorial_University" title="Grenfell Campus, Memorial University" class="mw-redirect">Main Article</a> on Grenfell Campus for more information.</i></p> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Grenfell_Campus" title="Grenfell Campus">Grenfell Campus</a> is a 185-acre (75&nbsp;ha) site in <a href="/wiki/Corner_Brook,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Corner Brook, Newfoundland and Labrador" class="mw-redirect">Corner Brook</a>. It has approximately 1400 students, 156 faculty, 235 staff and offers programs in the Arts, Fine Arts, Business, Science, Resource Management and Nursing.</p> 
     <p>It had been referred to as <b>Sir Wilfred Grenfell College</b> until September 10, 2010.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Harlow">Harlow</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Harlow">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The Harlow Campus, located in Old Harlow, Essex, England consists of the Maltings, which can accommodate 30 students and Cabot House which can accommodate 10 students. A former schoolhouse and a teacher's cottage have been converted into a lecture facility and an apartment and a former shop has been converted into apartments. Memorial's Harlow Campus is not a school unto itself. Rather, professional schools and academic departments at Memorial use the Harlow Campus to deliver special programs. Harlow offers courses in Biology, Business, Drama &amp; Performance, English Cultural Landscape, Fine Arts (both Theatre and Visual Arts), Faith, Love &amp; Lore and History &amp; Political Science.</p>

     <p>There are over 17,000 students currently enrolled in full and part-time studies at MUN. This number includes both undergraduate and graduate students. Approximately 85% of students are from Newfoundland and Labrador. The most popular programs include Engineering, Business Administration, Nursing, Education, English, Folklore, French, History, Biology, Biochemistry, and Psychology.      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: History">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>Memorial University of Newfoundland is a non-denominational university in <a href="/wiki/St_John%27s,_Newfoundland" title="St John's, Newfoundland" class="mw-redirect">St John's, Newfoundland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Memorial University began as Memorial University College (MUC), which opened in September 1925 at a campus on Parade Street in St. John's.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The first president was J. L. Paton. It offered the first two years of university studies. MUC's initial enrollment was 57 students, rising to a peak of over 400 in the 1940s. In 1933 it merged with the adjacent Normal School and took responsibility for teacher training. During the College's early years it received considerable support from the <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Corporation_of_New_York" title="Carnegie Corporation of New York">Carnegie Corporation of New York</a>.</p> 
     <p>The college was established as a memorial to the Newfoundlanders who had lost their lives on active service during the First World War.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> It was later rededicated to also encompass the province's war dead of the Second World War.<sup id="cite_ref-MUNHistory_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MUNHistory-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alan_Beddoe" title="Alan Beddoe">Alan Beddoe</a> designed the coats of arms for the Memorial University of Newfoundland.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The post-Confederation government elevated the status of Memorial University College to full university status in August 1949, renaming the institution to Memorial University of Newfoundland.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Memorial University was established by the Memorial University Act.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The enrollment in MUN's first year was 307 students. In 1961, enrollment having increased to 1400, MUN moved from Parade Street to its present location on Elizabeth Avenue (Main Campus).<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>On 8 March 1965, the government of Newfoundland announced free tuition for first year students enrolled at Memorial University in St. John's.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Medicine_of_Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland" title="Faculty of Medicine of Memorial University of Newfoundland">Faculty of Medicine of Memorial University of Newfoundland</a> was established in 1967, and the first students were admitted in 1969. It admits approximately 60 students into the M.D. program each year, and also offers M.Sc. and Ph.D. programs.</p> 
     <p>MUN maintains a campus in <a href="/wiki/Harlow" title="Harlow">Harlow</a>, England which opened to students in 1969. This campus has been a popular location for internships in Education, and now offers credit courses, work terms, and internships in a number of areas. The campus accommodates approximately 50 students.</p> 
     <p>Memorial established the Institut Frecker in <a href="/wiki/St._Pierre_and_Miquelon" title="St. Pierre and Miquelon" class="mw-redirect">St. Pierre</a> in 1973, to offer one-semester French immersion programs. It was housed in a building provided by the archdiocese of St. Pierre until 2000. Now known as the Programme Frecker, it is currently run from the <a href="/wiki/FrancoForum" title="FrancoForum">FrancoForum</a>, a language teaching facility owned by the government of St. Pierre. The program is partly supported by the governments of Canada and Newfoundland and Labrador.</p> 
     <p>In September 1975 a campus was opened in <a href="/wiki/Corner_Brook,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Corner Brook, Newfoundland and Labrador" class="mw-redirect">Corner Brook</a>; it was first renamed <a href="/wiki/Sir_Wilfred_Grenfell_College" title="Sir Wilfred Grenfell College" class="mw-redirect">Sir Wilfred Grenfell College</a> in 1979 and renamed again in 2010 as <a href="/wiki/Grenfell_Campus,_Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland" title="Grenfell Campus, Memorial University of Newfoundland" class="mw-redirect">Grenfell Campus, Memorial University of Newfoundland</a>. Currently 1300 students attend Grenfell, which offers full degree programs in several disciplines, including Fine Arts, and partial programs, which can be completed at the main campus, in many other subjects.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>On 1 January 1943 &amp; 21 March 1946 Canada Post issued 'Memorial University College' based on a design by Herman Herbert Schwartz. The 2&cent; / 30&cent;stamps are perforated 12 and were printed by Canadian Bank Note Company, Limited. <sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Schools_and_faculties">Schools and faculties</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Schools and faculties">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>Memorial has six faculties (Arts, Business Administration, Education, Engineering, Medicine, and Science) and six Schools (Graduate Studies, Music, Nursing, Pharmacy, Human Kinetics, Recreation, and Social Work). These offer a wide variety of undergraduate and graduate degree programs.</p> 
     <p>Memorial?s Faculty of Business Administration is recognized as a leader in Canadian business education, offering innovative programs at the undergraduate and graduate levels, including bachelor of commerce, international bachelor of business administration, bachelor of business administration, master of business administration, master of employment relations and PhD degrees.</p> 
     <p>Students can choose to specialize in the following engineering disciplines: <a href="/wiki/Civil_Engineering" title="Civil Engineering" class="mw-redirect">Civil Engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_Engineering" title="Computer Engineering" class="mw-redirect">Computer Engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electrical_Engineering" title="Electrical Engineering" class="mw-redirect">Electrical Engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_Engineering" title="Mechanical Engineering" class="mw-redirect">Mechanical Engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Offshore_construction" title="Offshore construction">Ocean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Naval_architecture" title="Naval architecture">Naval Architectural</a> Engineering (combined degree), <a href="/wiki/Engineering_Management" title="Engineering Management" class="mw-redirect">Engineering Management</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Process_Engineering" title="Process Engineering" class="mw-redirect">Process Engineering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The Memorial University of Newfoundland Department of Biochemistry has an accredited dietetic program. The university is accredited by a professional organization such as the <a href="/wiki/Dietitians_of_Canada" title="Dietitians of Canada">Dietitians of Canada</a> and the university's graduates may subsequently become registered dietitians. <a href="/wiki/List_of_universities_with_accredited_dietetic_programs" title="List of universities with accredited dietetic programs">List of universities with accredited dietetic programs</a></p> 
     <p>Queen's College, an affiliated College of Memorial University, offers diploma and degree studies in theology, pastoral studies, church history and related programs. Queen's College is an associate member of the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Theological_Schools_in_the_United_States_and_Canada" title="Association of Theological Schools in the United States and Canada">Association of Theological Schools in the United States and Canada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The university operates the <a href="/wiki/Bonne_Bay_Marine_Station" title="Bonne Bay Marine Station">Bonne Bay Marine Station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gros_Morne_National_Park" title="Gros Morne National Park">Gros Morne National Park</a>.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Motto.2C_shield.2C_and_arms">Motto, shield, and arms</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Motto, shield, and arms">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>The university's motto, <i>Provehito in Altum</i> (Launch forth into the deep), captures the spirit of the adventure of learning and urges students to extend the frontiers of knowledge. The shield of arms bears a representation of the sea, books, and a cross. The Arms of Memorial University have as their central element a <a href="/wiki/Cross_(heraldry)" title="Cross (heraldry)" class="mw-redirect">cross moline</a>, which is a fitting symbol for an institution dedicated to the memory of soldiers of Newfoundland who died during the Great Wars.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland_Botanical_Garden">Memorial University of Newfoundland Botanical Garden</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Memorial University of Newfoundland Botanical Garden">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland_Botanical_Garden" title="Memorial University of Newfoundland Botanical Garden">Memorial University of Newfoundland Botanical Garden</a> is a botanical garden operated by the university. The collection consists of perennial and annual plants, aquatic plant, spruce, fir, and alder trees. The garden is located at Oxen Pond along Mount Scio Road in the suburbs of St. John's and was founded in 1972. <sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup>The park has adopted the Twinflower (<i><a href="/wiki/Linnaea_borealis" title="Linnaea borealis" class="mw-redirect">Linnaea borealis</a></i>) as its emblem.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports.2C_clubs.2C_and_traditions">Sports, clubs, and traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Sports, clubs, and traditions">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>MUN's sports teams, the <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Sea-Hawks" title="Memorial Sea-Hawks">Memorial Sea-Hawks</a> (formerly The <a href="/wiki/Beothuks" title="Beothuks" class="mw-redirect">Beothuks</a>), are part of the Atlantic University Sport (AUS) league of <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Interuniversity_Sport" title="Canadian Interuniversity Sport">Canadian Interuniversity Sport</a>. There are varsity teams in men's and women's basketball, cross-country, soccer, swimming, volleyball and wrestling.</p> 
     <p>Memorial University also has a very active student media including a radio station, <a href="/wiki/CHMR-FM" title="CHMR-FM">CHMR-FM</a>, and a newspaper, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muse_(student_paper)" title="The Muse (student paper)">The Muse</a></i>.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rankings">Rankings</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Rankings">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>The Globe and Mail's 2009 University Report Card, which is a national survey on student satisfaction gave MUN the following grades in the Medium-sized University category:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Quality of Teaching: B+ (6th in Canada)</li> 
      <li>Quality of Education: A- (6th in Canada)</li> 
      <li>Most Satisfied Students: A- (6th in Canada)</li> 
      <li>Class Size: A- (4th in Canada)</li> 
      <li>Student Faculty Interaction: B (6th in Canada)</li> 
      <li>Course Availability: B (4th in Canada)</li> 
      <li>Student Services: B+ (2nd in Canada)</li> 
      <li>Libraries: A- (3rd in Canada)</li> 
      <li>Academic reputation: B (7th in Canada)</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>On November 11, 2010 Maclean's magazine ranked MUN number 5 of 12 in its comprehensive university rankings for the second year in a row.</p> 
     <p>In the 2007 Academic Ranking of World Universities published by The Institute of Higher Education of Shanghai Jiao Tong University, MUN placed in the fourth quintile category (305th?401st overall) of the top 500 universities of the world.</p>
