     <p>The <b>University of Windsor</b> (<b>U of W</b> or <b>UWindsor</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public</a> comprehensive and <a href="/wiki/Research_university" title="Research university" class="mw-redirect">research university</a> in <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Ontario" title="Windsor, Ontario">Windsor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It is Canada's southernmost university.<sup id="cite_ref-aucc.ca_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aucc.ca-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> It has a student population of approximately 15,000 full-time and part-time undergraduate students and over 1000 graduate students.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The University of Windsor has graduated more than 100,000 alumni since its founding.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The University of Windsor has nine faculties, including the Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences, the Faculty of Education, the Faculty of Engineering, <a href="/wiki/Odette_School_of_Business" title="Odette School of Business">Odette School of Business</a>, the Faculty of Graduate Studies, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Windsor_Faculty_of_Human_Kinetics" title="University of Windsor Faculty of Human Kinetics" class="mw-redirect">Faculty of Human Kinetics</a>, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Windsor_Faculty_of_Law" title="University of Windsor Faculty of Law">Faculty of Law</a>, the Faculty of Nursing, and the Faculty of Science. Through its various faculties and independent schools, Windsor's primary research interests focus on automotive, environmental, and social justice research, yet it has increasingly began focusing on health, natural science, and entrepreneurship research.<sup id="cite_ref-UWindsor_Research_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UWindsor_Research-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Recently, the University of Windsor has established a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.uwindsor.ca/medicine/">School of Medicine</a> in partnership with the Schulich School of Medicine &amp; Dentistry from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Western_Ontario" title="University of Western Ontario">University of Western Ontario</a>. Currently, the University of Windsor is constructing a $112-million <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.uwindsor.ca/cei/">Centre for Engineering Innovation</a>, which will house the Faculty of Engineering and its research centre.<sup id="cite_ref-UWindsor_Engineering_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UWindsor_Engineering-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>The University dates back to the founding of <a href="/wiki/Assumption_University_(Windsor,_Ontario)" title="Assumption University (Windsor, Ontario)">Assumption College</a> <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic" class="mw-redirect">Roman Catholic</a> in <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Ontario" title="Windsor, Ontario">Windsor, Ontario</a> in 1857.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Assumption College, a primarily theological institution, was founded by the Basilian Fathers of the priestly teaching Order of St. Basil, in 1857. The college grew steadily, expanding its curriculum and affiliating with numerous other colleges over the years.<sup id="cite_ref-heritagefdn.on.ca_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-heritagefdn.on.ca-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 1919 Assumption College in Windsor affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Western_Ontario" title="University of Western Ontario">University of Western Ontario</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Originally, Assumption was one the largest colleges associated with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Western_Ontario" title="University of Western Ontario">University of Western Ontario</a>.</p> 
     <p>Escalating costs forced Assumption University (a denominational university) to become a public institution in order to qualify for public support.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>It was granted university status in 1953.<sup id="cite_ref-heritagefdn.on.ca_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-heritagefdn.on.ca-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The War Memorial Hall (more generally known as Memorial Hall) is a landmark building on the campus of the University of Windsor used as classrooms, labs, and offices. Memorial Hall honours alumni who had enlisted and died in the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War" class="mw-redirect">First World War</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War" class="mw-redirect">Second World War</a>. A bronze tablet remembers the alumni of Assumption College who died in the Second World War.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 1950, Assumption College welcomed its first women students. In 1953, it ended its affiliation with the University of Western Ontario. It was incorporated as the independent non-denominational University of Windsor through an Act of the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Ontario" title="Legislative Assembly of Ontario">Legislative Assembly of Ontario</a> on December 19, 1962. Soon after, affiliation agreements were made with Holy Redeemer College, Canterbury College and the new Iona College (affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/United_Church_of_Canada" title="United Church of Canada">United Church of Canada</a>). <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.uwindsor.ca/canterbury">Canterbury College</a> became the first <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Church_of_Canada" title="Anglican Church of Canada">Anglican</a> college in the world to affiliate with a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic" class="mw-redirect">Roman Catholic</a> University. In 1963, University of Windsor affiliated with Essex College. <sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Essex College's Arms and Badge were registered with the Canadian Heraldic Authority on March 15, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>On July 1, 1963, the University of Windsor assumed control of the campus. <a href="/wiki/Alan_Beddoe" title="Alan Beddoe">Alan Beddoe</a> designed the University's coats of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>In 1964 Dr. <a href="/wiki/John_Francis_Leddy" title="John Francis Leddy" class="mw-redirect">John Francis Leddy</a> became President of the University of Windsor, and presided over a period of significant growth. From 1967 to 1977, Windsor grew from approximately 1,500 to 8,000 full-time students. In the 1980s and early 1990s, this growth continued. Among the new buildings erected were the Odette Business Building and the CAW Student Centre.</p> 
     <p>Enrollment at the University reached record heights in Fall 2003 with the elimination of Grade 13 (<a href="/wiki/Ontario_Academic_Credit" title="Ontario Academic Credit">Ontario Academic Credit</a>) in Ontario. The university has developed a number of partnerships with local businesses and industry, such as the University of Windsor/<a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler</a> Canada Ltd. Automotive Research and Development Centre, the only one of its kind in North America.</p> 
     <p>University of Windsor's Arms were registered with the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Heraldic_Authority" title="Canadian Heraldic Authority">Canadian Heraldic Authority</a> on March 15, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The University of Windsor has entered into a strategic partnership with <a href="/wiki/Maple_Leaf_Sports_and_Entertainment" title="Maple Leaf Sports and Entertainment" class="mw-redirect">Maple Leaf Sports and Entertainment</a>.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Together, the two will create new student internships, pursue research opportunities and establish promotions with the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Maple_Leafs" title="Toronto Maple Leafs">Toronto Maple Leafs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Raptors" title="Toronto Raptors">Raptors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toronto_FC" title="Toronto FC">Toronto FC</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Marlies" title="Toronto Marlies">Toronto Marlies</a> hockey club. The agreement is the first partnership of its kind for both organizations.</p>

     <p>The Faculty of Law is one of six in Ontario, and has a major teaching and research focus on <a href="/wiki/Social_Justice" title="Social Justice" class="mw-redirect">Social Justice</a> issues. It publishes two <a href="/wiki/Law_journals" title="Law journals" class="mw-redirect">law journals</a> the Faculty led <i>Access to Justice</i> and the student run, peer-reviewed <i>Windsor Review of Legal and Social Issues</i>.</p> 
     <p>Law students may study <a href="/wiki/Human_Rights_Law" title="Human Rights Law" class="mw-redirect">Human Rights Law</a>, Poverty Law, Aboriginal rights law and legal issues affecting women, minorities and children. The faculty, in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/Legal_Aid_Ontario" title="Legal Aid Ontario">Legal Aid Ontario</a>, runs a downtown Windsor community <a href="/wiki/Legal_clinic" title="Legal clinic">legal clinic</a> called Legal Assistance Windsor, that is staffed with supervising lawyers, law students, and social workers; it is aimed at meeting the legal needs of persons traditionally denied access to justice. This clinic operates in the area of landlord tenant law as well as social benefits.</p> 
     <p>The University of Windsor runs a second <a href="/wiki/Legal_clinic" title="Legal clinic">legal clinic</a>, Community Legal Aid, located at the corner of Sunset and University. This clinic is a Student Legal Aid Services Society (SLASS) clinic, which is staffed primarily by volunteer law students and overseen by supervising lawyers, called review counsel. This clinic operates primarily in the areas of criminal law, landlord tenant law, and small claims court. The clinic offers free legal services to those who qualify financially and all students of the University of Windsor.</p> 
     <p>The faculty also has a joint, <a href="/wiki/American_Bar_Association" title="American Bar Association">American Bar Association</a> ABA-Approved <a href="/wiki/LL.B" title="LL.B" class="mw-redirect">LL.B</a>-<a href="/wiki/Juris_Doctor" title="Juris Doctor">J.D.degree</a> program with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Detroit_Mercy" title="University of Detroit Mercy">University of Detroit Mercy</a>. The program is completed in three years with students taking courses at both the University of Windsor and the University of Detroit Mercy. Upon completion students earn both Canadian and American legal accreditation and can pursue licensing in any Province in Canada (aside from civil law in Quebec) and any State in the United States of America.</p> 
     <p>As of 2008, the University of Windsor is also home to a satellite campus of the Schulich School of Medicine &amp; Dentistry of the University of Western Ontario. There are currently 92 medical students studying full-time at the Windsor campus.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>Located in Canada's traditional &quot;automotive capital&quot; across the border from <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>, the campus is situated near the United States and its busy port of entry to and from the United States. It is framed by the <a href="/wiki/Ambassador_Bridge" title="Ambassador Bridge">Ambassador Bridge</a> to the west and the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_River" title="Detroit River">Detroit River</a> to the north.</p> 
     <p>The campus covers 51 hectares (130&nbsp;acres) (contiguous) and is surrounded by a residential neighborhood. The campus features a small arboretum which represents most of the species from the <a href="/wiki/Carolinian_forest" title="Carolinian forest">Carolinian forest</a>. Campus is approximately a 10 minute drive from downtown Windsor. The CAW Student Centre has a view of the Ambassador Bridge and houses retail stores, a food court and &quot;The Basement&quot;, the student-operated pub.</p> 
     <p>The St. Denis Centre, located at the south end of campus on College Avenue, is the major athletic and recreational facility for students; a weight room, exercise facilities and a swimming pool. The new South Campus Stadium built for the 2005 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pan-American_Junior_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pan-American Junior Games (page does not exist)">Pan-American Junior Games</a> is beside the St. Denis Centre - which also has dressing rooms for Lancer teams - and borders Huron Church Road, the major avenue to and from the border crossing. The athletics department has become well known for Track &amp; Field, and Men and Women's Basketball. The majority of the Lancer teams made the playoffs this year (2010) and the program continues to grow in championship titles.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>International students make up approximately 10% of the student population; about 1500 students from more than 70 countries.</p> 
     <p>Despite the large number of international students, the majority of students are domestic and come from the southwestern Ontario counties of Essex, <a href="/wiki/Chatham-Kent" title="Chatham-Kent">Chatham-Kent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lambton_County" title="Lambton County">Lambton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Martin_Law_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Paul Martin Law Library (page does not exist)">Paul Martin Law Library</a> serves the Faculty of Law. The <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Auto_Workers_Union" title="Canadian Auto Workers Union" class="mw-redirect">Canadian Auto Workers Union</a> helped to build the <a href="/w/index.php?title=CAW_Student_Centre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="CAW Student Centre (page does not exist)">CAW Student Centre</a> which is a central meeting place for students. The University has a unique agreement with the <a href="/wiki/Ambassador_Bridge" title="Ambassador Bridge">Ambassador</a> Duty-Free Store, at Canada's busiest border crossing, which provides student jobs, 400 parking spaces, and an annual cash annuity to the school.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Residence_life">Residence life</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Windsor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Residence life">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>The University houses male and female students in six different residence halls across campus.</p> 
     <p>Alumni Hall is the only building on campus that houses Beyond First Year and first year students in the same building but not in the same rooms. It is a suite-style residence where rooms have two bedrooms that share a kitchenette, and 3-piece bathroom. Because of demand, entrance to Alumni Hall is based on grade-point average for first year undergraduate students.</p> 
     <p>Cartier, Laurier and Macdonald Halls are home to first-year undergraduate students; Laurier Hall hosts alternating single-gender floors while Cartier and Macdonald Halls are entirely co-ed floors.</p> 
     <p>Clark Residence, another Beyond First Year residence (returning, undergraduates, graduate, transfer and exhange students, is a collection of townhouse style apartments which include full kitchen facilities in each unit. Clark is available only to Beyond First students.</p> 
     <p>Electa Hall is a co-ed building which houses Beyond First Year students (returning, undergraduates, graduate, transfer and exchange students) and students in professional programs such as education and law. Electa Hall hosts the Law residence community and many other communities.</p>

     <p>The University is represented in <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Interuniversity_Sport" title="Canadian Interuniversity Sport">Canadian Interuniversity Sport</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Windsor_Lancers" title="Windsor Lancers">Windsor Lancers</a>. Windsor Lancers Ice Hockey team plays at the Windsor Arena, previous home to the Windsor Spitfires OHL hockey team.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scholarships">Scholarships</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Windsor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Scholarships">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>The University joined Project Hero, a scholarship program cofounded by General (Ret'd) <a href="/wiki/Rick_Hillier" title="Rick Hillier">Rick Hillier</a>, for the families of fallen <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Forces" title="Canadian Forces">Canadian Forces</a> members.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The University established Rosa Schreiber Award with the assistance of former University of Windsor Professor Economics, <a href="/wiki/Alan_A._Brown" title="Alan A. Brown">Alan A. Brown</a>. From the University's Senate Committee on Student Awards: The competition award is open to Arts or Social Science students in Year 2 or beyond. Applicants must submit a 1,500-2,000 word essay on some aspect of moral courage. Submission must be made to the Office of Student Awards. This competition will be held in alternate years. It was established in 1995 to honour Rosa Schreiber, an Austrian Freedom Fighter who risked her life to help others during World War II.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Windsor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>The University's current President is Dr. Alan Wildeman, formerly VP Research at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Guelph" title="University of Guelph">University of Guelph</a>. He took office on July 1, 2008, as the University’s sixth President and Vice-Chancellor, succeeding Dr. <a href="/wiki/Ross_H._Paul" title="Ross H. Paul">Ross H. Paul</a>.</p>
