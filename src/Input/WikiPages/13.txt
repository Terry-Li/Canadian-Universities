     <p><b>Simon Fraser University</b> (<b>SFU</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Canadian" title="Canadian" class="mw-redirect">Canadian</a> <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public research university</a> in <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> with its main campus on <a href="/wiki/Burnaby_Mountain" title="Burnaby Mountain">Burnaby Mountain</a> in <a href="/wiki/Burnaby" title="Burnaby">Burnaby</a>, and operates satellite campuses in downtown <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a> and <a href="/wiki/Surrey,_British_Columbia" title="Surrey, British Columbia">Surrey</a>. The 1.7&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> (0.66&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi) main campus on Burnaby Mountain, located 20&nbsp;km (12&nbsp;mi) from downtown Vancouver, was established in 1965 and comprises more than 30,000 students and approximately 950 faculty members. The university is adjacent to an urban village, <a href="/wiki/UniverCity" title="UniverCity">UniverCity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The university was named after <a href="/wiki/Simon_Fraser_(explorer)" title="Simon Fraser (explorer)">Simon Fraser</a>, a <a href="/wiki/North_West_Company" title="North West Company">North West Company</a> fur trader and explorer. Undergraduate and graduate programs operate on a year-round tri-semester schedule.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> It is the only non-American university in the <a href="/wiki/NCAA" title="NCAA" class="mw-redirect">National Collegiate Athletic Association</a> (NCAA). In 2007, Simon Fraser University was the first and remains the only university to be awarded the Prix du XXe si&egrave;cle from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Architectural_Institute_of_Canada" title="Royal Architectural Institute of Canada">Royal Architectural Institute of Canada</a> recognizing the &quot;enduring excellence of nationally significant architecture&quot;.<sup id="cite_ref-sfu.ca_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfu.ca-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>SFU was ranked first among Canada's comprehensive universities in 2009, 2010, 2011 and 2012 by <i><a href="/wiki/Maclean%27s" title="Maclean's">Maclean's</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-macleans_rank_2009_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-macleans_rank_2009-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-macleans_rank_2010_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-macleans_rank_2010-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-macleans_rank_2011_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-macleans_rank_2011-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a>]</span></h2>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding">Founding</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Founding">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Simon Fraser University was founded upon the recommendation of a 1962 report entitled <i>Higher Education in British Columbia and a Plan for the Future</i>, by Dr. J.B. Macdonald, who recommended the creation of a new university in the Lower Mainland. The British Columbia Legislature gave formal assent on March 1, 1963 for the establishment of the university in Burnaby.<sup id="cite_ref-Pound_2005_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pound_2005-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In May of the same year, Dr. <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Shrum" title="Gordon Shrum">Gordon M. Shrum</a> was appointed as the university's first Chancellor. From a variety of sites which were offered, Shrum recommended to the provincial government that the summit of Burnaby Mountain, 365 meters above sea level, be chosen for the new university. Architects <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Erickson" title="Arthur Erickson">Arthur Erickson</a> and Geoffrey Massey won a competition to design the university, and construction began in the spring of 1964. The campus faces northwest over Burrard Inlet. Eighteen months later, on September 9, 1965, the university began its first semester<sup id="cite_ref-Pound_2005_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pound_2005-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> with 2,500 students.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_activism">Early activism</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early activism">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The campus was noted in the 1960s and early 1970s as a hotbed of political activism, culminating in a crisis in the Department of Political Science, Sociology, and Anthropology in a dispute involving ideological differences among faculty. The resolution to the crisis included the dismantling of the department into today's separate departments.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template"><span title="&nbsp;since April 2013" style="white-space: nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot">dead link</a></i>]</span></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_Arms">Coat of Arms</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Coat of Arms">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The school's original <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> was used from the university's inception until 2006, at which point the Board of Governors voted to adapt the old coat of arms and thereby register a second coat of arms. The adaptation replaced two crosslets with books after some in the university asserted the crosses had misled prospective foreign students into believing SFU was a private, religious institution rather than a public, secular one.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2007, the university decided to register both the old coat of arms and the revised coat of arms featuring the books. In 2007, a new marketing logo was unveiled, consisting of white letters on block red.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_University_today">The University today</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The University today">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Andrew_petter_.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Andrew_petter_.jpg/220px-Andrew_petter_.jpg" width="220" height="304" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Andrew_petter_.jpg/330px-Andrew_petter_.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Andrew_petter_.jpg/440px-Andrew_petter_.jpg 2x" /></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:Andrew_petter_.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf9/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a>
        </div> Prof. Andrew Petter, the President and Vice Chancellor of SFU.
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>SFU's president is <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Petter" title="Andrew Petter">Andrew Petter</a>, whose term began on September 1, 2010. Petter succeeds Dr. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Stevenson" title="Michael Stevenson">Michael Stevenson</a>. who held a decade-long post as President from 2000 to 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 2009, SFU became the first Canadian university to be accepted into the <a href="/wiki/NCAA" title="NCAA" class="mw-redirect">National Collegiate Athletic Association</a> (NCAA).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Starting in the 2011-2012 season, SFU will be competing in the NCAA's Division II Great Northwest Athletic Conference (GNAC). Once completed, the transition will move all 19 <a href="/wiki/Simon_Fraser_Clan" title="Simon Fraser Clan">Simon Fraser Clan</a> teams into the NCAA.</p> 
     <p>SFU's research efforts have led it to obtain the highest publication impact among Canadian comprehensive universities, and the highest success rates per faculty member in competitions for Federal research council funding from <a href="/wiki/NSERC" title="NSERC" class="mw-redirect">NSERC</a> and <a href="/wiki/SSHRC" title="SSHRC" class="mw-redirect">SSHRC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2007, the University began offering <a href="/wiki/Double_degree" title="Double degree">dual and double degrees</a> by partnering with international universities. The first partnership involved <a href="/wiki/Zhejiang_University" title="Zhejiang University">Zhejiang University</a> and a dual Computing-Science degree. One year later, the University partnered with Australia's <a href="/wiki/Monash_University" title="Monash University">Monash University</a>, to offer a double Bachelor of Arts degree.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academics">Academics</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Academics">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>SFU has been rated as Canada's best comprehensive university (in 1993, 1996, 1997, 1998, 2000, 2008, 2009, 2010, 2011, and 2012) in the annual rankings of Canadian universities in <i><a href="/wiki/Maclean%27s" title="Maclean's">Maclean's</a></i> magazine since 1991. The <a href="/wiki/Rankings_of_universities_in_Canada#Higher_Education_Strategy_Associates.27_rankings" title="Rankings of universities in Canada">Higher Education Strategy Associates</a> ranked Simon Fraser University 6th nationally in Science and Engineering and 10th nationally in Social Sciences and Humanities.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> Research Infosource, Canada's leading provider of research intelligence evaluation, named SFU the top comprehensive university in Canada for &quot;publication effectiveness&quot; in 2006. Similar to most <a href="/wiki/List_of_universities_in_Canada" title="List of universities in Canada">Canadian universities</a>, SFU is a public university, with more than half of funding coming from taxpayers and the remaining from tuition fees.</p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Undergraduate">Undergraduate</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Undergraduate">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>In <a href="/wiki/Academic_year" title="Academic year" class="mw-redirect">academic year</a> 2010-11, SFU is home to 29,697 undergraduates, with 14,911 of them being full-time and 14,786 part-time.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> The university has grown in recent years recently achieving an alumni population of over 100,000. It has 946 faculty members and 3,403 staff.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> In fall semester 2012, 4,269 International students enrolled, making up 17% of the undergraduate student body, one of the highest among Canadian universities. The majority of these international students (60%) come from Mainland China, followed by Hong Kong (6%) and South Korea (6%).<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> SFU's student union is known as the <a href="/wiki/Simon_Fraser_Student_Society" title="Simon Fraser Student Society">Simon Fraser Student Society</a> (SFSS), which includes undergraduates who study at SFU.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Graduate">Graduate</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Graduate">edit</a>]</span></h3>

A Graduate Student Society<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> supports and advocates for graduate students at the university.</p>

     <p>Simon Fraser University has three campuses, each located in different parts of <a href="/wiki/Greater_Vancouver" title="Greater Vancouver">Greater Vancouver</a>. SFU's main campus is located in Burnaby, atop Burnaby Mountain. Two satellite campuses are located in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver's</a> Downtown at <a href="/wiki/Harbour_Centre" title="Harbour Centre">Harbour Centre</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Surrey,_British_Columbia" title="Surrey, British Columbia">Surrey</a>. The downtown campus has expanded to include several other buildings in recent years, including the <a href="/wiki/Segal_Graduate_School_of_Business" title="Segal Graduate School of Business" class="mw-redirect">Segal Graduate School of Business</a>, now known officially as <a href="/wiki/SFU_Vancouver" title="SFU Vancouver" class="mw-redirect">SFU Vancouver</a>. In September 2010, SFU Contemporary Arts<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> moved into the Woodward's redevelopment, known as the Goldcorp Centre for the Arts. SFU's three campuses are all accessible by public transit. The Vancouver campus is a block away from the <a href="/wiki/Waterfront_Station_(Vancouver)" title="Waterfront Station (Vancouver)">Waterfront SkyTrain station</a> while the Surrey campus is adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/Surrey_Central_Station" title="Surrey Central Station">Surrey Central SkyTrain station</a>. The Burnaby campus is linked to the <a href="/wiki/Production_Way-University_Station" title="Production Way-University Station" class="mw-redirect">Production Way-University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sperling-Burnaby_Lake_Station" title="Sperling-Burnaby Lake Station" class="mw-redirect">Sperling-Burnaby Lake</a> SkyTrain stations by frequent shuttle bus service.</p>

     <p>The main campus is located atop <a href="/wiki/Burnaby_Mountain" title="Burnaby Mountain">Burnaby Mountain</a>, at an elevation of 365 metres, overlooking the Burrard inlet to the north. All major departments in the university are housed at the Burnaby campus. The library on the main campus is called the <a href="/wiki/W._A._C._Bennett" title="W. A. C. Bennett">W. A. C. Bennett</a> Library, named after the <a href="/wiki/Social_Credit" title="Social Credit" class="mw-redirect">Social Credit</a> Premier of B.C. who established it. The campus also has two gym-complexes, named the Lorne-Davies Complex and Chancellor's Gym. An international-sized swimming pool is located within the Lorne-Davies Complex. Since the relocation of the School of Contemporary Arts to the Woodward's location, the Burnaby campus production theatre has been vacant. Located within the heart of the campus is the Museum of Archaeology and Ethnology and three art galleries. The campus has been awarded numerous architectural awards over the years, including the Gold Medal for Lieutenant-Governor 2009 Awards in Architecture and the 2007 Royal Architectural Institute of Canada's Prix du XXe si&egrave;cle.<sup id="cite_ref-sfu.ca_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfu.ca-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Libraries.2C_archives.2C_museums_and_galleries">Libraries, archives, museums and galleries</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Libraries, archives, museums and galleries">edit</a>]</span></h4> 
     <p>The SFU Burnaby Campus has a single library called the W. A. C. Bennett Library which holds over 2 million published books, and 6000 print subscriptions. Along with the UniverCity development agreement, residents of UniverCity are also allowed to borrow books from the library. An additional art gallery is located inside the library. SFU also has a Museum of Archeology and Ethnology, which holds many exhibits on lease from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_British_Columbia_Museum" title="Royal British Columbia Museum">Royal British Columbia Museum</a> in Victoria. The exhibits are created by students as part of the museum studies courses offered in the Department of Archaeology. Archaeological collections arising from excavations and other research by faculty, staff and students are also housed in the museum.</p> 
     <p>Also located at the SFU Library is the Electronic Document Centre, which provides internet access to digitized documents from a number of archival collections, such as <a href="/wiki/Harrison_Brown" title="Harrison Brown">Harrison Brown</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an_Incident" title="Xi'an Incident">Xi'an Incident</a> collection,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> and the history of British Columbia and Western Canada in general, including documents from the <a href="/wiki/Doukhobor" title="Doukhobor">Doukhobor</a> migration from the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> to <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a> and then to British Columbia assembled for donation to the university by <a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Keenlyside&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="John Keenlyside (page does not exist)">John Keenlyside</a><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The Bill Reid Centre for Northwest Coast Art Studies at SFU houses a collection of 50,000 objects, primarily digital images and digitized textual documents, which document the art, culture and history of different First Nations cultures of the Northwest Coast. The collection includes explorers? drawings, sketches, paintings and original photography.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UniverCity">UniverCity</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: UniverCity">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article: 
      <a href="/wiki/UniverCity" title="UniverCity">UniverCity</a>
     </div> 
     <p>UniverCity is an urban community located on top of <a href="/wiki/Burnaby_Mountain" title="Burnaby Mountain">Burnaby Mountain</a>, adjacent to Simon Fraser University. It has won several awards for sustainable planning and development.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> Envisioned in 1963 by Arthur Erickson and Geoffrey Massey, the area adjacent to the University was not officially rezoned for development until 30 years later. Development of the community began in early 2000, when Simon Fraser University commenced construction on a new residential and commercial area occupying approximately 200 acres (0.81&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>) adjacent to the campus. As of September 2011, approximately 3000 people live in UniverCity. The main commercial district on University High Street now houses restaurants, stores, and a 20,000 square foot Nester's Market. A new elementary school, University Highlands Elementary, opened on September 1, 2010. Several new residential developments are currently in progress, including the construction of a 12-storey highrise in the heart of UniverCity.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>The SFU Surrey campus is the most recent satellite campus. It is located in downtown <a href="/wiki/Surrey,_British_Columbia" title="Surrey, British Columbia">Surrey</a>, B.C., a quickly growing suburb of Vancouver. The campus is part of <a href="/wiki/Central_City_Shopping_Centre" title="Central City Shopping Centre">Central City</a>, an architectural complex adjacent to the Surrey Central <a href="/wiki/SkyTrain_(Vancouver)" title="SkyTrain (Vancouver)">SkyTrain</a> station. It was established in 2002 to absorb the students and programs of the former <a href="/wiki/Technical_University_of_British_Columbia" title="Technical University of British Columbia">Technical University of British Columbia</a> which was closed by the provincial government. It has since expanded to house the Surrey operations of other SFU programs. The Central City complex that houses the campus was designed by architect <a href="/wiki/Bing_Thom" title="Bing Thom">Bing Thom</a> and opened in 2006.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SFU_Vancouver">SFU Vancouver</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: SFU Vancouver">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>SFU Vancouver was launched in the 1980s with a store-front classroom. It was the first urban university classroom in British Columbia. A significant portion of funding for the building of the campus came from the private sector. The Vancouver campus has four buildings spread across the downtown core: SFU <a href="/wiki/Harbour_Centre" title="Harbour Centre">Harbour Centre</a>, the Morris J Wosk Centre for Dialogue, the <a href="/wiki/SFU_Business" title="SFU Business" class="mw-redirect">Segal Graduate School of Business</a> and SFU Contemporary Arts at the restored <a href="/wiki/Woodward%27s_Building" title="Woodward's Building">Woodward's Building</a>. The original campus building at Harbour Centre, a rebuilt heritage department store, officially opened on May 5, 1989. Today, the entire campus serves more than 70,000 people annually. Approximately 10,000 are graduate and undergraduate students enrolled in courses and degree programs based downtown.</p> 
     <p>In September 2010, SFU Contemporary Arts relocated to the historic Woodward's district in downtown Vancouver known as the Goldcorp Centre for the Arts. The 130,000-square-foot (12,077&nbsp;m<sup>2</sup>) SFU facility is part of the <a href="/wiki/Woodward%27s" title="Woodward's">Woodward's</a> revitalization project. The new facility accommodates the increasing enrollment of students in the programme and new cultural facilities, including the Fei and Milton Wong Experimental theatre, screening rooms, sound studios, and art galleries.</p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_Life">Student Life</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Student Life">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The student newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Peak_(newspaper)" title="The Peak (newspaper)">The Peak</a></i> was established shortly after the university opened and is circulated throughout the University. <a href="/wiki/CJSF-FM" title="CJSF-FM">CJSF-FM</a> radio is the school's radio station, broadcasting from 90.1 FM to Burnaby and surrounding communities, online at www.cjsf.ca or on cable at 93.9 FM. The Simon Fraser Student Society provides funding for over 100 campus clubs. Various campus events include the annual Terry Fox Run, <a href="/wiki/Gung_Haggis_Fat_Choy" title="Gung Haggis Fat Choy">Gung Haggis Fat Choy</a>, Clubs Week, and other multi-cultural events.</p> 
     <p>The Tau chapter of <a href="/wiki/Phrateres" title="Phrateres">Phrateres</a>, a non-exclusive, non-profit social-service club, was installed here in 1966. Between 1924 and 1967, 23 chapters of Phrateres were installed in universities across North America, including the Theta chapter nearby at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">University of British Columbia</a>.</p>

     <p>The university's varsity sports teams are called the Simon Fraser Clan, and the mascot is a <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Terrier" title="Scottish Terrier">Scottish Terrier</a> named <a href="/w/index.php?title=McFogg_the_Dog&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="McFogg the Dog (page does not exist)">McFogg the Dog</a>. In sports and other competitions, there tends to be a strong rivalry between SFU and <a href="/wiki/The_University_of_British_Columbia" title="The University of British Columbia" class="mw-redirect">The University of British Columbia</a>.</p> 
     <p>The Clan competes in <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Interuniversity_Sport" title="Canadian Interuniversity Sport">Canadian Interuniversity Sport</a> (CIS) and the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">National Collegiate Athletic Association</a> (NCAA) and the <a href="/wiki/Men%27s_Collegiate_Lacrosse_Association" title="Men's Collegiate Lacrosse Association">Men's Collegiate Lacrosse Association</a> (MCLA). In total, SFU has 15 varsity sport teams and 300 athletes. Football, men's and women's basketball, women's volleyball and women's wrestling compete for CIS championships only. Men's and women's soccer, women's softball, men's and women's swimming, men's and women's cross-country and men's and women's track and field compete for NAIA championships only. Men's wrestling competes for championships of both organizations. The lacrosse team plays in the MCLA, and has reached the final four of the national tournament five times. The team is the only collegiate lacrosse team in Canada to compete for a national title. SFU has won the NAIA <a href="/wiki/NACDA_Director%27s_Cup" title="NACDA Director's Cup" class="mw-redirect">NACDA Director's Cup</a> five times, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> On Friday, July 10, 2009, the <a href="/wiki/NCAA" title="NCAA" class="mw-redirect">NCAA</a> announced that it has accepted SFU as a Division II member that will begin after a two year transition period. SFU will compete in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northwest_Athletic_Conference" title="Great Northwest Athletic Conference">Great Northwest Athletic Conference</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup> It is the first Canadian university to be accepted as a member of the <a href="/wiki/NCAA" title="NCAA" class="mw-redirect">NCAA</a> at any level.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Many former Clan athletes later represented Canada during the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a>, including gold medalists <a href="/wiki/Carole_Huynh" title="Carole Huynh" class="mw-redirect">Carole Huynh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Igali" title="Daniel Igali">Daniel Igali</a>, and Olympic medalists <a href="/wiki/Sue_Holloway" title="Sue Holloway">Sue Holloway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Fisher_(canoeist)" title="Hugh Fisher (canoeist)">Hugh Fisher</a>. Other Clan alumni include: <a href="/wiki/Jay_Triano" title="Jay Triano">Jay Triano</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeff_Thue&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jeff Thue (page does not exist)">Jeff Thue</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Molle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bob Molle (page does not exist)">Bob Molle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Rinke" title="Chris Rinke" class="mw-redirect">Chris Rinke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carolyn_Murray" title="Carolyn Murray">Carolyn Murray</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Garry_MacDonald_(athlete)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Garry MacDonald (athlete) (page does not exist)">Garry MacDonald</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bruce_Roberston_(rower)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bruce Roberston (rower) (page does not exist)">Bruce Robertson</a>.</p> 
     <p>There are also teams at Simon Fraser University that compete against other university teams at the varsity level. These sports include rowing, lacrosse (in the <a href="/wiki/Men%27s_Collegiate_Lacrosse_Association" title="Men's Collegiate Lacrosse Association">MCLA</a>), hockey and cheerleading.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance_and_administration">Governance and administration</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Governance and administration">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Convocation">Convocation</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Convocation">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The Convocation is composed of all faculty members, senators, and graduates (degree holders, including honorary alumni) of the university. Its main function is to elect the Chancellor (who acts as Chair of Convocation) and four Convocation Senators. Convocation ceremonies are held twice annually to confer degrees (including honorary degrees) as well as award diplomas and certificates.</p>

     <p><b>Board of Governors</b></p> 
     <p>The Board is composed of the Chancellor, the President, two student members, two faculty members, one staff member, and eight individuals appointed by the <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> government. Traditionally, the Board is chaired by one of the government appointees. The Board is responsible for the general management and governance of the university.</p>

      </dt>

     <p>The Senate is composed of the Chancellor, the President, Vice-President, Academic, Vice-President, Research, Deans of Faculties, Dean of Graduate Studies, Dean of Continuing Studies, Associate Vice-President, Academic, University Librarian, Registrar (as Senate secretary), 14 student members, 28 faculty members, and 4 convocation members (who are not faculty members). The Senate is chaired by the President. The academic governance of the university is vested in the Senate.</p>

     <p><a href="/wiki/Terry_Fox" title="Terry Fox">Terry Fox</a> was a notable alumnus of SFU. Diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Bone_cancer" title="Bone cancer" class="mw-redirect">bone cancer</a> which resulted in the amputation of his leg, the 18-year-old kinesiology major set out to run across <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Marathon_of_Hope" title="Marathon of Hope" class="mw-redirect">Marathon of Hope</a> to raise funding and awareness about cancer. As a result of Terry Fox's legacy, running for charitable causes is now integrated within communities worldwide. He also inspired friend <a href="/wiki/Rick_Hansen" title="Rick Hansen">Rick Hansen</a>'s Man in Motion world tour by wheelchair. In 2001, SFU conferred an honorary degree to <a href="/wiki/Betty_Fox" title="Betty Fox">Betty Fox</a>, mother of Terry Fox and Honorary Chair of the <a href="/wiki/Terry_Fox_Run" title="Terry Fox Run">Terry Fox Foundation</a>.</p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Honorary_alumni">Honorary alumni</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Honorary alumni">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>In 1967, SFU awarded an honorary LL.D. (doctor of laws) to <a href="/wiki/Marshall_McLuhan" title="Marshall McLuhan">Marshall McLuhan</a>, the first honorary degree awarded by the university.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> On April 20, 2004, SFU conferred honorary degrees upon three <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</a> recipients: the <a href="/wiki/Tenzin_Gyatso,_14th_Dalai_Lama" title="Tenzin Gyatso, 14th Dalai Lama" class="mw-redirect">14th Dalai Lama</a>, Bishop <a href="/wiki/Desmond_Tutu" title="Desmond Tutu">Desmond Tutu</a>, and human rights activist <a href="/wiki/Shirin_Ebadi" title="Shirin Ebadi">Shirin Ebadi</a>.      <p>Due to the contemporary <a href="/wiki/Brutalist_architecture" title="Brutalist architecture">Brutalist architecture</a> of the Burnaby Mountain campus, many buildings, including the WAC Bennet Library and Academic Quadrangle have been used for location shots in a variety of films and television programmes over the years.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_film">In film</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Fraser_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: In film">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Its first use as a film set was for the 1972 science fiction film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Groundstar_Conspiracy" title="The Groundstar Conspiracy">The Groundstar Conspiracy</a></i>, in which the entire campus complex was used. It was then followed by <i>The Fly 2</i>, which has scenes shot inside and outside the Burnaby campus. The campus also appeared in the 1989 movie <i><a href="/wiki/American_Boyfriends" title="American Boyfriends">American Boyfriends</a></i>, set in 1965, with the buildings dressed to look like they were still under construction. The campus served as a high-tech corporate setting in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Antitrust_(film)" title="Antitrust (film)">Antitrust</a></i>. Recently, in addition to other <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>-area landmarks, many parts of the Burnaby campus were used for the filming of the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_6th_Day" title="The 6th Day">The 6th Day</a></i> as well as <i><a href="/wiki/Agent_Cody_Banks" title="Agent Cody Banks">Agent Cody Banks</a></i>. The 2007 film <i><a href="/wiki/Personal_Effects" title="Personal Effects">Personal Effects</a></i>, was filmed in the newly-constructed Blusson Hall at the Burnaby Campus. In early 2008, the Burnaby campus was again used for filming, this time for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Day_the_Earth_Stood_Still_(2008_film)" title="The Day the Earth Stood Still (2008 film)">The Day the Earth Stood Still</a></i> (2008 Remake). Filming of the 2012 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Underworld:_Awakening" title="Underworld: Awakening">Underworld: Awakening</a></i> starring Kate Beckinsale, began in early 2011 with parts of the AQ modified as part of the set. The SFU Surrey Campus has also been featured in blockbuster movies such as <i><a href="/wiki/I,_Robot_(film)" title="I, Robot (film)">I, Robot</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four_(film)" title="Fantastic Four (film)">Fantastic Four</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Catwoman_(film)" title="Catwoman (film)">Catwoman</a></i>. SFU was also the film location for <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_4:_Forward_Unto_Dawn" title="Halo 4: Forward Unto Dawn">Halo 4: Forward Unto Dawn</a></i>, representing the Corbulo Academy of Military Science. In <a href="/wiki/Stargate_SG-1" title="Stargate SG-1">Stargate SG-1</a>, SFU was the homeworld for the technologically advanced Tollan, as seen in the Tollan-centric episodes &quot;Pretense&quot; (Season 3 Ep. 15) and &quot;Between Two Fires&quot; (Season 5 Ep. 9).</p>
