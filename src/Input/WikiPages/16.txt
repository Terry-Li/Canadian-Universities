     <p>The <b>University of New Brunswick</b> (<b>UNB</b>) is a non-denominational university located in <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>. It is the oldest English language university in Canada and one of four schools that claim the title of oldest public university in North America (the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Georgia" title="University of Georgia">University of Georgia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Carolina_at_Chapel_Hill" title="University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill">University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_College_of_William_and_Mary" title="The College of William and Mary" class="mw-redirect">The College of William and Mary</a> also <a href="/wiki/Oldest_public_university_in_the_United_States" title="Oldest public university in the United States">claim this title</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-facts_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-facts-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> UNB was founded by a group of seven <a href="/wiki/Loyalist_(American_Revolution)" title="Loyalist (American Revolution)">Loyalists</a> who left the United States after the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Happy_Birthday_to_the_University_of_New_Brunswick_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Happy_Birthday_to_the_University_of_New_Brunswick-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>UNB has two main campuses: the original campus, founded in 1785 in <a href="/wiki/Fredericton" title="Fredericton">Fredericton</a>, and a smaller campus which opened in <a href="/wiki/Saint_John,_New_Brunswick" title="Saint John, New Brunswick">Saint John</a> in 1964. In addition, there are two small satellite health sciences campuses located in <a href="/wiki/Moncton" title="Moncton">Moncton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bathurst,_New_Brunswick" title="Bathurst, New Brunswick">Bathurst, New Brunswick</a>, and two offices in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. UNB offers over 75 degrees in fourteen faculties at the <a href="/wiki/Undergraduate" title="Undergraduate" class="mw-redirect">undergraduate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Graduate_student" title="Graduate student" class="mw-redirect">graduate</a> levels with a total student enrollment of approximately 11,400 between the two principal campuses.<sup id="cite_ref-UNB_Quick_Facts_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNB_Quick_Facts-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> In the fall of 2010, UNB partnered with <a href="/wiki/Dalhousie_University" title="Dalhousie University">Dalhousie University</a> and the government of New Brunswick to open the first English-language medical school in the province at the Saint John campus.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding_and_charters">Founding and charters</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Founding and charters">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>In 1783, <a href="/wiki/Loyalist_(American_Revolution)" title="Loyalist (American Revolution)">Loyalist</a> settlers began to build upon the ruins of a former <a href="/wiki/Acadian" title="Acadian" class="mw-redirect">Acadian</a> village called Ste-Anne-des-Pays-Bas. The new settlement was named Frederick's Town in honour of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Frederick,_Duke_of_York_and_Albany" title="Prince Frederick, Duke of York and Albany">Prince Frederick</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/George_III_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George III of the United Kingdom">King George III</a> and uncle of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fredericton_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fredericton-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Initially modeled on the <a href="/wiki/Anglican" title="Anglican" class="mw-redirect">Anglican</a> ideals of older, European institutions, the University of New Brunswick was founded in 1785 as the Academy of Liberal Arts and Sciences.<sup id="cite_ref-University_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-University-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The petition requesting the establishment of the school, titled &quot;The Founders' Petition of 1785,&quot; was addressed to Governor <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Carleton" title="Thomas Carleton">Thomas Carleton</a> and was signed by seven <a href="/wiki/Loyalist_(American_Revolution)" title="Loyalist (American Revolution)">Loyalist</a> men: <a href="/wiki/William_Paine_(physician)" title="William Paine (physician)">William Paine</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Wanton" title="William Wanton">William Wanton</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Sproule&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="George Sproule (page does not exist)">George Sproule</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zephaniah_Kingsley" title="Zephaniah Kingsley">Zephaniah Kingsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Coffin_(judge)" title="John Coffin (judge)">John Coffin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ward_Chipman" title="Ward Chipman">Ward Chipman</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adino_Paddock&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Adino Paddock (page does not exist)">Adino Paddock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Founders.27_Petition_of_1785_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Founders.27_Petition_of_1785-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <blockquote class="toccolours" style="float:none; padding: 10px 15px 10px 15px; display:table;"> 
      <p>To his Excellency Thomas Carleton Esquire Governor Captain General, and Commander in Chief, of the Province of New Brunswick, and the territories thereunto belonging, Vice Admiral Chancellor &amp;c &amp;c &amp;c: —</p> 
      <dl> 
       <dd>
        Your memorialists whose names are hereunto subscribed, beg leave to represent, and state to your consideration the Necessity and expediency of an early attention to the Establishment in this Infant Province of an Academy, or School of liberal Arts and Sciences.
       </dd> 
      </dl> 
      <dl> 
       <dd>
        Your Excellency need not be reminded of the many Peculiarities attending the Settlement of this Country The Settlement of other Provinces has generally originated in the voluntary Exertions of a few enterprising Individuals, unincumbered, and prosecuting their Labor at their Leisure, and as they found it convenient, and most for their Advantage – Far different is the Situation in which the loyal Adventurers here find themselves – Many of them upon removing had Sons, whose Time of life, and former Hopes, call for an immediate attention to their Education – Many publick advantages, and many Conveniences would result to Individuals could this be affected within this Province, the Particulars of which it is unnecessary to ennumerate – Your Memorialists do therefore most earnestly request your Excellency will be pleased to grant a Charter for the establishing, and founding such an Academy . . .
        <sup id="cite_ref-The_Founders.27_Petition_of_1785_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Founders.27_Petition_of_1785-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup>

     <p>By an 1800 provincial charter, signed by <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Odell" title="Jonathan Odell">Jonathan Odell</a>, the Academy of Liberal Arts and Sciences became the College of New Brunswick.<sup id="cite_ref-Historical_Sketch_of_UNB_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Historical_Sketch_of_UNB-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> The College was succeeded by King's College, which was granted by royal charter in December 1827. King's College operated under the control of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> until 1859, when it was made non-sectarian by an act of the provincial legislature that transformed the College into the University of New Brunswick.<sup id="cite_ref-UNB.27s_Heraldic_Tapestries_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNB.27s_Heraldic_Tapestries-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1866, Mary Kingsley Tibbits became the first regularly admitted female student of UNB.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th.2F21st_centuries">20th/21st centuries</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 20th/21st centuries">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>In 1906, UNB established a bicameral system of university government consisting of a senate responsible for academic policy, and a board of governors exercising exclusive control over financial policy and other matters. The president, appointed by the board, was to provide a link between the two bodies and to provide institutional leadership. By 1967, the University of New Brunswick had two faculties: Arts and Applied Science. It awarded the degrees of <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Bachelor of Science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts_(postgraduate)" title="Master of Arts (postgraduate)" class="mw-redirect">Master of Arts</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Science" title="Doctor of Science">Doctor of Science</a>. The latter was awarded only in the fields of <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineering" title="Civil engineering">civil engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a>. At this time, the university had 156 male students, 21 female students, and only eleven academic staff, who were all male.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In the 1960s, University policies changed in response to social pressure and the belief that higher education was a key to social justice and economic productivity for individuals and for society. In 1964, a second, smaller campus was established in <a href="/wiki/Saint_John,_New_Brunswick" title="Saint John, New Brunswick">Saint John, New Brunswick</a>. The growth of the UNBSJ campus is particularly notable, for the campus began with only 96 students spread throughout various buildings in Saint John's central business district. In 1968, UNBSJ moved to its new home at Tucker Park.</p> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Association_of_University_of_New_Brunswick_Teachers" title="Association of University of New Brunswick Teachers">Association of University of New Brunswick Teachers</a> (AUNBT) was established in 1954; in 1979, this association became the bargaining agent for all full-time academic staff, and in 2008, it achieved certification for contract academic staff.</p> 
     <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Relocation_of_the_Faculty_of_Law">Relocation of the Faculty of Law</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Relocation of the Faculty of Law">edit</a>]</span></h4> 
     <div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article: 
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_New_Brunswick_Faculty_of_Law" title="University of New Brunswick Faculty of Law">University of New Brunswick Faculty of Law</a>
     </div> 
     <p>In 1959, the Faculty of Law moved from Saint John to Fredericton following a report on the status of legal education in Canada by Professor Maxwell Cohen from <a href="/wiki/McGill_University" title="McGill University">McGill University</a>. In his report, Cohen stated that the Saint John Law School was only &quot;nominally a faculty of UNB&quot;. This prompted <a href="/wiki/Max_Aitken,_Lord_Beaverbrook" title="Max Aitken, Lord Beaverbrook">Lord Beaverbrook</a>, as Chancellor, and UNB President <a href="/wiki/Colin_B._Mackay" title="Colin B. Mackay">Colin B. Mackay</a>, to permanently move the Saint John Law School to the UNB Fredericton campus, despite the Dean's objections.</p> 
     <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Polytechnic_controversy">Polytechnic controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Polytechnic controversy">edit</a>]</span></h4> 
     <p>In the fall of 2007, a report commissioned by the provincial government recommended that UNBSJ and the <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick_Community_College" title="New Brunswick Community College">New Brunswick Community College</a> be reformed and consolidated into a new <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_technology" title="Institute of technology">polytechnic</a> post-secondary institute. The proposal immediately came under heavy criticism and led to the organization of several protests. Under heavy fire from the public, the <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Graham" title="Shawn Graham">Graham government</a> eventually announced that it would set aside the possibility of UNB Saint John losing its status as a university and would refer the report to a working group for further study.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> The government would go on to announce in January that UNBSJ would retain its liberal arts program and its association with UNB<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> and the working group reported back to government in May, with its findings and government's response being made public in June.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campuses">Campuses</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Campuses">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>Currently UNBF has approximately 9,000 students while UNBSJ has 3,000, although UNBSJ is growing at a faster rate. Both campuses have undergone significant expansion over the years, and many University buildings have received funding from Lord Beaverbrook and other prominent industrialists and philanthropists. UNB's largest expansion coincided with the <a href="/wiki/Post%E2%80%93World_War_II_baby_boom" title="Post–World War II baby boom">baby boom</a>, when its Fredericton campus tripled in size.</p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fredericton">Fredericton</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Fredericton">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>UNB Fredericton is located on the banks of the <a href="/wiki/Saint_John_River_(Bay_of_Fundy)" title="Saint John River (Bay of Fundy)">St. John River</a>. The campus is well known for its colourful fall foliage, Georgian style red-brick buildings, and a very steep hill. UNB Fredericton has shared the &quot;College Hill&quot; with <a href="/wiki/St._Thomas_University_(New_Brunswick)" title="St. Thomas University (New Brunswick)">St. Thomas University</a> (STU) since 1964, when the former St. Thomas College moved from Chatham, NB (now <a href="/wiki/Miramichi,_New_Brunswick" title="Miramichi, New Brunswick">Miramichi</a>). While the universities share some infrastructure, they remain separate institutions.</p> 
     <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a>]</span></h4> 
     <p>Architect <a href="/wiki/G._Ernest_Fairweather" title="G. Ernest Fairweather">G. Ernest Fairweather</a> designed several of the campus buildings, including the Old Civil Engineering Building (1900) and the Gymnasium (1906).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> In addition, several of the stained glass windows in the Convocation Hall were created by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_McCausland_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Robert McCausland Limited (page does not exist)">Robert McCausland Limited</a>.</p> 
     <p>UNBF's War Memorial Hall (usually referred to as Memorial Hall), originally built as a science building in 1924, honours the 35 UNB Alumni who died in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.</p> 
     <p>UNBF's Brigadier Milton F. Gregg, V.C., Centre for the Study of War and Society (usually referred to as The Gregg Centre) was created in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Historic_Sites">National Historic Sites</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: National Historic Sites">edit</a>]</span></h4> 
     <p>The Fredericton campus features two sites of national historic importance, both designated as <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Sites_of_Canada" title="National Historic Sites of Canada">National Historic Sites of Canada</a>: the 1827 Sir Howard Douglas Hall (Arts Building)<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> and the 1851 William Brydone Jack Observatory.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saint_John">Saint John</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Saint John">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The UNB Saint John campus (UNBSJ) is located in Tucker Park in the <a href="/wiki/Millidgeville,_New_Brunswick" title="Millidgeville, New Brunswick">Millidgeville</a> neighbourhood, several kilometres north of the city's central business district, and offers spectacular views of the Kennebecasis River and Grand Bay. New Brunswick's largest health care facility, <a href="/wiki/Saint_John_Regional_Hospital" title="Saint John Regional Hospital">Saint John Regional Hospital</a>, is located adjacent to the UNBSJ campus. In 2010, a new medical school, a joint project between <a href="/wiki/Dalhousie_University" title="Dalhousie University">Dalhousie University</a>, UNBSJ, and the Regional Hospital, took in its first class.</p> 
     <p>The Saint John campus has undergone expansion over the years and is the fastest growing component of the UNB system with many new buildings constructed between the 1970s and the first decade of the 21st century. A trend in recent years has been a growth in the number of international students.</p> 
     <p>Notable differences from its parent campus in Fredericton lay in the campus culture. While UNB Fredericton has a substantial amount of students living in its on-campus residences, this is not the case for UNBSJ. The majority of students do not live within walking distance of the campus due to its remote location, so unlike Fredericton, Saint John is predominantly a &quot;commuter campus&quot;.</p>

     <p>On April 1, 2010, construction began on the Hans W. Klohn Commons, one of the most environmentally friendly buildings in Atlantic Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-A_smarter.2C_greener_library_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-A_smarter.2C_greener_library-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> The building features an electric elevator that produces power for the commons. The building is part of the Tucker Park enhancement project, which will include the refurbishment of the Canada Games Stadium, the new Dalhousie Medicine New Brunswick facility, and the New Brunswick Community College’s Allied Health building.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainability">Sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sustainability">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The University of New Brunswick has attempted to reduce its environmental impact through installing a natural gas burning microturbine at the Central Heating Plant that produces 100&nbsp;kW of electricity for the university.<sup id="cite_ref-Campus_Sustainability_Projects_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campus_Sustainability_Projects-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> Heat energy, a result of this process, is used to contribute to the overall heating of the campus to increase its overall energy efficiency. In addition, all produce and dairy products used within its dining services are obtained from local farmers and local producers; the campus offers grass-fed beef, fair trade coffee, and dining locations offer biodegradable to-go containers. UNB recycles electronics in addition to traditional materials and operates a move-out materials collection program. The university adheres to a green purchasing policy and has reduced greenhouse gas emissions 26 percent since 1990; they have also decreased water consumption since 2005 through the adoption of new technologies. With these efforts to create a more sustainable campus, the University of New Brunswick received an overall &quot;B+&quot; grade in the 2011 College Sustainability Report Card put out by the Sustainable Endowment’s Institute.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_and_academics">Research and academics</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Research and academics">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>UNB is the seat of 16 Canada Research Chairs<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> and is home to more than 60 research centers and institutes. It conducts about seventy five percent of all university research in the province. Between 2004 and 2009, the university's research revenue increased by 77 per cent: the highest increase among Canadian comprehensive universities.<sup id="cite_ref-UNB_Research_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNB_Research-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reputation">Reputation</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Reputation">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>In 2013, <i><a href="/wiki/Maclean%27s" title="Maclean's">Maclean's</a></i> magazine ranked UNB 4th nationally in its evaluation of the top fifteen comprehensive universities in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> For several years running, UNB's libraries have been ranked in the top spot nationally in terms of the percentage of the overall budget devoted to libraries and in terms of holdings per student.</p> 
     <p>In 2008, the <i><a href="/wiki/National_Post" title="National Post">National Post</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Ottawa_Citizen" title="Ottawa Citizen">Ottawa Citizen</a></i> recognized UNB as being among the top three comprehensive research universities in Canada for the highest percentage growth of research income across a five-year period.<sup id="cite_ref-UNB_Research_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNB_Research-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 2012, UNB's law school was ranked 2nd nationally in elite firm hiring by <i>Maclean's</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> According to Canadian Lawyer Magazine, the law school ranks among the top five in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Poets.27_Corner">Poets' Corner</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Poets' Corner">edit</a>]</span></h4> 
     <p>Because so many of UNB's students, alumni, and professors have produced celebrated poetry, the city of Fredericton has earned the nickname &quot;Poets' Corner.&quot; Two of Canada's four <a href="/wiki/Confederation_Poets" title="Confederation Poets">Confederation Poets</a> - <a href="/wiki/Charles_G._D._Roberts" title="Charles G. D. Roberts">Sir Charles G.D. Roberts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bliss_Carman" title="Bliss Carman">Bliss Carman</a> - were educated at UNB, as was <a href="/wiki/Francis_Joseph_Sherman" title="Francis Joseph Sherman">Francis Joseph Sherman</a>, along with a number of notable 20th and 21st century Canadian writers. In 1947, the Historic Sites and Monuments Board of Canada unveiled a &quot;Poet's Corner&quot; monument in honour of Carman, Roberts, and Sherman.<sup id="cite_ref-Poets.27_Corner_of_Canada_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Poets.27_Corner_of_Canada-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Poets.27_Corner_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Poets.27_Corner-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Institute_of_Biomedical_Engineering">Institute of Biomedical Engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Institute of Biomedical Engineering">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The Institute of Biomedical Engineering (IBME) on the Fredericton campus is one of the leading research institutes in biomedical engineering in Canada. It was founded in 1965 as the Bio-Engineering Institute, making it one of the oldest research institutes to be solely dedicated to the field of biomedical engineering. The institute is also the region's prosthetic fitting centre where amputees are fitted with state-of-the-art intelligent artificial limbs. The institute also carries out research in the field of myoelectric signal processing, biomedical instrumentation and human <a href="/wiki/Motion_analysis" title="Motion analysis">motion analysis</a>. The IBME also developed the UNB Test of Prosthetic Function which is used by researchers all over the world. Although the institute does not offer degrees in biomedical engineering, students at UNB usually enroll in one of the other faculties of engineering such as electrical or mechanical and pursue their research in biomedical engineering at the IBME.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canadian_Rivers_Institute">Canadian Rivers Institute</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Canadian Rivers Institute">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article: 
      <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Rivers_Institute" title="Canadian Rivers Institute">Canadian Rivers Institute</a>
     </div> 
     <p>The Canadian Rivers Institute was founded in 2000 and is a leading site of <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> sciences research. The mandate of the CRI is to conduct both multi-disciplinary basic and applied research focusing on rivers from their <a href="/wiki/Headwaters" title="Headwaters" class="mw-redirect">headwaters</a> to their <a href="/wiki/Estuaries" title="Estuaries" class="mw-redirect">estuaries</a>, to promote the conservation, protection and sustainable use of water, and to educate professionals, graduate students and the public on water sciences. Members of the CRI conduct research on regional, national and international issues related to rivers and their land-water linkages.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mi.27kmaq-Maliseet_Institute">Mi'kmaq-Maliseet Institute</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Mi'kmaq-Maliseet Institute">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>UNB created its BEd program for <a href="/wiki/First_Nations" title="First Nations">First Nations</a> students in 1977 in an effort to help First Nations communities take control of their own schools. In 1981, the Mi'kmaq-Maliseet Institute opened its doors with an expanded mandate to train professionals and improve First Nations access to First Nations education. The Institute provides a variety of services, including research, curriculum development, language education, policy development, children's literacy, and more. In addition, the Institute funds the Mi'kmaq-Maliseet Resource Collection, which contains materials that are immensely valuable to knowledge of First Nations culture, history, and perspective in the region.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scholarships">Scholarships</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Scholarships">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>UNB award over five million dollars in scholarships each year.<sup id="cite_ref-UNB_Quick_Facts_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNB_Quick_Facts-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The most prestigious of these are the <a href="/wiki/Currie_Scholarship" title="Currie Scholarship">Blake-Kirkpatrick</a>, Beaverbrook, and President's scholarships. UNB has a scholarship guarantee in which any admitted student with an average of 80% or higher will receive a guaranteed amount of five hundred dollars.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_facts_and_milestones">Notable facts and milestones</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_New_Brunswick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Notable facts and milestones">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <ul> 
      <li>UNB was the only institution to grant both President <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> and his brother <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert F. Kennedy</a> Honorary Doctorates in their lifetimes.</li> 
      <li>UNB owns 7,700 acres (3,100&nbsp;ha) of real property in several sites, including an extensive wood lot adjacent to the Maritime Forestry Complex, formerly used for forestry education and research. Until 1968, the university had the power to expropriate land &quot;it may deem necessary for the purpose of the University&quot; without approval from the government.</li> 
      <li>The Faculty of Computer Science in Fredericton was the first computer science faculty in Canada, and it went on to develop the first software engineering degree program in <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a>.</li> 
      <li>UNB Saint John was the first university in Canada to offer an <a href="/wiki/E-business" title="E-business" class="mw-redirect">e-business</a> program with its bachelor of business administration in electronic commerce. The university has since been ranked by <i>Canadian Business Magazine</i> as first in e-business.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></li> 
      <li>UNB offered Canada's first university-level <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> program, established in 1855. The first engineers graduated in 1857.</li> 
      <li>The university has the longest running undergraduate <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a> program in Canada, and its <a href="/wiki/Forest_engineering" title="Forest engineering" class="mw-redirect">forest engineering</a> program is the only accredited program of its kind in Canada.</li> 
      <li>UNB's Planetary Space Science Centre is the only <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>-supported planetary imaging facility in Canada.</li> 
      <li>UNB’s Centre for Coastal Studies and Aquaculture does work in Canada, the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>.</li> 
      <li>UNB’s Gregg Centre for the Study of War and Society is one of the few research institutes in Canada and the U.S. studying the role of <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> in current and future conflicts.</li> 
     </ul>
