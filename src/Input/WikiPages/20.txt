     <p><b>York University</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Universit&eacute; York</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public</a> <a href="/wiki/Research_university" title="Research university" class="mw-redirect">research university</a> in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. It is Ontario's second-largest <a href="/wiki/Graduate_school" title="Graduate school">graduate school</a> and Canada's third-largest university.</p> 
     <p>York University has a student population of approximately 55,000, 7,000 faculty and staff, and 250,000 alumni worldwide. It has eleven faculties, namely the <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Liberal_Arts_%26_Professional_Studies" title="Faculty of Liberal Arts &amp; Professional Studies">Faculty of Liberal Arts &amp; Professional Studies</a>, Faculty of Science, <a href="/wiki/Schulich_School_of_Business" title="Schulich School of Business">Schulich School of Business</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osgoode_Hall_Law_School" title="Osgoode Hall Law School">Osgoode Hall Law School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glendon_College" title="Glendon College">Glendon College</a>, Faculty of Education, the Faculty of Fine Arts, Faculty of Health, <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Environmental_Studies" title="Faculty of Environmental Studies" class="mw-redirect">Faculty of Environmental Studies</a>, Lassonde School of Engineering, <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Graduate_Studies" title="Faculty of Graduate Studies">Faculty of Graduate Studies</a>, and 28 research centres.</p> 
     <p>York University is one of the top ten research universities in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It participates in the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Space_Program" title="Canadian Space Program" class="mw-redirect">Canadian Space Program</a>. The Faculties of Science and Engineering are Canada's primary research facility into Martian exploration, and have designed several <a href="/wiki/Space_research" title="Space research">space research</a> instruments and applications currently used by <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> York has pioneered some of the first <a href="/wiki/PhD" title="PhD" class="mw-redirect">PhD</a> programs in Canada, in various fields including <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_studies" title="Women's studies">women's studies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The school of <a href="/wiki/Social_work" title="Social work">social work</a> is recognized as having one of the most socially responsive programs in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> York University's <a href="/wiki/Schulich_School_of_Business" title="Schulich School of Business">business school</a> and <a href="/wiki/Osgoode_Hall_Law_School" title="Osgoode Hall Law School">law school</a> have continuously been ranked among the top schools in Canada and the world.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:YorkFaculty1961.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/YorkFaculty1961.jpg/220px-YorkFaculty1961.jpg" width="220" height="178" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/YorkFaculty1961.jpg/330px-YorkFaculty1961.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/YorkFaculty1961.jpg/440px-YorkFaculty1961.jpg 2x" /></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:YorkFaculty1961.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf9/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a>
        </div> York University Faculty Members, 1961
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>York University was established in 1959 as a non-denominational institution by the <i>York University Act</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> which received <a href="/wiki/Royal_Assent" title="Royal Assent">Royal Assent</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Ontario" title="Legislative Assembly of Ontario">Legislative Assembly of Ontario</a> on 26 March of that year.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Its first class was held in September 1960<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> in Falconer Hall on the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a> campus with a total of 76 students.</p> 
     <p>The policy of university education initiated in the 1960s responded to population pressure and the belief that higher education was a key to social justice and economic productivity for individuals and for society.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The governance was modelled on the provincial University of Toronto Act of 1906, which established a bicameral system of university government consisting of a senate (faculty), responsible for academic policy, and a board of governors (citizens) exercising exclusive control over financial policy and having formal authority in all other matters. The president, appointed by the board, was to provide a link between the two bodies and to perform institutional leadership.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In the fall of 1961, York moved to its first campus, <a href="/wiki/Glendon_College" title="Glendon College">Glendon College</a>, and began to emphasize liberal arts and part-time adult education.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> It became independent in 1965, after an initial period of affiliation with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a> (U of T), under the <i>York University Act</i>, 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Its main campus on the northern outskirts of Toronto opened in 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Murray Ross, who continues to be honoured today at the University in several ways ? including the <a href="/wiki/Murray_G._Ross_Award" title="Murray G. Ross Award" class="mw-redirect">Murray G. Ross Award</a>, was still vice-president of U of T when he was approached to become York University's new president.<sup id="cite_ref-Pound2005_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pound2005-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> At the time, York University was envisaged as a feeder campus to U of T, until Ross's powerful vision led it to become a completely separate institution.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 1965, the university opened a second campus, the <a href="/wiki/Keele_Campus_(York_University)" title="Keele Campus (York University)">Keele Campus</a>, in <a href="/wiki/North_York" title="North York">North York</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Glendon_College" title="Glendon College">Glendon</a> campus became a <a href="/wiki/Bilingualism_in_Canada" title="Bilingualism in Canada" class="mw-redirect">bilingual</a> <a href="/wiki/Liberal_arts_college" title="Liberal arts college">liberal arts college</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Escott_Reid" title="Escott Reid">Escott Reid</a>, who envisaged it as a national institution to educate Canada's future leaders, a vision shared by Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Lester_Pearson" title="Lester Pearson" class="mw-redirect">Lester Pearson</a>, who formally opened Glendon College in 1966.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The first Canadian undergraduate program in dance opened at York University in 1970.<sup id="cite_ref-Pound2005_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pound2005-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1972, <a href="/wiki/Canada_Post" title="Canada Post">Canada Post</a> featured the nascent institution on 8&cent; stamps, entitled <i>York University Campus, North York, Ont.</i><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> The first Canadian PhD. program in Women's Studies opened with five candidates in January 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-Pound2005_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pound2005-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Its bilingual mandate and focus on the liberal arts continue to shape Glendon's special status within York University.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> The new <a href="/wiki/Keele_Campus_(York_University)" title="Keele Campus (York University)">Keele Campus</a> was regarded as somewhat isolated, in a generally industrialized part of the city. Petrol storage facilities are still located across the street. Some of the early architecture was unpopular with many, not only for the <a href="/wiki/Brutalism" title="Brutalism" class="mw-redirect">brutalist</a> designs, but the vast expanses between buildings, which was not viewed as suitable for the climate. In the last two decades, the campus has been intensified with new buildings, including a dedicated student centre and new fine arts, computer science and business administration buildings, a small shopping mall, and a <a href="/wiki/Beatrice_Ice_Gardens" title="Beatrice Ice Gardens" class="mw-redirect">hockey arena</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Rexall_Centre" title="Rexall Centre">Rexall Centre</a> tennis stadium, built in 2004, is a perennial host of the <a href="/wiki/Canada_Masters" title="Canada Masters" class="mw-redirect">Canada Masters</a> tennis tournament. As Toronto has spread further out, York has found itself in a relatively central location within the built-up <a href="/wiki/Greater_Toronto_Area" title="Greater Toronto Area">Greater Toronto Area</a> (GTA), and in particular, near the <a href="/wiki/Jane_and_Finch" title="Jane and Finch">Jane and Finch</a> neighbourhood. Its master plan envisages a denser on-campus environment commensurate with that location. Students occupied the university's administration offices in March 1997, protesting escalating tuition hikes.<sup id="cite_ref-Pound2005_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pound2005-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>On November 6, 2008, the York University Senate suspended classes because of a strike by <a href="/wiki/CUPE" title="CUPE" class="mw-redirect">CUPE</a> Local 3903. The local represents contract professors, teaching assistants, and graduate assistants. Classes resumed on Monday, February 2, 2009 after back-to-work legislation was passed by the Ontario Legislative Assembly<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> (see: <i><a href="/wiki/2008-09_York_University_Strike" title="2008-09 York University Strike" class="mw-redirect">2008-09 York University Strike</a></i>)</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academics">Academics</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Academics">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>York has educated some of the current and past directors and <a href="/wiki/CEO" title="CEO" class="mw-redirect">CEOs</a> of Canada's major banks (<a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Nova_Scotia" title="Bank of Nova Scotia" class="mw-redirect">Bank of Nova Scotia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Montreal" title="Bank of Montreal">Bank of Montreal</a>), the largest and most prominent media networks in Canada (<a href="/wiki/CTV_Television_Network" title="CTV Television Network">CTV Television Network</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rogers_Communications" title="Rogers Communications">Rogers Communications</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Canadian Broadcasting Corporation">Canadian Broadcasting Corporation</a>), and numerous judges, <a href="/wiki/Diplomats" title="Diplomats" class="mw-redirect">diplomats</a>, and senior politicians including a former <a href="/wiki/Chief_Justice" title="Chief Justice">Chief Justice</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Court_of_Appeal" title="Federal Court of Appeal" class="mw-redirect">Federal Court of Appeal</a> of Canada, the <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Finance_(Canada)" title="Minister of Finance (Canada)">Minister of Finance</a> of Canada and the former <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Ambassador_to_the_United_Nations" title="Canadian Ambassador to the United Nations" class="mw-redirect">Canadian Ambassador to the United Nations</a>. Astronaut <a href="/wiki/Steven_MacLean_(astronaut)" title="Steven MacLean (astronaut)" class="mw-redirect">Steve MacLean</a> was educated at York University in the <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a> department and later taught there before going to work at <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>.</p>

     <p>York's approximately 2,450 full-time faculty and academic librarians are represented by the York University Faculty Association.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> Contract faculty, teaching assistants, and graduate assistants are represented by CUPE Local 3903.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Admissions">Admissions</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Admissions">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>For the 2011-2012 academic year, York received 41,527 undergraduate applications; approximately 11,000 students accepted admission offers and were enrolled, maintaining a high school entrance average of 81.6% (A-).<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>The 2011?2012 <a href="/wiki/Times_Higher_Education_World_University_Rankings" title="Times Higher Education World University Rankings">Times Higher Education World University Rankings</a> placed York 276-300 in the world, placing 16-17 in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-USUnivRankings_THES_W_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USUnivRankings_THES_W-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> The 2011 <a href="/wiki/QS_World_University_Rankings" title="QS World University Rankings">QS World University Rankings</a> ranked the university 364th in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> According to the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Academic_Ranking_of_World_Universities" title="Academic Ranking of World Universities">Academic Ranking of World Universities</a> (ARWU) rankings, the university ranked 401-500th in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-USUnivRankings_ARWU_W_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USUnivRankings_ARWU_W-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> In terms of national rankings, <i><a href="/wiki/Maclean%27s" title="Maclean's">Maclean's</a></i> ranked York 8th in their 2013 comprehensive university rankings.<sup id="cite_ref-Macdoc_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Macdoc-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Rankings_of_universities_in_Canada#Higher_Education_Strategy_Associates.27_rankings" title="Rankings of universities in Canada">Higher Education Strategy Associates</a> ranked York 8th nationally in Social Sciences and Humanities. <sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Several of York's programs had also gained notable recognition both nationally and internationally. In the <a href="/wiki/Corporate_Knights" title="Corporate Knights">Corporate Knights</a> 2011 ranking of teacher-education programs in Canada, York had placed fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> York's Osgoode Hall Law School ranked second in Canada, in Maclean's 2012 ranking of Canadian <a href="/wiki/Common_law" title="Common law">common law</a> schools.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> In the Corporate Knights ranking of law programs in Canada, Osgoode Hall had also placed second.<sup id="cite_ref-CorpL_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CorpL-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> In the QS ranking of law programs, York had placed 42nd in the world, and fifth in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The Schulich School of Business had also gained national and international accolades. In the Corporate Knights 2011 rankings of MBA programs in Canada, the school had placed first.<sup id="cite_ref-CorpL_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CorpL-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> In the same year, the Corporate Knights had also ranked the school third in Canada for undergraduate business programs. In <a href="/wiki/Beyond_Grey_Pinstripes" title="Beyond Grey Pinstripes">Beyond Grey Pinstripes</a> 2011 rankings of MBA programs, York had placed second in the world, and first in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Businessweek" title="Bloomberg Businessweek">Bloomberg Businessweek</a>'s 2010 rankings of the best business schools outside the United States, Schulich had ranked seventh, the second highest of any Canadian business school.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> 2010 rankings of the best two-year MBA program outside the United States, Schulich had ranked 10th in the world, the highest out of any Canadian business school.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/CNN_Expansion" title="CNN Expansion" class="mw-redirect">CNN Expansion</a>'s ranking of MBA programs, the school had ranked 18th in the world, placing first in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a> 2011 ranking of MBA programs, Schulich had ranked 49th in the world, and third in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a>'s 2011 full-time MBA rankings, Schulich had ranked ninth in the world, and first in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Faculties">Faculties</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Faculties">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>York University has eleven faculties, including the Faculties of Liberal Arts and Professional Studies (which was formed in July 2009 by the merger of the Atkinson Faculty of Liberal and Professional Studies and the Faculty of Arts), <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">Science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineering</a>, Education, and <a href="/wiki/Glendon_College" title="Glendon College">Glendon College</a>. Some faculties' programs overlap: for instance, more than one house separate mathematics departments, although some of these are being merged; the <a href="/wiki/Schulich_School_of_Business" title="Schulich School of Business">Schulich School of Business</a> offers undergraduate and graduate International Business Administration programmes and the Faculty of Liberal Arts &amp; Professional Studies offers a Bachelor of Administrative Studies with streams in Accounting, Business Research, Finance, Human Resources Management, Management, Management Science, and Marketing, as well as a minor in Business, to be taken in conjunction with a major in another discipline. The Schulich School of Business offers undergraduates with the option of pursuing a BBA or iBBA program with a component of mandatory exchange. Also, the Glendon and Schulich units are offering or are in the processes of preparing to offer degrees in public policy and administration. The University administration has, however, taken steps in some cases to unify departments in separate faculties, in part to support York's efforts to brand itself as a university focused on interdisciplinarity. For example, the Faculty of Health, opened on 1 July 2006, houses the School of Health Policy and Management, School of <a href="/wiki/Kinesiology" title="Kinesiology">Kinesiology</a> and Health Science, School of Nursing, and the Department of <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">Psychology</a>.</p> 
     <p>York University offers the first and largest <a href="/wiki/Graphic_design" title="Graphic design">graphic design</a> program in Ontario <a href="/wiki/York/Sheridan_Design_(YSDN)" title="York/Sheridan Design (YSDN)" class="mw-redirect">York/Sheridan Design (YSDN)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-design.yorku.ca_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-design.yorku.ca-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> It is a four-year University degree delivered jointly by the two leading educational institutions of design in Canada (York University and <a href="/wiki/Sheridan_College" title="Sheridan College">Sheridan College</a>), and is recognized throughout North America for maintaining the highest academic and professional standards.</p> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Osgoode_Hall_Law_School" title="Osgoode Hall Law School">Osgoode Hall Law School</a> moved from a downtown location to the York campus in 1969, following the requirement that every law school affiliate with a university. The law school has several flexible degrees available, including the Osgoode-NYU JD/LLB degree in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/New_York_University_School_of_Law" title="New York University School of Law">New York University School of Law</a>. Osgoode Hall Law School of York University has been ranked the top law school in Canada in <i>Canadian Lawyer</i> magazine?s 2008 Law School Survey.</p> 
     <p>York University's Faculty of <a href="/wiki/Graduate_Studies" title="Graduate Studies" class="mw-redirect">Graduate Studies</a> offers graduate degrees in a variety of disciplines, and there are several joint graduate programmes with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryerson_University" title="Ryerson University">Ryerson University</a>. It is the second largest graduate school in the Province of Ontario.</p> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Ph.D." title="Ph.D." class="mw-redirect">Ph.D.</a> students in the Social and Political Thought<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> program have won the award for best PhD thesis in Canada. The School of Women's Studies at York University<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> offers a large array of courses in the field, some of which are offered in French. The Canadian Centre for Germanic and European Studies<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> is co-housed at York University and Universit&eacute; de Montr&eacute;al. The Centre is funded by the <a href="/wiki/German_Academic_Exchange_Service" title="German Academic Exchange Service">German Academic Exchange Service</a>.</p>

     <p>The Art Gallery of York University houses the permanent art collections.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup> The collection of 1500 objects includes Canadian, American, Inuit, and European mixed media, multimedia, installations, painting, photography, prints, drawings, sculpture, sketchbooks, film and video. <sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The Faculty of Fine Arts<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup> offers programmes such as design, <a href="/wiki/Ethnomusicology" title="Ethnomusicology">ethnomusicology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cultural_studies" title="Cultural studies">cultural studies</a>, visual arts, music, dance, and theatre. York's <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">Jazz</a> Department was once overseen by <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Peterson" title="Oscar Peterson">Oscar Peterson</a>. York also has a joint Bachelor of Design program with <a href="/wiki/Sheridan_College" title="Sheridan College">Sheridan College</a>. York's Departments of Film, Theatre and Creative Writing (which is not officially affiliated with the Faculty of Fine Arts) offers programmes in <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> production/directing, acting, and writing respectively, producing many award-winning graduates. The founders of Toronto's <a href="/wiki/Hot_Docs" title="Hot Docs" class="mw-redirect">Hot Docs</a> International Documentary Film Festival and <a href="/wiki/CineACTION" title="CineACTION" class="mw-redirect">CineACTION</a> film theory magazine were graduates of York's Faculty of Fine Arts.</p> 
     <p>York's Dance department was founded by <a href="/wiki/National_Ballet_of_Canada" title="National Ballet of Canada">National Ballet of Canada</a>'s first choreographer <a href="/wiki/Grant_Strate" title="Grant Strate">Grant Strate</a>.</p> 
     <p>York offers a Space and Communication Sciences undergraduate degree. York?s Centre for Vision Research has developed a ?<a href="/wiki/Virtual_reality" title="Virtual reality">virtual reality</a> room? called IVY (Immersive Virtual Environment at York) in order to study spatial orientation and perception of <a href="/wiki/Gravity" title="Gravity" class="mw-redirect">gravity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Motion_(physics)" title="Motion (physics)">motion</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Space_Agency" title="Canadian Space Agency">Canadian Space Agency</a> and National Space Biomedical Research Institute (NSBRI) use this room to strengthen astronauts? sense of ?up? and ?down? in zero-gravity environments. The room is a six-sided immersive environment made of the glass used in the <a href="/wiki/CN_Tower" title="CN Tower">CN Tower</a>?s observation deck and includes walls, ceiling, and a floor made of computer-generated <a href="/wiki/Pixel" title="Pixel">pixel</a> maps. York's Faculty of Science and Engineering most recently took part in the 2007 NASA <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_(spacecraft)" title="Phoenix (spacecraft)">Phoenix</a> Mars Mission.</p> 
     <p>York is also the only university in Canada with specialized programs in <a href="/wiki/Meteorological" title="Meteorological" class="mw-redirect">meteorological</a> sciences at both the undergraduate and graduate level.</p>

     <p>York's primary campus (&quot;The Keele Campus&quot;) is located in the north of the City of Toronto and bordering York Region. It is the largest post-secondary campus in Canada at 457 acres.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup> Most of the University's faculties reside here, including Liberal Arts, Fine Arts, Business, Law, Environmental Studies, Science and Engineering, Education, and Health. All together, nearly 50,000 students attend classes on the Keele campus.</p> 
     <p>York has over 200,000 living alumni. Although a large number of alumni live in Ontario, a significant number live in British Columbia, Nova Scotia, Alberta, New York, and Washington, D.C. York also has over 25,000 alumni overseas.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Glendon_Campus">Glendon Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Glendon Campus">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Glendon_College" title="Glendon College">Glendon College</a>, a bilingual liberal arts faculty which conducts its own recruitment and admissions and hosts its own academic programs, is also housed on its own campus on Bayview Avenue in North Toronto. Glendon is the only university-level institution in central Southern Ontario that offers university courses in both <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>; others elsewhere in Ontario include the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Ottawa" title="University of Ottawa">University of Ottawa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Laurentian_University" title="Laurentian University">Laurentian University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greater_Sudbury" title="Greater Sudbury">Sudbury</a>. A shuttle bus runs regularly between the Glendon and the Keele campuses. Glendon students are free to take courses at the Keele campus (and vice-versa). However, there are students who consider getting from one campus to the other to be inconvenient, and thus the attractiveness of this option is not universal.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Satellite_Campuses">Satellite Campuses</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Satellite Campuses">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>While most of the Schulich School of Business and Osgoode Hall Law School programs are offered at the Keele Campus, both of them maintain satellite facilities in downtown Toronto. Schulich operates the Miles S. Nadal Management Centre, while Osgoode Hall has a Professional Development Centre located at 1 Dundas Street West.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_life">Student life</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Student life">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>York has more than 50,000 students enrolled. Many students come from the Greater Toronto Area, but there is a sizeable population of students from across Canada and abroad, making York one of the most international universities in Canada. To serve this large population, there are 290 student clubs and organizations; six student-run publications and three broadcast programs; six art galleries; 33 on-campus eateries; and a retail mall. Undergraduate students at York are represented by the <a href="/wiki/York_Federation_of_Students" title="York Federation of Students">York Federation of Students</a>, a student-elected body that sponsors most of the clubs, and engages in lobbying with the university administration as well as the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ontario" title="Government of Ontario">provincial</a> and <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Canada" title="Government of Canada">federal</a> governments.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

     <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Village">The Village</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: The Village">edit</a>]</span></h4> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Village_at_York" title="Village at York" class="mw-redirect">Village at York</a> off-campus student housing area has become a popular area of accommodation for many upper-year and post-graduate students, and the area has had a large amount of attention particularly for large parties hosted by students, including the annual Battle of the Village kegger held in March. There have also been many reports of the level of noise pollution from late-night parties from students living in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> Safety has also been a pressing issue.</p> 
     <p>The Village is a residential neighbourhood within the City of Toronto, occupying about 130 acres beyond the south boundary of York University's property. Residential dwellings in The Village are privately owned, and homeowners in this community are City of Toronto taxpayers. York University participates in the Village At York Town and Gown Committee, with representatives of residents of The Village; the York Federation of Students; Toronto Police Service and Toronto Fire Service, as well as other key municipal agencies and the local Councillors' office. This committee addresses concerns of residents, students, the City and the University, such as community safety and by-law enforcement and compliance.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newspapers_and_other_publications">Newspapers and other publications</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Newspapers and other publications">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p><i><a href="/wiki/Excalibur_(newspaper)" title="Excalibur (newspaper)">Excalibur</a></i> has been the university's autonomous student newspaper since 1966. In 2008, the <i>YU Free Press</i> was formed as an alternative campus newspaper.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Colleges and some programs also have individual newspapers or magazines. They include: <i>The Flying Walrus</i> (Stong College), <i>MacMedia</i> (McLaughlin College), <i>The Pipe</i> (Calumet College), <i>Artichoke</i> (Winters College), <i>SOFA</i> (Spotlight On Fine Arts), <i>The Lexicon</i> (Norman Bethune College), <i>The Vandoo</i> (<a href="/wiki/Vanier_College_at_York_University" title="Vanier College at York University">Vanier College</a>), <i>The Phoenix</i> (Founders College), <i>Pro Tem</i> (Glendon College), <i>Obiter Dicta</i> (Osgoode Law School), and <i>The Insider</i> (Schulich School of Business).</p> 
     <p><i><a href="/wiki/Existere" title="Existere">Existere</a> ? Journal of Arts and Literature</i> (est. 1978) is a national publication with local and international contributors. It is financed by Vanier College Council. The journal publishes short fiction, poetry, non-fiction and art from novices and seasoned veterans. Several major writers got their early start in <i>Existere</i>.</p> 
     <p><i>YorkU Magazine</i> (est. 2003) is the official magazine of York University. It is published 3 times a year in both a print and digital format.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="thumb tleft"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:YorkUvsUofGuelph.jpg" class="image"><img alt="YorkUvsUofGuelph.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/YorkUvsUofGuelph.jpg/200px-YorkUvsUofGuelph.jpg" width="200" height="169" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/YorkUvsUofGuelph.jpg/300px-YorkUvsUofGuelph.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/YorkUvsUofGuelph.jpg/400px-YorkUvsUofGuelph.jpg 2x" /></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:YorkUvsUofGuelph.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf9/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a>
        </div> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>The University is represented in <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Interuniversity_Sport" title="Canadian Interuniversity Sport">Canadian Interuniversity Sport</a> by the <a href="/wiki/York_Lions" title="York Lions">York Lions</a>. Beginning in 1968 York's sporting teams were known as the &quot;<a href="/wiki/Yeomen" title="Yeomen" class="mw-redirect">Yeomen</a>&quot;, after the <a href="/wiki/Yeomen_Warders" title="Yeomen Warders">Yeomen Warders</a>, the guardians of the fortress and palace at the <a href="/wiki/Tower_of_London" title="Tower of London">Tower of London</a>, otherwise known as Beefeaters. Later, the name &quot;Yeowomen&quot; was introduced to encourage women to participate in sports. Popular sentiment ran against this name scheme, however, as many students were fond of noting that a &quot;Yeowoman&quot; was fictitious, neither a real word nor having any historical merit. In 2003, after conducting an extensive internal study, the University replaced both names with the &quot;Lions&quot;, as part of a larger renaming effort, and a new logo, now a white and red lion, was brought into line with the university's new visual scheme. The name change also brought York University in line with the 92% of other Canadian universities which use a single name for both sexes' sports teams. Ironically, students often refer to the female Lions teams as the &quot;York Lionesses&quot;, even though the name &quot;Lion&quot; is intended to apply to both sexes.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>York offers 29 interuniversity sport teams, 12 sport clubs, 35 intramural sport leagues, special events and 10 pick-up sport activities offered daily.</p> 
     <p>York University has several athletic facilities, some of which are used for major tournaments. These include a football stadium, 4 gymnasia, 5 sport playing fields, 4 softball fields, 9 outdoor tennis courts, 5 squash courts, 3 dance/aerobic studios, 6 ice arenas, a swimming pool, an expanding fitness centre and the new <a href="/wiki/Rexall_Centre" title="Rexall Centre">Rexall Centre</a> (home of the <a href="/wiki/Canada_Masters" title="Canada Masters" class="mw-redirect">Rogers Tennis Cup</a>).</p> 
     <p>In 2005, plans were made to build a new football and soccer stadium to host the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Argonauts" title="Toronto Argonauts">Toronto Argonauts</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Football_League" title="Canadian Football League">Canadian Football League</a> and future football tournaments. These plans were scuttled, however, when a deal was signed by the Argos to remain at the <a href="/wiki/Rogers_Centre" title="Rogers Centre">Rogers Centre</a>.</p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fraternities_and_Sororities">Fraternities and Sororities</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Fraternities and Sororities">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>With both fraternities and sororities operating on campus, York University is home to a vibrant &quot;Greek&quot; community. Though the organizations are not officially recognized by the University, they do provide member students with valuable experiences throughout their post-secondary academic careers to supplement campus life. Many lifelong friendships are forged through these societies, which place a great deal of emphasis on academic achievement, leadership and philanthropy.</p>

     <p>York University is sometimes referred to as a &quot;commuter school&quot;. Over 65% of the students and staff have home addresses in the Greater Toronto Area (GTA), particularly in York Region and downtown Toronto. Many students are opting for public transit owing to York's high parking fees. York intends to increase the fees for parking to combat congestion around campus and to support the goal of making Toronto more environmentally friendly.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p> 
     <p>Close to fourteen hundred buses move people through the campus each day. An extension of the <a href="/wiki/Yonge-University-Spadina_line" title="Yonge-University-Spadina line" class="mw-redirect">Yonge-University-Spadina line</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Subway_and_RT" title="Toronto Subway and RT" class="mw-redirect">Toronto Subway</a> is currently under construction. It will run directly under the campus, creating new stations at <a href="/wiki/Keele_Street" title="Keele Street">Keele Street</a> and <a href="/wiki/Finch_Avenue" title="Finch Avenue">Finch Avenue</a> (as <a href="/wiki/Finch_West_(TTC)" title="Finch West (TTC)">Finch West station</a>), at the centre of campus (as <a href="/wiki/York_University_(TTC)" title="York University (TTC)">York University station</a>), and at Steeles Avenue, interfacing with <a href="/wiki/York_Regional_Transit" title="York Regional Transit" class="mw-redirect">York Regional Transit</a> (as <a href="/wiki/Black_Creek_Pioneer_Village_(TTC)" title="Black Creek Pioneer Village (TTC)">Black Creek Pioneer Village station</a>).</p> 
     <p>York University's Glendon and Keele campuses are served by the TTC. The Keele site is also served by <a href="/wiki/York_Region_Transit" title="York Region Transit">York Region Transit</a> buses (both regular and <a href="/wiki/Viva_(bus_rapid_transit)" title="Viva (bus rapid transit)" class="mw-redirect">Viva</a>) from the immediate north, <a href="/wiki/GO_Transit" title="GO Transit">GO Transit</a> express buses from several other Toronto suburbs and colleges or universities (such as <a href="/wiki/Sheridan_College" title="Sheridan College">Sheridan College</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Ontario_Institute_of_Technology" title="University of Ontario Institute of Technology">UOIT</a>) along with <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_Lines" title="Greyhound Lines">Greyhound buses</a> for regional transportation. Transportation Services operates a shuttle service to GO Transit's York University train station on its Barrie corridor. As of November 20, 2009, express buses on the highly frequent 196 York University Rocket <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Transit_Commission" title="Toronto Transit Commission">Toronto Transit Commission</a> (TTC) <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Transit_Commission_buses" title="Toronto Transit Commission buses">bus</a> route now use the dedicated <a href="/wiki/York_University_Busway" title="York University Busway">York University Busway</a> to transport students from <a href="/wiki/Downsview_(TTC)" title="Downsview (TTC)">Downsview station</a> to York Lanes in about 15 minutes. It consists of bus-only lanes on Allen Road and Dufferin Street, and bus-only roadways through a <a href="/wiki/Hydro_corridor" title="Hydro corridor" class="mw-redirect">hydro corridor</a> north of Finch Avenue West, and along the east side of the campus. As of September 20, 2010, the <a href="/wiki/Z%C3%BCm" title="Z&uuml;m">Z&uuml;m</a> Route 501 provides service from <a href="/wiki/Bramalea_Terminal" title="Bramalea Terminal">Bramalea Terminal</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brampton" title="Brampton">Brampton</a> to York University.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campus_safety">Campus safety</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Campus safety">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>York University Department of Security Services provides security services on the university's campuses. York Security Services provide uniformed security staff which consist of campus liaison officers (community services unit) and the Investigative Unit which works closely with the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Police_Services" title="Toronto Police Services" class="mw-redirect">Toronto Police Services</a>. The investigative unit and Toronto Police Investigative Units and 31 Division work jointly on serious investigations. Patrols are conducted on foot, bicycle and vehicle. The security service is a member of the Ontario Association of College and University Security Administrators (OACUSA) and the International Association of Campus Law Enforcement Administrators (IACLEA). The campus community is over 55,000 people.</p> 
     <p>The department uses marked Ford Crown Victorias which are clearly identifiable with low-profile LED roof lights. The department responds to all calls for service, however incidents of a criminal nature should be reported to Toronto Police. Uniform security staff can be clearly identified by their dark cargo-style pants, red shirt (York University colour) and black exterior body armour (bulletproof vest covers). Residence watch staff are also posted nightly at all undergraduate residence buildings to provide an extra level of protection. As part of the campus safety system a student escort service is available to all members of the community. The escort staff are students hired on a part-time basis. In an emergency escort staff have immediate communication with Security Services.</p> 
     <p>In June 2008, the university announced it had commissioned an external safety audit after a string of rapes on the university campus. During <a href="/wiki/Frosh_week" title="Frosh week" class="mw-redirect">frosh week</a> 2007, two men entered a campus dormitory and raped two students.<sup id="cite_ref-newsite.excal.on.ca_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsite.excal.on.ca-69"><span>[</span>69<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70"><span>[</span>70<span>]</span></a></sup> The victims were 17 and 18 years old at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71"><span>[</span>71<span>]</span></a></sup> A lawsuit filed claims that &quot;the entrance door to the college and to the residential room areas of the college were virtually wide open to the public at large&quot;.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72"><span>[</span>72<span>]</span></a></sup> The next year, in January 2008, another student was sexually assaulted in the stairwell of a campus building.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73"><span>[</span>73<span>]</span></a></sup> In May 2009, a contract security guard was shot at a York University campus pub, The Underground.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74"><span>[</span>74<span>]</span></a></sup> In April 2010, a 20-year-old student was sexually assaulted while walking to her apartment, located minutes away from the main campus.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75"><span>[</span>75<span>]</span></a></sup> The incident, described by police as &quot;particularly severe&quot; and resulting in &quot;very serious&quot; injuries for the victim, was particularly notable as it occurred just a week after Daniel Katsnelson was sentenced for the 2007 rape on the university campus.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76"><span>[</span>76<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77"><span>[</span>77<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The incident further reignited criticism against the school for continued delays in releasing the campus safety audit, which was first announced nearly 2 years prior.<sup id="cite_ref-newsite.excal.on.ca_69-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsite.excal.on.ca-69"><span>[</span>69<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78"><span>[</span>78<span>]</span></a></sup> On March 31, 2010, less than a month prior to the April 2010 incident, the campus newspaper, <a href="/wiki/Excalibur" title="Excalibur">Excalibur</a> published an article lambasting the school administration for delays in releasing the audit.<sup id="cite_ref-newsite.excal.on.ca_69-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsite.excal.on.ca-69"><span>[</span>69<span>]</span></a></sup> A spokesman for the university responded that the school is still &quot;anxiously awaiting&quot; the report.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In April 2011, campus security was temporarily increased following the murder of a 23-year old <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/International_student" title="International student">overseas student</a><sup id="cite_ref-CBC_80-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBC-80"><span>[</span>80<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GNM_81-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GNM-81"><span>[</span>81<span>]</span></a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/Village_at_York" title="Village at York" class="mw-redirect">York University Village</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TOON_82-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TOON-82"><span>[</span>82<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IOg_83-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOg-83"><span>[</span>83<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84"><span>[</span>84<span>]</span></a></sup> The university temporarily hired paid duty Toronto Police Officers to patrol the campus.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On August 26, 2011, York University announced that in response to recommendations in the METRAC Safety Audit, the University will be modifying the service delivery model for York Security Services (YSS) personnel. Members will be trained and issued with handcuffs and batons. Cruisers are to also be equipped with &quot;silent partners&quot; for transporting arrested persons.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85"><span>[</span>85<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>On November 20, 2012, York University initiated the intervention capable delivery model for security services. Having been certified after undergoing training in August 2012, personnel were equipped with handcuffs and batons, and began wearing a dark grey uniform, replacing the red uniform, to signify the change in role for the community.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86"><span>[</span>86<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Violation_of_academic_freedom">Violation of academic freedom</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Violation of academic freedom">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>In the aftermath of an academic conference that took place in 2009, titled &quot;Israel/Palestine: Mapping Models of Statehood and Paths to Peace&quot;,<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88"><span>[</span>88<span>]</span></a></sup> which explored the possible models of statehood for Israel/Palestine, including the one state model, concerns were raised about the way the York Administration has handled the political pressure.<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89"><span>[</span>89<span>]</span></a></sup> Some of the organizers accused the York administration, mainly the then Dean of the Osgoode Hall Law School and the Associate Vice President for Research and Innovation, of putting undue pressure on the organizers in order to force them to change the content of the conference, invite or disinvite some speakers. The accusations<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup> were supported by documents and emails that were obtained through the Freedom of Information and Privacy Protection Act.</p> 
     <p>The York administration appointed former Supreme Court of Canada Judge to review the issue, but the Iacobucci Review was problematic, and the terms of reference<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91"><span>[</span>91<span>]</span></a></sup> for the review were seen as an attack on academic freedom. The whole issue is being investigated by the Canadian Association of University Teachers.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92"><span>[</span>92<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In response to the allegations made, a University spokesperson said that the University should be judged the fact that event took place despite the pressure not to hold it, and that there is always internal discussion as part of the planning of all events. He added that &quot;In the end, this conference did go on and we do not feel that academic freedom was breached.&quot;<sup id="cite_ref-theglobeandmail.com_93-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theglobeandmail.com-93"><span>[</span>93<span>]</span></a></sup> Vice President and Provost, Patrick Monahan, said, about these allegation that &quot;Justice Iacobucci has looked at that and he doesn?t see any purpose in conducting further inquiries. Obviously there are a lot of different views about it.?<sup id="cite_ref-theglobeandmail.com_93-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theglobeandmail.com-93"><span>[</span>93<span>]</span></a></sup> Yet, it seems that the Iaccobucci Report is seen by many faculty members at Osgoode as controversial. In a letter from the Osgoode Hall Faculty Association, the Association said that it &quot;considers the Iacobucci Report to be unsound and unreliable.&quot;<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94"><span>[</span>94<span>]</span></a></sup> The Association also said that &quot;the Report both jeopardizes academic freedom and fails to consider the troubling conduct of the York officials.&quot;</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Intimidation_of_Pro-Israel_Groups">Intimidation of Pro-Israel Groups</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Intimidation of Pro-Israel Groups">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>On February 11, 2009, approximately 100 pro-Palestinian students reportedly initiated a near-riot against a group of Jewish students during a news conference where speakers called for the impeachment of the York Federation of Students (YFS) executive. According to witnesses, the demonstrators, which reportedly consisted of members of the YFS and Students Against Israeli Apartheid (SAIA), shouted ?Zionism equals racism!? and ?Racists off campus!? One witness stated that ?a riot broke out. They [YFS supporters] started banging the door and windows, intimidating Jewish students and screaming antisemitic slurs.? The students barricaded themselves inside the Hillel offices, where protesters reportedly banged on the windows and attempted to force their way in. Eventually police were called to escort Jewish students through the protesters.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95"><span>[</span>95<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96"><span>[</span>96<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97"><span>[</span>97<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98"><span>[</span>98<span>]</span></a></sup> The claims of anti-Semitic slurs were not reported by the media that were present at the time, as many believe that this accusation lacks credibility.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99"><span>[</span>99<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Krisna Saravanamuttu, York Federation of Students' vice-president of equity, who took part in the protest, denied that the protesters shouted anti-semitic slogans, stating that &quot;That is categorically false. I heard nothing of that nature at all.&quot; He did however, confirm that the protesters shouted &quot;racism off campus&quot; and &quot;students united will never be defeated.&quot;<sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100"><span>[</span>100<span>]</span></a></sup> Some York faculty and students have rejected these claims of anti-Semitism as media spin and exaggeration <sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101"><span>[</span>101<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In May 2009, York adjudicator Janet Mosher, who is an associate dean at York?s Osgoode Hall Law School, ruled that two York students, Krisna Saravanamuttu and Jesse Zimmerman, had violated the Student Code of Conduct due to their behaviour at the protest, which she described as ?exclusionary and offensive? and which promoted an atmosphere of ?hostility, incivility and intimidation.? Mosher noted that both students participated in the protest which pursued a group of Jewish students to Hillel?s lounge in York?s Student Centre, and swarmed outside shouting taunts. On a video of the incident, Saravanamuttu was shown clapping and apparently leading a chant of ?Whose campus? Our campus!? as well as participating in a chant of ?Racists off campus.&quot; Saravanamuttu was fined $150 and both he and Zimmerman were given an official reprimand and human rights training.<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102"><span>[</span>102<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103"><span>[</span>103<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104"><span>[</span>104<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In February 2010, the campus group the Christians United for Israel (CUFI) and <i>My Canada</i> applied to use university space to host the <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.imaginewithus.ca/">Imagine With Us</a></i> coalition event consisting of pro-Israel speakers. The University replied that the event could only proceed under certain conditions (which ultimately led to the event's cancellation when the organizers declined to comply with the terms):</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>the organizers would be required to pay for security, including both campus and Toronto police;</li> 
      <li>the organizers must provide an advance list of all program attendees and advance minute-by-minute summaries of all the speeches; and</li> 
      <li>No advertising for the program would be permitted on campus.<sup id="cite_ref-Frum20100227_105-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frum20100227-105"><span>[</span>105<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-JT20100224_106-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JT20100224-106"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup></li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>These conditions drew criticism because they were not imposed on the organizers of <a href="/wiki/Israel_Apartheid_Week" title="Israel Apartheid Week" class="mw-redirect">Israel Apartheid Week</a> which was being held on campus the same month.<sup id="cite_ref-Frum20100227_105-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frum20100227-105"><span>[</span>105<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-JT20100224_106-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JT20100224-106"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup> York's decision drew sharp criticism from <a href="/wiki/David_Frum" title="David Frum">David Frum</a> who wrote in the <i><a href="/wiki/National_Post" title="National Post">National Post</a></i> that &quot;Since the anti-Israel people might use violence, the speech of the pro-Israel people must be limited. On the other hand, since the pro-Israel people do not use violence, the speech of the anti-Israel people can proceed without restraint.&quot; A York University spokesman subsequently told Frum that &quot;all student groups that request university space&quot; must meet &quot;precisely same requirements&quot; but that while the ?process? and the ?protocols? that were the same, a ?needs-based assessment? of each particular case is necessary. Frum subsequently criticized the &quot;utterly arbitrary ad hoc decision-making of a fathomlessly cowardly university administration.&quot; Frank Dimant, CEO of <a href="/wiki/B%27nai_Brith_Canada" title="B'nai Brith Canada">B'nai Brith Canada</a> also sharply criticized York's justification, arguing that &quot;York?s continued appeasement of anti-Israel agitators at the expense of Zionist Christians and Jews is unacceptable.?<sup id="cite_ref-Frum20100227_105-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frum20100227-105"><span>[</span>105<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Professor <a href="/wiki/Ed_Morgan_(professor)" title="Ed Morgan (professor)">Ed Morgan</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a> criticized York, citing a 1992 ruling by the U.S. Supreme Court that struck down a county government's increased fee for police protection for a controversial speaker because &quot;speech cannot be financially burdened, any more than it can be punished or banned, simply because it might offend a hostile mob.&quot; Regarding the situation at York, Morgan wrote that &quot;It's bad enough that there are &quot;hostile mobs&quot; on our campuses; making others pay for that hostility only rubs salt in our wounded freedoms.&quot;<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107"><span>[</span>107<span>]</span></a></sup> Prof. Howard C. Tenenbaum, also of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a>, wrote that York &quot;has lost all stature as an academic institution whose remit is to provide for full academic discourse, freedom from hatred on campus and freedom of speech, unless of course that freedom only includes unabashed hatred for the State of Israel.&quot;<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108"><span>[</span>108<span>]</span></a></sup> David Murrell of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_New_Brunswick" title="University of New Brunswick">University of New Brunswick</a> wrote that &quot;Everyone has a supposed right to free speech at York University ? so long as groups can afford to pay security against leftist intimidation.&quot;<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109"><span>[</span>109<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Strikes">Strikes</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=York_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Strikes">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>York University has a history of faculty and teaching assistant strikes. In 1997, there was a faculty strike by <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.yufa.org/">YUFA</a> that lasted seven weeks. At the time, this was the second longest strike in Canadian University history.<sup id="cite_ref-110" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-110"><span>[</span>110<span>]</span></a></sup> Key issues in the strike included retirement, funding, and institutional governance. In 2001, teaching assistants and contract faculty went on strike for 11 weeks, when the university broke its own record.<sup id="cite_ref-111" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-111"><span>[</span>111<span>]</span></a></sup> The central issue in the 2001 disruption was the administration's proposed attempts to remove tuition indexation language.</p> 
     <p>A strike beginning on November 6, 2008<sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112"><span>[</span>112<span>]</span></a></sup> concerned a variety of institutional grievances, including job security for contract professors, elimination of the Non-Academic Student Code of Conduct, creation of whistleblower protection, and fund indexation. On January 20, 2009, CUPE 3903 defeated a forced ratification vote that would have ended the strike. On January 24, Ontario <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_Ontario" title="Premier of Ontario">premier</a> <a href="/wiki/Dalton_McGuinty" title="Dalton McGuinty">Dalton McGuinty</a> announced a rare Sunday recall of the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Ontario" title="Legislative Assembly of Ontario">provincial legislature</a> in order to pass back-to-work legislation mandating an immediate end to the strike.<sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-113"><span>[</span>113<span>]</span></a></sup> On January 29, the York University Labour Disputes Resolution Act was passed in the provincial parliament on a count of 42?8 ending the long 85-day strike and setting a precedent for future university strikes in Ontario.</p>
