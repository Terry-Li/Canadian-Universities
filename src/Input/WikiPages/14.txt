     <p>The <b>University of Victoria</b>, often referred to as <b>UVic</b>, a research intensive <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Saanich,_British_Columbia" title="Saanich, British Columbia">Saanich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oak_Bay,_British_Columbia" title="Oak Bay, British Columbia">Oak Bay</a>, about 5&nbsp;mi (8&nbsp;km) northeast of downtown <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_British_Columbia" title="Victoria, British Columbia">Victoria, British Columbia</a>. The University's annual enrollment is about 20,000 students. UVic's campus is known for its innovative architecture, beautiful gardens and <a href="/wiki/Oak_Bay,_British_Columbia#Climate" title="Oak Bay, British Columbia">mild climate</a>. The university was closely affiliated with and established by <a href="/wiki/McGill_University" title="McGill University">McGill University</a>, which is also credited with the beginnings of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">University of British Columbia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The University attracts many students in part because of its size, its picturesque location, and its Cooperative Education, Earth &amp; Ocean Sciences, Engineering, and Law programs. The University is the nation's lead institution in the <a href="/wiki/VENUS" title="VENUS">VENUS</a> and <a href="/wiki/NEPTUNE" title="NEPTUNE">NEPTUNE</a> deep-water seafloor observatory projects.</p> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Vikes" title="Victoria Vikes">Victoria Vikes</a> (more commonly known as the <i>UVic Vikes</i> or simply the <i>Vikes</i>) represent the University in the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Interuniversity_Sport" title="Canadian Interuniversity Sport">Canadian Interuniversity Sport</a> (CIS) community in a number of competitive sports, as well as through a variety of intercollegiate leagues. The Vikes have especially long and eminent ties to <a href="/wiki/Rowing_(sport)" title="Rowing (sport)">competitive rowing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>.</p> 
     <p>The University is ranked among the top 200 universities in the world<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> and ninth place<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> in Canada in the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Times_Higher_Education_World_University_Rankings" title="Times Higher Education World University Rankings">Times Higher Education World University Rankings</a> and first place in Canada in the Times Higher Education?s 100-under-50 rankings.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> UVic was the top-ranked university in Canada without an autonomous medical school in the THE rankings. The University has also been home to more than 40 faculty members who are Fellows of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Canada" title="Royal Society of Canada">Royal Society of Canada</a> since the University of Victoria's founding.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>University of Victoria was established on 1 July 1963 in <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_British_Columbia" title="Victoria, British Columbia">Victoria, British Columbia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Victoria_College,_British_Columbia" title="Victoria College, British Columbia">Victoria College</a>, which had been established in 1903 as an affiliated college of <a href="/wiki/McGill_University" title="McGill University">McGill University</a>, gained autonomy and full degree granting status on March 1, 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The non-denominational university had enjoyed 60 years of prior teaching tradition at the university level as <b><a href="/wiki/Victoria_College,_British_Columbia" title="Victoria College, British Columbia">Victoria College</a></b>. This 60 years of history may be viewed conveniently in three distinct stages. Between the years 1903 and 1915, Victoria College was affiliated with <a href="/wiki/McGill_University" title="McGill University">McGill University</a>, offering first- and second-year McGill courses in Arts and Science.<sup id="cite_ref-PgNm.3DTCE.26Params.3DU1ARTU0003541_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PgNm.3DTCE.26Params.3DU1ARTU0003541-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Administered locally by the Victoria School Board, the College was an adjunct to <a href="/wiki/Victoria_High_School_(British_Columbia)" title="Victoria High School (British Columbia)">Victoria High School</a> and shared its facilities. Both institutions were under the direction of a single Principal: E.B. Paul, 1903?1908; and S.J. Willis, 1908?1915.</p> 
     <p>The opening in 1915 of the University of British Columbia, established by Act of Legislature in 1908, obliged the College to suspend operations in higher education in Victoria. University of British Columbia was created in 1908. A single, public provincial university, it was modeled on the American state university, with an emphasis on extension work and applied research. The governance was modeled on the provincial University of Toronto Act of 1906 which established a bicameral system of university government consisting of a senate (faculty), responsible for academic policy, and a board of governors (citizens) exercising exclusive control over financial policy and having formal authority in all other matters. The president, appointed by the board, was to provide a link between the two bodies and to perform institutional leadership.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 1920, as a result of local demands, Victoria College began the second stage of its development, reborn in affiliation with the University of British Columbia.<sup id="cite_ref-PgNm.3DTCE.26Params.3DU1ARTU0003541_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PgNm.3DTCE.26Params.3DU1ARTU0003541-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Though still administered by the Victoria School Board, the College was now completely separated from Victoria High School, moving in 1921 into the magnificent Dunsmuir mansion known as <a href="/wiki/Craigdarroch_Castle" title="Craigdarroch Castle">Craigdarroch Castle</a>. Over the next two decades, under Principals E.B. Paul and P.H. Elliott, Victoria College built a reputation for thorough and scholarly instruction in first- and second-year Arts and Science. It was also during this period that future author <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Berton" title="Pierre Berton">Pierre Berton</a> edited and served as principal cartoonist for the student newsletter, <i>The Microscope</i>.</p>

     <p>The final stage, between the years 1945 and 1963, saw the transition from two year college to university, under Principals J.M. Ewing and W.H. Hickman.<sup id="cite_ref-PgNm.3DTCE.26Params.3DU1ARTU0003541_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PgNm.3DTCE.26Params.3DU1ARTU0003541-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> During this period, the College was governed by the Victoria College Council, representative of the parent University of British Columbia, the Greater Victoria School Board, and the provincial Department of Education. Physical changes were many. In 1946 the College was forced by postwar enrollment to move from Craigdarroch to the Lansdowne campus of the Provincial Normal School (This is the current location of the <a href="/wiki/Camosun_College" title="Camosun College">Camosun College</a> Lansdowne Campus). The Normal School, itself an institution with a long and honourable history, joined Victoria College in 1956 as its Faculty of Education. Late in this transitional period (through the co-operation of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_National_Defence_(Canada)" title="Department of National Defence (Canada)">Department of National Defence</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hudson%27s_Bay_Company" title="Hudson's Bay Company">Hudson's Bay Company</a>) the 284&nbsp;acre (1,1&nbsp;km&sup2;) now 385&nbsp;acre (1.6&nbsp;km&sup2;) campus at Gordon Head was acquired. Academic expansion was rapid after 1956, until in 1961 the College, still in affiliation with UBC awarded its first bachelor's degrees.</p> 
     <p>In the early part of this century, professional education expanded beyond the traditional fields of theology, law and medicine. Graduate training based on the German-inspired American model of specialized course work and the completion of a research thesis was introduced.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The policy of university education initiated in the 1960s responded to population pressure and the belief that higher education was a key to social justice and economic productivity for individuals and for society.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The university gained its autonomy in 1963 as the University of Victoria.<sup id="cite_ref-PgNm.3DTCE.26Params.3DU1ARTU0003541_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PgNm.3DTCE.26Params.3DU1ARTU0003541-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The University Act of 1963 vested administrative authority in a chancellor elected by the convocation of the university, a board of governors, and a president appointed by the board; academic authority was given to the senate which was representative both of the faculties and of the convocation.</p> 
     <p>University of Victoria's Arms were registered with the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Heraldic_Authority" title="Canadian Heraldic Authority">Canadian Heraldic Authority</a> on April 3, 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> The historical traditions of the university are reflected in the coat of arms, its academic regalia and its house flag. The BA hood is of solid red, a colour that recalls the early affiliation with McGill. The BSc hood, of gold, and the BEd hood, of blue, show the colours of the University of British Columbia. Blue and gold have been retained as the official colours of the University of Victoria. The motto at the top of the Arms of the University, in Hebrew characters, is &quot;Let there be Light&quot;; the motto at the bottom, in Latin, is &quot;A Multitude of the Wise is the Health of the World.&quot;</p>

     <p>The main Campus is located in the <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Head" title="Gordon Head">Gordon Head</a> area of Greater Victoria. With a total area of 403 acres (163&nbsp;ha), the campus spans the border between the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Oak_Bay,_British_Columbia" title="Oak Bay, British Columbia">Oak Bay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saanich,_British_Columbia" title="Saanich, British Columbia">Saanich</a>. The original campus plan was prepared by the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> architecture and planning firm of <a href="/wiki/Wurster,_Bernardi_%26_Emmons" title="Wurster, Bernardi &amp; Emmons" class="mw-redirect">Wurster, Bernardi &amp; Emmons</a>. The general concept of the original design is still being followed with the academic portions of the campus located inside Ring Road which forms a perfect circle 600&nbsp;m (1,969&nbsp;ft) in diameter. Outside of Ring Road are the parking lots, Student Union Building, residence buildings, the sports facilities as well as some of the academic facilities that are more self-contained (Law and Theatre for example).</p>

     <p>The university also offers on-campus housing for over 3,200 students. An extensive variety of housing is available, including single and double rooms, apartment-style housing with four people per unit (Cluster Housing) and family housing (Lam Family Housing). Four buildings in one of the oldest residential complexes at the university are named for <a href="/wiki/Emily_Carr" title="Emily Carr">Emily Carr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Currie" title="Arthur Currie">Arthur Currie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Newton" title="Margaret Newton">Margaret Newton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/David_Thompson_(explorer)" title="David Thompson (explorer)">David Thompson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Craigdarroch_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Craigdarroch-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Construction on the South Tower Complex was completed in January 2011. The largest residence building in terms of capacity is Ring Road Hall, which holds 294 beds and is split into three wings.</p> 
     <p>Much of the university estate has been dedicated to nature, notably <a href="/wiki/Finnerty_Gardens" title="Finnerty Gardens">Finnerty Gardens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mystic_Vale" title="Mystic Vale">Mystic Vale</a>, a 4.4&nbsp;ha (11&nbsp;acres) forested ravine. The campus is home to deer, owls, squirrels and many other wild animals native to the area. A large population of domestic <a href="/wiki/Rabbit" title="Rabbit">rabbits</a>, which likely descended from abandoned house pets from the surrounding community, was a memorable feature of the campus in years past. In May 2010, the University began trapping and <a href="/wiki/Animal_euthanasia" title="Animal euthanasia">euthanizing</a> the rabbits <sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> as they have been known to put athletes at risk in the playing fields. It has been documented that local veterinarians have offered to perform neutering of the male rabbits. As of July 2011 the UVic campus is free of rabbits. 900 rabbits have been saved and sent to shelters.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_profile">Academic profile</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Academic profile">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Libraries_and_Museum_system">Libraries and Museum system</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Libraries and Museum system">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The University of Victoria Libraries system is the second largest in British Columbia being composed of three 'on-campus' libraries, the William C. Mearns Center for Learning/McPherson Library, the Diana M. Priestly Law Library, and the MacLaurin Curriculum Library. The Library System has undergone significant growth in recent years thanks to the University's investment in library purchases and research. Amongst the highlights in the University of Victoria Archives and Special Collections are priceless items from <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japan" title="Imperial Japan" class="mw-redirect">Imperial Japan</a>, to carbon dated original manuscripts of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sancti_Epiphanii&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sancti Epiphanii (page does not exist)">Sancti Epiphanii</a>. The collection also boasts extensive histories of colonial <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_British_Columbia" title="Victoria, British Columbia">Victoria</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Colony_of_Vancouver_Island" title="Colony of Vancouver Island">Colony of Vancouver Island</a> among other documents. The library's digitization programme is becoming increasingly active in making materials available for scholars and to the wider world. Renovations and new construction over the past decade have resulted in modernized facilities that include special collections classrooms, an innovative Learning Commons and an art gallery. The UVic libraries collection includes extensive digital resources, over 2.0 million books, 2.3 million items in microforms, plus serial subscriptions, sound recordings, music scores, films and videos, and archival materials.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The University of Victoria houses the Education Heritage Museum, which displays educational history artifacts in the main hallway of the MacLaurin building. The collection consists of manuscripts, texts, photographs, audio-visual material, lesson plans, posters, bells, ink bottles, fountain pens, desks, maps, athletic clothing, photographs, and school yearbooks used in kindergarten to grade 12 schools in Canada from the mid-1800s to the 1980s.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The University of Victoria has two art collections (University and Maltwood) which host loan exhibitions, and exhibits of the works of students and faculty in the University Centre Exhibition Gallery. The University Collection, founded in 1953 by Dr. W.H. Hickman, Principal of Victoria College (1953-1963), consists of 6,000 works, mainly by contemporary artists practicing in British Columbia. The Maltwood Art Museum and Gallery, founded through the bequest of English sculptress and antiquarian, Katharine Emma Maltwood, F.R.S.A. (1878-1961), reflects her and her husband John Maltwood's taste. The collection of 12,000 works of fine, decorative and applied arts includes Oriental ceramics, costumes, rugs, seventeenth century English furniture, Canadian paintings and Katherine Maltwood's own sculptures.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>In the 2010 <i>Re$earch Infosource</i> ranking of Canada's research universities, UVic topped all other comprehensive universities in Canada in two out of three measures of research performance over the last decade: growth in research income and growth in research intensity.</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Bamfield Marine Research Station</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>The University maintains a field station on the west coast of Vancouver Island to conduct marine research. The facility is jointly run by the University of British Columbia, Simon Fraser University, the University of Alberta and the University of Calgary. Undergraduates at the University of Victoria have full access to research and learning at this facility.</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>SEOS Oceanic Vessel</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>In 2011 the University, in collaboration with the provincial government purchased and modified a state of the art ocean vessel capable of launching 'deep sea submersibles' and conducting long range marine biology research expeditions. The 'floating laboratory' is undergoing upgrades and expansions currently and will be in service by late 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>VENUS/NEPTUNE</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>The School of Earth &amp; Ocean Sciences is also home to the renowned <a href="/wiki/VENUS" title="VENUS">VENUS</a> and <a href="/wiki/NEPTUNE" title="NEPTUNE">NEPTUNE</a> research institutes responsible for seismic, oceanic and climate change research.</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Centre for Law</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>Located in the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Victoria" title="Greater Victoria">Greater Victoria</a> area the University's legal centre provides free legal assistance to the disadvantaged as well as dealing with important environmental cases in British Columbia. The UVic Law Center is the only full-time, term clinical program offered by a Canadian law school. The Program reflects the Faculty's emphasis on integrating legal theory, legal skills, and community service while providing students with unique education and research opportunities.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Vancouver Island Technology Park (VITP)</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>Located in the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Victoria,_British_Columbia" title="Greater Victoria, British Columbia" class="mw-redirect">Greater Victoria, British Columbia</a> area the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vancouver_Island_Technology_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vancouver Island Technology Park (page does not exist)">Vancouver Island Technology Park</a> is a state of the art, 35&nbsp;acre commercial research facility. It is the largest university-owned technology centre in BC. The venture allows the University to work with leading technology and biomedical companies while provided students with unparalleled research opportunities. The facility focuses on fuel cell, new media, wireless, and life science/biotechnological research. The UVic Genome BC Proteomics Centre and a number of other research institutes are based out of the research park. The <a href="/wiki/Capital_Regional_District" title="Capital Regional District">Capital Regional District</a> is a major commercial hub for technology companies.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Admissions">Admissions</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Admissions">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>Admission to the University of Victoria is based on a selective academic system. UVic requires all applicants to submit gross percentage averages to be considered for admission. The University accepts qualified applicants studying under IB programs, AP programs or other international distinctions. The University of Victoria offers scholarships and financial aid to a large number of students.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_exchanges">International exchanges</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: International exchanges">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The University Of Victoria has partnered with a number of research institutions to provide UVic students with the opportunity to gain research experience abroad. Both UVic undergraduate and graduate students may travel abroad with UVic's many partner universities. This international exchange programs develops the collegial yet international atmosphere at the University of Victoria, and promotes an exchange of information.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_student_life">Culture and student life</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Culture and student life">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Martlet_student_newspaper"><i>The Martlet</i> student newspaper</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: The Martlet student newspaper">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>UVic's oldest and most recognized weekly student newspaper, founded in 1948, is <i><a href="/wiki/The_Martlet" title="The Martlet">The Martlet</a></i>. It is distributed all over campus and the Greater Victoria area. The paper is named after the legendary martlet bird, whose inability to land is often seen to symbolize the constant quest for knowledge, learning, and adventure. <i>The Martlet</i> is partly funded by student fees.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_University_of_Victoria_Students_Society_.28UVSS.29">The University of Victoria Students Society (UVSS)</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: The University of Victoria Students Society (UVSS)">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Victoria_Students%27_Society" title="University of Victoria Students' Society">University of Victoria Students' Society</a> is a student society which represents the UVic undergraduate student body, plans campus wide events and maintains the Student Union Building. The student society's leadership is elected annually during campus wide undergraduate student elections. As a multi-million dollar organization, the UVSS is among one of the larger student unions which exist in Canada.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_University_of_Victoria_Graduate_Student_Society_.28GSS.29">The University of Victoria Graduate Student Society (GSS)</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: The University of Victoria Graduate Student Society (GSS)">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://gss.uvic.ca">GSS</a> offers services and support for UVic's 3,000 Graduate students. The society's services include the Grad House Restaurant, health and dental plan, funding for grad student events, and reduced-cost membership in the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://victoriacarshare.ca">Victoria Car Share Co-operative</a>.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_station_CFUV">Radio station CFUV</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Radio station CFUV">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/CFUV" title="CFUV" class="mw-redirect">CFUV</a> is on-campus radio station focusing on the campus and the surrounding community. CFUV serves <a href="/wiki/Greater_Victoria" title="Greater Victoria">Greater Victoria</a> at 101.9, and via cable on 104.3, <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Island" title="Vancouver Island">Vancouver Island</a> and many areas in the <a href="/wiki/Lower_Mainland" title="Lower Mainland">Lower Mainland</a> and northwestern <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington state</a>.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greek_Life">Greek Life</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Greek Life">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>UVic students have started one fraternity and two sororities. It should be noted that the university itself does not endorse the fraternity or the sororities. In that sense they have very little to do with UVic.<a href="/wiki/Delta_Kappa_Epsilon" title="Delta Kappa Epsilon">Delta Kappa Epsilon</a> Fraternity was installed in 2010, the International sorority <a href="/wiki/Kappa_Beta_Gamma" title="Kappa Beta Gamma">Kappa Beta Gamma</a> installed in 2011, and a local sorority <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Chi_Theta&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alpha Chi Theta (page does not exist)">Alpha Chi Theta</a> that was installed in 2013. The Fraternity/Sororities have no affiliation with the University of Victoria itself, however, they continue to thrive with membership growing yearly. The Omega chapter of <a href="/wiki/Phrateres" title="Phrateres">Phrateres</a>, a non-exclusive, non-profit social-service club, was installed here in 1961. Between 1924 and 1967, 23 chapters of Phrateres were installed in universities across North America, including three other chapters in Canadian universities.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletics">Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Athletics">edit</a>]</span></h2>

The facility was built in 1975.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>Notable among a number of songs commonly played and sung at various events such as commencement and convocation, and athletic games is 'Rack and Ruin' a reminder of the tradition of the founding Victoria College. <i>&quot;Rack and Ruin,</i> Blood and Gore, Victoria College Evermore!&quot;</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Frosh_Week">Frosh Week</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Frosh Week">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The first week of school and a celebration of admission to the University. Originally, Frosh Week was celebrated by students in all faculties, but its current iteration is exclusive to individual departments; most notably engineering and commerce. Engineering Frosh is a week-long series of extra-curricular events organized by the Engineering Students' Society. These activities include all types of games that pit the first years against each other in enrapturing games. Frosh familiarizes new students to the university, their upperclassmen, and their society in fun social events intended to ease the transition to university life.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Martlet_icon">Martlet icon</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Victoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Martlet icon">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Martlet" title="Martlet">martlet</a> and its red colour adorns many parts of the University of Victoria, including the crest, coast of arms, and flag representing the university's previous affiliation to <a href="/wiki/McGill_University" title="McGill University">McGill University</a> which also uses the martlet. The legendary martlet bird's inability to land is often seen to symbolize the constant quest for knowledge, learning, and adventure. The oldest student newspaper on campus, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Martlet" title="The Martlet">The Martlet</a></i>, is named after the bird.</p>
