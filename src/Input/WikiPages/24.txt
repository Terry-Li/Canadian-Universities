     <p><b>Ryerson University</b> is a public <a href="/wiki/Research_university" title="Research university" class="mw-redirect">research university</a> located in downtown <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. Its urban <a href="/wiki/Campus" title="Campus">campus</a> surrounds the <a href="/wiki/Yonge-Dundas_Square" title="Yonge-Dundas Square">Yonge-Dundas Square</a>, located at the busiest intersection in <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Toronto" title="Downtown Toronto">downtown Toronto</a>. The university has a focus on applied, career-oriented education. The majority of its buildings are in the blocks northeast of the Yonge-Dundas Square in Toronto's <a href="/wiki/Garden_District,_Toronto" title="Garden District, Toronto">Garden District</a>. Ryerson's business school, <a href="/wiki/Ted_Rogers_School_of_Management" title="Ted Rogers School of Management" class="mw-redirect">Ted Rogers School of Management</a> is on the southwest end of the Yonge-Dundas Square, located on Bay Street, slightly north of Toronto's <a href="/wiki/Financial_District,_Toronto" title="Financial District, Toronto">Financial District</a> and is attached to the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Eaton_Centre" title="Toronto Eaton Centre">Toronto Eaton Centre</a>. The university's most recent expansion, the Mattamy Athletic Centre, is located in the historical <a href="/wiki/Maple_Leaf_Gardens" title="Maple Leaf Gardens">Maple Leaf Gardens</a> hockey arena, the original home of the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Maple_Leafs" title="Toronto Maple Leafs">Toronto Maple Leafs</a>. The university is composed of 33,000+ undergraduate students, 2,300+ graduate students, and 65,000+ certificate and continuing education students.<sup id="cite_ref-2011-2012_Figures_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011-2012_Figures-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-aucc.ca_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aucc.ca-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Ryerson University is home to Canada's largest undergraduate business school, the <a href="/wiki/Ryerson_University#Ted_Rogers_School_of_Management" title="Ryerson University">Ted Rogers School of Management</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> and the Faculty of Engineering and Architectural Science, as well as the Faculty of Arts, Faculty of Communication &amp; Design, Faculty of Community Services, and the Faculty of Science.</p> 
     <p>In addition to offering full-time and part-time undergraduate and graduate programs leading to <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master's degree">Master's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doctorate" title="Doctorate">Doctoral</a> degrees, the university also offers part-time degrees, distance education and certificates through the <a href="/wiki/G._Raymond_Chang_School_of_Continuing_Education" title="G. Raymond Chang School of Continuing Education">G. Raymond Chang School of Continuing Education</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryerson_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <div class="thumb tleft">

     <p>In 1852 at the core of the present main campus, the historic St. James Square, <a href="/wiki/Egerton_Ryerson" title="Egerton Ryerson">Egerton Ryerson</a> founded Ontario's first teacher training facility, the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Normal_School" title="Toronto Normal School">Toronto Normal School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> It also housed the Department of Education and the Museum of Natural History and Fine Arts, which became the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Ontario_Museum" title="Royal Ontario Museum">Royal Ontario Museum</a>. An agricultural laboratory on the site led to the later founding of the <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Agricultural_College" title="Ontario Agricultural College">Ontario Agricultural College</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Guelph" title="University of Guelph">University of Guelph</a>. St. James Square went through various other educational uses before housing a namesake of its original founder.</p> 
     <p>Egerton Ryerson was a leading educator, politician, and Methodist minister.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> He is known as the father of Ontario's public school system.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> He is also a founder of the first publishing company in Canada in 1829, The Methodist Book and Publishing House, which was renamed <a href="/wiki/The_Ryerson_Press" title="The Ryerson Press" class="mw-redirect">The Ryerson Press</a> in 1919 and today is part of <a href="/wiki/McGraw-Hill_Ryerson" title="McGraw-Hill Ryerson" class="mw-redirect">McGraw-Hill Ryerson</a>, Canadian publisher of educational and professional books, which still bears Egerton Ryerson's name for its Canadian operations.</p> 
     <p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Toronto_Training_and_Re-establishment_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Toronto Training and Re-establishment Institute (page does not exist)">Toronto Training and Re-establishment Institute</a> was created in 1945 on the former site of the Toronto Normal School at St James Square, bounded by Gerrard, Church, Yonge and Gould. The Gothic-Romanesque building was designed by architects <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Ridout" title="Thomas Ridout">Thomas Ridout</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frederick_William_Cumberland" title="Frederick William Cumberland">Frederick William Cumberland</a> in 1852.<sup id="cite_ref-Pound2005_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pound2005-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The site had been used as a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Air_Force" title="Royal Canadian Air Force">Royal Canadian Air Force</a> training facility during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The institute was a joint venture of the federal and provincial government to train ex-servicemen and women for re-entry into civilian life.</p> 
     <p>The Ryerson Institute of Technology was founded in 1948, inheriting the staff and facilities of the Toronto Training and Re-establishment Institute. In 1966, its name was changed to Ryerson Polytechnical Institute. It became a university-degree granting institution in 1971 accredited by both provincial government legislation and by the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Universities_and_Colleges_of_Canada" title="Association of Universities and Colleges of Canada">Association of Universities and Colleges of Canada</a> (AUCC).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> That year, it also became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Ontario_Universities" title="Council of Ontario Universities">Council of Ontario Universities</a> (COU).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1992, Ryerson became Toronto?s second school of engineering following accreditation from the Canadian Engineering Accreditation Board.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 1993 Ryerson received approval to also grant graduate degrees (master's and doctorates). The same year it changed its name to Ryerson Polytechnic University, reflecting a stronger emphasis on research associated with graduate programs and its expansion from being a university offering undergraduate degrees. Students occupied the university's administration offices in March 1997, protesting escalating tuition hikes.<sup id="cite_ref-Pound2005_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pound2005-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In June 2001, the school assumed its present name as Ryerson University. Today, Ryerson University offers programs in chemical, civil, mechanical, industrial, electrical, biomedical and computer engineering. The B.Eng biomedical engineering program is the first standalone undergraduate biomedical engineering program in Canada. The university is also one of only two Ontario universities to offer a program in aerospace engineering.</p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ted_Rogers_School_of_Management">Ted Rogers School of Management</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryerson_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ted Rogers School of Management">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:RyeTRSM1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f3/RyeTRSM1.jpg/175px-RyeTRSM1.jpg" width="175" height="260" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f3/RyeTRSM1.jpg/263px-RyeTRSM1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f3/RyeTRSM1.jpg/350px-RyeTRSM1.jpg 2x" /></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:RyeTRSM1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf8/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a>

     <p>The Ted Rogers School of Management (TRSM) is an <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.aacsb.net/eweb/DynamicPage.aspx?Site=AACSB&amp;WebKey=ED088FF2-979E-48C6-B104-33768F1DE01D">AACSB accredited</a> business school at Ryerson University. Located on <a href="/wiki/Bay_Street" title="Bay Street">Bay Street</a> near Toronto's financial district, the TRSM offers various programs in a variety of business disciplines. The school houses Canada's largest undergraduate management program, along with several graduate programs.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> The school's undergraduate Bachelor of Commerce (BComm) programs are grouped into:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Business Management</li> 
      <li>Business Technology Management <i>(Co-op option available)</i></li> 
      <li>Hospitality and Tourism Management</li> 
      <li>Retail Management</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>The Ted Rogers School of Management is a recognized leader in entrepreneurship education in Canada and houses the <a href="/wiki/Ryerson_University_Entrepreneurship_Program" title="Ryerson University Entrepreneurship Program">Ryerson University Entrepreneurship Program</a>, one of the largest entrepreneurship programs in North America.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Graduate studies consist of an MBA with a global focus, and an MBA in the Management of Technology and Innovation. The school also offers a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">Master of Management Science (MMSc)</a> in the Management of Technology and Innovation.</p> 
     <p>In the 2009-2010 academic year, Ryerson introduced two new majors to the Business Management program: Law &amp; Business, and Global Management Studies. The Global Management Studies major is a successor of the Management major, which was last offered in 2010-2011.<sup id="cite_ref-ryerson.ca_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ryerson.ca-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In the fall of 2013, Ted Rogers School of Management is expected to launch the new School of Accounting and Finance. The design of the new school reflects TRSM?s on-going commitment to providing high quality programs with a rigorous curriculum, entry standards, and a supportive staff and faculty.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Accounting and Finance majors continue to be offered through the Ted Rogers School of Business Management, and are set to expand in 2013.</p> 
     <p>The business programs previously housed on campus in the &quot;Business Building&quot;, moved into its new facilities after a $15 million donation from <a href="/wiki/Edward_Samuel_Rogers" title="Edward Samuel Rogers">Ted Rogers</a>. The school is located within a new wing of the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Eaton_Centre" title="Toronto Eaton Centre">Toronto Eaton Centre</a> at the southeast corner of Bay and Dundas Streets. The school occupies three floors of the nine-floor wing (two floors are occupied by retail uses, with an above-grade parking garage occupying the remaining three storeys). The integration of the Ryerson faculty with commercial uses in the same building has been praised as an innovative solution for the downtown university.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The school received national notoriety when one of its professors (James Norrie) insulted the cast of the <a href="/wiki/Dragon%27s_Den" title="Dragon's Den" class="mw-redirect">Dragon's Den</a> during the final negotiations stage of a successful pitch by students of the school. The deal ultimately fell through because of the professor's actions. The same professor would later be banned from campus and would sue the university as well. <sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <p>Ryerson?s Faculty of Community Services offers multi-disciplinary programs in health, early childhood studies, social justice and community development.</p> 
     <p>The faculty incorporates health and safety programs under the School of Occupational and Public Health. The School of Occupational and Public Health (SOPHe) is considered to be a well-known leader in injury and disease prevention education. Ryerson University is the only school that offers a degree program in occupational health and safety in the province of Ontario. Certificate programs in health and safety can be completed through the Chang School of continuing education.</p> 
     <p>The faculty also houses the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ryerson.ca/midwife">Midwifery Education Program</a> (MEP), which will celebrate its 20th anniversary in 2013. The Ryerson MEP site is part of the longest-running consortium of its kind in Canada (with sister-sites at Laurentian University and McMaster University).</p> 
     <p>In keeping with Ryerson's brand of a career-focused education, students partner with various mentors, supervisors, practitioners and professionals to ensure a career-relevant experience is provided, in addition to the theoretical instructions commonly offered in a classroom setting.</p> 
     <p>The University also hosts a large nursing faculty, Daphne Cockwell School of Nursing, named after a World War II nurse.</p>

     <p>The Ryerson Faculty of Engineering and Architectural Science (formerly Faculty of Engineering, Architecture &amp; Science) is one of the largest engineering faculties in Canada, with 4,100 undergraduate students enrolled in 13 bachelor programs, and 480 graduate students in six master?s and four doctoral programs. In September 2006, FEAS will introduce two new master?s programs, and by 2007, there will be ten master?s programs in all. Ryerson?s Aerospace Computational Laboratory is a node for the High Performance Computational Virtual Laboratory for the Greater Toronto Area. The HPCVL is an interuniversity high-speed computation network which acts as a virtual supercomputer, providing the intensive computation power needed in the solution of complex problems in engineering and other disciplines.</p> 
     <p>The Faculty of Engineering and Architectural Science grants Bachelor of Architectural Science, Bachelor of Engineering degrees in 17 programs. Students can choose to specialize in the following disciplines: aerospace engineering, chemical engineering, civil engineering, computer engineering, electrical engineering, mechanical engineering, industrial engineering, architecture, project management and building science.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The Faculty of Engineering and Architectural Science offers graduate programs in <a href="/wiki/Aerospace_engineering" title="Aerospace engineering">aerospace engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biomedical_engineering" title="Biomedical engineering">biomedical engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a>, building science, <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineering" title="Chemical engineering">chemical engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineering" title="Civil engineering">civil engineering</a>, computer networks, <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Molecular_physics" title="Molecular physics">molecular physics</a>, environmental applied science and <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">mechanical engineering</a>.</p> 
     <p>Ryerson University?s <a href="/w/index.php?title=Department_of_Architectural_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Department of Architectural Science (page does not exist)">Department of Architectural Science</a> is housed in a building located at 325 Church Street designed by the prominent Canadian architect <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Thom" title="Ronald Thom">Ronald Thom</a> (Ryersonian). It offers a program in architecture accredited by the Canadian Architectural Certification Board at both the bachelor level (B.Arch.) and the master's level (M.Arch.).<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The Centre for Computing and Engineering opened in September 2004. It is a state-of-the-art science, technology, and research facility spanning almost an entire city block in downtown Toronto. The building was renamed the <a href="/wiki/George_Vari_Engineering_and_Computing_Centre" title="George Vari Engineering and Computing Centre">George Vari Engineering and Computing Centre</a> in November 2005. Ryerson researchers in the engineering and science disciplines have earned prestigious Premier?s Research Excellence Awards (PREA), <a href="/wiki/Canada_Research_Chair" title="Canada Research Chair">Canada Research Chairs</a>, NSERC Industrial Research Chair. A biomedical engineering program started at Ryerson in fall 2008 is the first such program in Canada.</p> 
     <p>The faculty hosts the <a href="/wiki/Centre_for_Urban_Energy" title="Centre for Urban Energy">Centre for Urban Energy</a>. CUE is co-sponsored by Hydro One, Ontario Power Authority and Toronto Hydro. The centre focuses on energy research and urban energy challenges.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Faculty_of_Science">Faculty of Science</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryerson_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Faculty of Science">edit</a>]</span></h3> 
     <p>On June 29, 2011 it was announced that the Faculty of Science had been approved by the Ryerson University Senate, and is the newest faculty at Ryerson University in about 40 years. The Faculty of Science will consist of the four founding departments - Chemistry &amp; Biology, Physics, Mathematics, and Computer Science.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Ryerson University's Faculty of Science offers a Bachelor of Science (BSc) degree in areas of applied mathematics, biology, biomedical science, chemistry, computer science, financial mathematics, and medical physics. Graduate studies consist of areas in biomolecular, biomedical, computational and mathematical studies.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campus_expansion">Campus expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryerson_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Campus expansion">edit</a>]</span></h2> 
     <p>Recently, the university has been undertaking the largest campus expansion in its history, with six new buildings constructed since 2000 and two additional constructions announced.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> In January 2008, Ryerson acquired $40 million worth of real-estate as part of its expansion efforts. The most notable acquisition being three properties on Yonge Street, including the former <a href="/wiki/Sam_the_Record_Man" title="Sam the Record Man">Sam the Record Man</a> store, Future Shop and World of Posters.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> There has been a strong desire among students, faculty, and administrators alike to have Gould Street closed between Yonge and Church in order to provide greater safety for pedestrians on campus. <a href="/wiki/Ryerson_Theatre" title="Ryerson Theatre">Ryerson Theatre</a>, which is one of the largest theatres in downtown Toronto with over 1200 seats has also had extensive renovations completed in the past five years. The theatre is home to several <a href="/wiki/Red_carpet" title="Red carpet">red carpet</a> premieres as part of the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">Toronto International Film Festival</a>.</p>

     <p>The Ryerson Library collection consists of over 500,000 books, 3,700 print journal titles and over $2 million of electronic resources, including approximately 23,000 e-journals, approximately over 90,000 e-books, databases and indexes, geospatial data, and catalogued websites or electronic documents. Most of the electronic resources can be accessed remotely by Ryerson community members with internet access, although authentication of Ryerson Library registration is required for access to all commercial resources. The library acquires materials to support the curriculum taught at the university and to support the research needs of faculty. All hard copy materials are housed in the library building at Gould and Victoria Streets.</p> 
     <p>The 11-storey tower was built in 1974, and is a classic example of <a href="/wiki/Brutalist_architecture" title="Brutalist architecture">Brutalist architecture</a>. The library buildings also holds administrative office, the Nursing Collaborative and until 2007 the urban and regional planning program. Urban and regional planning vacated the building in 2007, leaving more space for the existing library.</p> 
     <p>As part of the Ryerson University Master Plan, the library is expected to either relocate or be the subject of extensive renovations in the next several years. On January 18, 2008 the university announced the acquisition of properties including the former site of <a href="/wiki/Sam_the_Record_Man" title="Sam the Record Man">Sam the Record Man</a> which will allow expansion of the library to a prime <a href="/wiki/Yonge_Street" title="Yonge Street">Yonge Street</a> location. To improve study space, the entire fourth floor of the library underwent construction during the 2008 academic year. The renovation included the addition of lounges, a graduate reading room, and LCD panels.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_life">Student life</span><span class="mw-editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryerson_University&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Student life">edit</a>]</span></h2>
